## Slide 1 – Title
- **Chapter 12: Prophecies Relating to Christ**
- A Study on the Messiah in the Old and New Testaments

***

## Slide 2 – Q1: The Prophet Like Moses
- **Question:** In what words did Moses speak of Christ?
- **Key Text:** Deuteronomy 18:15 – “The Lord thy God will raise up unto thee a Prophet from the midst of thee, of thy brethren, like unto me, unto Him ye shall hearken.”

***

## Slide 3 – Q1: Key Points
- **A Type of Christ:** Moses was a type (symbolic foreshadowing).
  - Moses led Israel out of literal Egyptian bondage.
  - Christ leads humanity out of the spiritual bondage of sin (John 8:34–36).
- **The Mediator:** Moses mediated at Sinai; Christ is the "one mediator between God and men" (1 Timothy 2:5).
- **The Incarnation:** "Of thy brethren" predicts Christ taking human nature.
  - He took "the seed of Abraham" to be a merciful High Priest (Hebrews 2:16; 4:15).
- **Obedience:** "Unto Him ye shall hearken" is a command to obey from the heart.

***

## Slide 4 – Q1: Illustration
- **The Map vs. The Guide:**
  - Imagine travelers lost in a deadly desert.
  - Moses was like a **map** pointing toward the Promised Land.
  - Christ is the **Living Guide** who has walked the way Himself.
  - He does not just point the way; He takes our hand and leads us to life.

***

## Slide 5 – Q2: Identifying the Prophet
- **Question:** How may we know that the Prophet here spoken of is Christ?
- **Key Text:** Acts 3:22, 24 – “For Moses truly said unto the fathers, A Prophet shall the Lord your God raise up unto you of your brethren, like unto me... Yea, and all the prophets from Samuel and those that follow after, as many as have spoken, have likewise foretold of these days.”

***

## Slide 6 – Q2: Key Points
- **Inspired Interpretation:** The New Testament interprets the Old.
  - Peter, filled with the Spirit, applies Moses' prophecy directly to Jesus.
- **Unity of Scripture:** The Bible is one book with one Hero.
  - The OT is the gospel concealed in types.
  - The NT is the gospel revealed in Christ.
- **Authority:** If Jesus is the Prophet, His words carry God’s weight.
  - To reject His teaching on the law or salvation is to reject God (John 12:48–49).

***

## Slide 7 – Q3: The Virgin Birth
- **Question:** Was this prophecy fulfilled?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 1:22, 23 – “Now all this was done, that it might be fulfilled which was spoken of the Lord by the prophet, saying, Behold, a virgin shall be with child, and shall bring forth a son, and they shall call His name Emmanuel, which being interpreted is, God with us.”

***

## Slide 8 – Q3: Key Points (The Incarnation)
- **Miracle of Birth:** Born of a virgin, signifying God as His Father.
  - He is the "second Adam," untainted by inherited guilt but bearing our nature to redeem it.
- **Incarnation:** God the Son took human flesh, becoming fully man while remaining fully God (John 1:14).
- **Emmanuel (God with us):** The heart of the Righteousness by Faith message.
  - God *with* us in proximity (on earth).
  - God *with* us in nature (in flesh).
  - God *in* us by His Spirit (Colossians 1:27).

***

## Slide 9 – Q3: Application (Victory Over Sin)
- **Victory is Possible:**
  - Because He is "God with us," we do not fight sin alone.
  - His divinity combined with humanity allowed Him to endure every temptation without yielding.
  - He offers that same victorious life to us today.

***

## Slide 10 – Q3: Illustration
- **The Savior in the River:**
  - A king on the bank shouting instructions cannot save a drowning man.
  - A Savior-King dives into the treacherous water Himself.
  - He battles the same cold current and waves.
  - By entering the water ("God with us"), He grabs the drowning sinner and pulls him to safety.

***

## Slide 11 – Q4: The Place of Birth
- **Question:** Where was He to be born?
- **Key Text:** Micah 5:2 – “But thou, Bethlehem Ephratah, though thou be little among the thousands of Judah, yet out of thee shall He come forth unto Me that is to be ruler in Israel.”

***

## Slide 12 – Q4: Key Points
- **Humility:** Bethlehem was "little among the thousands."
  - God chooses weak things to confound the mighty (1 Corinthians 1:27).
  - True greatness is found in character, not position.
- **Pre-existence:** His "goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting."
  - Christ is the eternal Son of God entering time.
- **Ruler in Israel:** Born to rule on the throne of the heart.

***

## Slide 13 – Q5: Fulfillment in Bethlehem
- **Question:** Was this prediction fulfilled?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 2:1 – “Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judea in the days of Herod the king.”

***

## Slide 14 – Q5: Key Points
- **History Confirms Prophecy:**
  - God used Caesar Augustus' decree (Luke 2:1–7) to move Joseph and Mary to the right place at the right time.
- **Providence:** Even pagan emperors move under God's control to fulfill His word.
- **House of Bread:** "Bethlehem" means "House of Bread."
  - Fitting for the birth of the "Bread of Life" (John 6:35).

***

## Slide 15 – Q6: Preparing the Way
- **Question:** How was His advent to be heralded?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 40:3 – “The voice of him that crieth in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the Lord, make straight in the desert a highway for our God.”

***

## Slide 16 – Q6: Key Points
- **The Wilderness:** The unregenerate heart is a spiritual wilderness until watered by grace.
- **Making the Way Straight (Isaiah 40:4):**
  - *Mountains made low:* Pride and self-righteousness must be leveled.
  - *Valleys exalted:* Despair must be lifted by faith in God’s love.
- **Repentance:** We cannot have Christ's righteousness while holding onto pride or cherished sin.

***

## Slide 17 – Q6: Illustration
- **Building the Highway:**
  - Engineers must blast through rock and fill ravines to build a safe road.
  - Paving over holes or rocks results in failure.
  - We cannot just "add" Jesus to a life full of the rocks of pride.
  - The heart must be broken up by repentance so the King can travel smoothly.

***

## Slide 18 – Q7: John the Baptist
- **Question:** By whom was this fulfilled?
- **Key Text:** John 1:23 – “He said, I am the voice of one crying in the wilderness, Make straight the way of the Lord, as said the prophet Esaias.”

***

## Slide 19 – Q7: Key Points
- **A Voice, Not an Echo:** John spoke with authority, calling for "fruits meet for repentance" (Matthew 3:8).
- **Pointing to the Sanctuary:**
  - John did not gather followers for himself.
  - He pointed to the fulfillment of the sacrifice: "Behold the Lamb of God" (John 1:29).
- **Application:** God’s people today must prepare the way for the Second Coming by proclaiming repentance and the cleansing of the soul temple.

***

## Slide 20 – Q8: Balaam's Prophecy
- **Question:** Under what striking emblem was He prophesied of by Balaam?
- **Key Text:** Numbers 24:17 – “There shall come a Star out of Jacob and a Scepter shall rise out of Israel.”

***

## Slide 21 – Q8: Key Points
- **The Star:** Represents light, guidance, and glory.
  - Sanctuary connection: The candlestick gave light in the Holy Place; Christ is the Light of the world.
- **The Scepter:** Represents kingly authority.
  - Christ has the right to rule the nations and the individual life.

***

## Slide 22 – Q9: The Morning Star
- **Question:** Is He designated under the same emblem in the New Testament?
- **Key Text:** Revelation 22:16 – “I am the root and the offspring of David, and the bright and morning star.”

***

## Slide 23 – Q9: Key Points
- **Root and Offspring:**
  - *Root:* David's Creator (God).
  - *Offspring:* David's Descendant (Man).
- **The Morning Star:**
  - Appears in the darkest hour before dawn.
  - Christ is the promise of eternal day in a dark world.
- **Application:** As we study prophecy, the "Day Star" arises in our hearts (2 Peter 1:19), dispelling doubt and sin.

***

## Slide 24 – Q10: Weeping for Children
- **Question:** What was predicted of the little children in connection with the birth of Christ?
- **Key Text:** Jeremiah 31:15 – “A voice was heard in Ramah, lamentation, and bitter weeping; Rachel weeping for her children refused to be comforted for her children, because they were not.”

***

## Slide 25 – Q10: Key Points
- **The Great Controversy:**
  - Satan knew the "Seed of the woman" had come.
  - He used a worldly king to try to destroy the Child.
- **Nature of the Enemy:** Sin is ruthless and causes the innocent to suffer.

***

## Slide 26 – Q11: Herod's Cruelty
- **Question:** How were these words of the prophet verified?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 2:16 – “Then Herod... sent forth, and slew all the children that were in Bethlehem, and in all the coasts thereof, from two years old and under...”

***

## Slide 27 – Q11: Key Points
- **Rage of the Enemy:** Whenever Christ is born in a heart, the enemy becomes wrathful.
- **God’s Protection:**
  - God preserved His Son because His hour had not yet come.
  - Christ was preserved to die at the appointed time (Passover) as the true Sacrifice.

***

## Slide 28 – Q12: The Suffering Servant
- **Question:** How was He to be received by His own people?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 53:3 – “He is despised and rejected of men; a Man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief, and we hid as it were our faces from Him; He was despised, and we esteemed Him not.”

***

## Slide 29 – Q12: Key Points
- **Despised and Rejected:**
  - His pure life was a rebuke to sin.
  - The people wanted political power; He offered righteousness.
- **Man of Sorrows:** He bore the weight of the world's woe.
- **Hiding Our Faces:** The carnal heart naturally turns away from the self-denial of the Cross.

***

## Slide 30 – Q12: Illustration
- **The Doctor and the Patient:**
  - A patient might reject a surgeon who suggests a painful, life-saving operation.
  - The Jews rejected the Great Physician because He diagnosed their fatal disease (sin).
  - His prescription (humility and faith) was too hard for their pride.

***

## Slide 31 – Q13: Rejection and Acceptance
- **Question:** Did this prophecy prove true?
- **Key Text:** John 1:11 – “He came unto His own, and His own received Him not.”

***

## Slide 32 – Q13: Key Points
- **The Blindness of Sin:** The Creator walked among His creatures, but they only saw a carpenter's son.
- **The Exception (John 1:12):**
  - "But as many as received Him, to them gave He power to become the sons of God."
  - To receive Him is to allow Him to blot out sin and write the law in the heart.

***

## Slide 33 – Q14: The Messiah's Mission
- **Question:** What was predicted of His preaching?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 61:1 – “The Spirit of the Lord God is upon Me; because the Lord hath anointed Me to preach good tidings unto the meek; He hath sent Me to bind up the brokenhearted, to proclaim liberty to the captives...”

***

## Slide 34 – Q14: Key Points
- **The Anointed One:**
  - "Messiah" means Anointed.
  - Like the High Priest in the sanctuary, Jesus is anointed with the Spirit for ministry.
- **The Work of the Gospel:**
  1.  **To the Meek:** Spiritual riches are for the poor in spirit.
  2.  **Bind the Brokenhearted:** Healing the wounds of sin.
  3.  **Liberty to Captives:** Freedom from habits, addiction, and condemnation (Romans 8:1–2).

***

## Slide 35 – Q15: Fulfillment in Nazareth
- **Question:** In what way was this prediction verified?
- **Key Text:** Luke 4:21 – “And He began to say unto them, This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears.”

***

## Slide 36 – Q15: Key Points
- **Sabbath Custom:** Jesus set the example by attending the synagogue on the Sabbath.
- **Present Truth:** "This day is this scripture fulfilled."
  - He is the Liberator *now*.
  - He offers freedom from the power of sin today, not just in the future.

***

## Slide 37 – Q16: The Lamb of God
- **Question:** What was said He would do when on trial?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 53:7 – “He was oppressed, and He was afflicted, yet He opened not His mouth: He is brought as a lamb to the slaughter...”

***

## Slide 38 – Q16: Key Points
- **Sanctuary Type:** The sacrificial lamb did not struggle against the priest.
- **Antitype:** Christ submitted voluntarily to the cross to make atonement.
- **Victory over Self:** True power is shown in self-control and silence, entrusting judgment to God (1 Peter 2:23).

***

## Slide 39 – Q17: Silence Before Pilate
- **Question:** When accused by His enemies before Pilate, how did Christ receive their words?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 27:14 – “And He answered him to never a word; insomuch that the governor marveled greatly.”

***

## Slide 40 – Q17: Key Points
- **Why Silence?**
  1.  His innocence was obvious; truth needs no defense against lies.
  2.  **Substitution:** He stood in our place.
      - We are guilty and have no defense.
      - He took our guilt and stood silent, so we might claim His righteousness in the judgment.

***

## Slide 41 – Q18: Parting His Garments
- **Question:** How did the prophecy state that Christ's murderers would dispose of His garments?
- **Key Text:** Psalm 22:18 – “They part My garments among them, and cast lots upon My vesture.”

***

## Slide 42 – Q18: Key Points
- **Stripped of Dignity:** He became poor that we might become rich (2 Corinthians 8:9).
- **Imputed Righteousness:**
  - While they took His earthly robe, He provided a "Robe of Righteousness" (Isaiah 61:10).
  - He was stripped naked so we might be clothed with His perfect character in the judgment.

***

## Slide 43 – Q19: Literal Fulfillment
- **Question:** Was this literally accomplished?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 27:35 – “And they crucified Him, and parted His garments, casting lots: that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophet...”

***

## Slide 44 – Q19: Key Points
- **Exact Fulfillment:** Roman soldiers, ignorant of Hebrew prophecy, fulfilled it to the letter.
- **Inspiration:** This stands as proof of the divine inspiration of Scripture.

***

## Slide 45 – Q20: Gall and Vinegar
- **Question:** How was it said they would treat Him while on the cross?
- **Key Text:** Psalm 69:21 – “They gave Me also gall for My meat; and in My thirst they gave Me vinegar to drink.”

***

## Slide 46 – Q20: Key Points
- **The Cup of Suffering:**
  - In the Garden, He prayed, "Let this cup pass."
  - On the Cross, He drained the cup of separation from God so we might drink the water of life.

***

## Slide 47 – Q21: Refusing the Narcotic
- **Question:** What was offered Him, when in His dying agony?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 27:34 – “They gave Him vinegar to drink mingled with gall: and when He had tasted thereof, He would not drink.”

***

## Slide 48 – Q21: Key Points
- **A Clear Mind:** The mixture was a painkiller (narcotic).
- **Why He Refused:**
  - He would not allow His mind to be clouded.
  - He conquered sin fully conscious of the cost.
- **Temperance Lesson:** A powerful lesson to keep our bodies and minds pure from anything that numbs our moral faculties.

***

## Slide 49 – Q22: The Grave Prophecy
- **Question:** Where was the grave of Christ to be made?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 53:9 – “And He made His grave with the wicked, and with the rich in His death.”

***

## Slide 50 – Q22: Key Points
- **A Seeming Contradiction:**
  - *With the Wicked:* Crucified between thieves; intended for a criminal's grave.
  - *With the Rich:* God intervened to fulfill the prophecy that He would be with the rich in His death (burial).

***

## Slide 51 – Q23: The Empty Tomb
- **Question:** What was actually done with His body, after it was taken down from the cross?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 27:57-60 – “A rich man of Arimathea, named Joseph,... begged the body of Jesus... and laid it in His own new tomb, which he had hewn out of the rock.”

***

## Slide 52 – Q23: Key Points
- **God Overrules:** Joseph of Arimathea, a rich man, stepped forward.
- **The New Tomb:** A tomb where no man had lain, preventing any doubt about the resurrection miracle.
- **Sabbath Rest:**
  - Jesus said, "It is finished," and rested in the tomb over the Sabbath.
  - He rested after Redemption just as He rested after Creation.

***

## Slide 53 – Conclusion
- **Faith in the Messiah:**
  - These prophecies prove Jesus is the Christ.
  - He loved us enough to suffer, die, and rise again.
  - Faith in *this* Christ brings justification, sanctification, and eternal life.
