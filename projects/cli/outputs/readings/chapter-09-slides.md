## Slide 1 – Q1: The Attributes of the Father
- **Question:** What attributes did Christ ascribe to His Father while addressing Him just before His trial in Gethsemane?
- **Key Text:** John 17:11 – “And now I am no more in the world, but these are in the world, and I come to thee. Holy Father, keep through thine own name those whom thou hast given me, that they may be one, as we are.”
- **Key Text:** John 17:25 – “O righteous Father, the world hath not known thee: but I have known thee, and these have known that thou hast sent me.”

## Slide 2 – Q1: The Holy Father
- In Christ’s “High Priestly Prayer,” He highlights God’s **holiness**.
- **Holiness Defined:** Absolute purity and moral perfection.
- **Scripture:** Isaiah 6:3 – “And one cried unto another, and said, Holy, holy, holy, is the LORD of hosts: the whole earth is full of his glory.”
- **Sanctuary Connection:**
  - In the earthly sanctuary, vessels were “holy” (set apart for God).
  - Christ, our High Priest, bears His people before the “Holy Father.”
  - He asks God to keep His disciples separate from the sin of the world.

## Slide 3 – Q1: The Righteous Father
- **Righteousness Defined:** Being right, just, and acting in perfect accordance with God’s law of love.
- **Scripture:** Psalm 119:172 – “My tongue shall speak of thy word: for all thy commandments are righteousness.”
- **The Believer’s Need:**
  - Because God is holy and man is sinful, we cannot stand before Him in our own “filthy rags” (Isaiah 64:6).
  - We need a Mediator to cover us with merit and wash us by His Spirit.

## Slide 4 – Q1: Illustration
- **The Sterile Surgeon:**
  - Imagine a surgeon preparing for a delicate operation.
  - He must be perfectly clean and sterile (holy) to save the patient; contamination means death.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - God’s holiness is not a barrier to keep us away.
  - It is a purity that must be imparted to us so we can live in His presence without being consumed.

## Slide 5 – Q2: Christ’s Attributes
- **Question:** Does Christ possess these attributes?
- **Key Text:** Acts 2:27 – “Because thou wilt not leave my soul in hell, neither wilt thou suffer thine Holy One to see corruption.”
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 53:11 – “He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied: by his knowledge shall my righteous servant justify many; for he shall bear their iniquities.”

## Slide 6 – Q2: The Holy One
- The Scriptures attribute divine holiness and righteousness to Jesus.
- **The Holy One:**
  - Christ passed through a sinful world without being tainted.
  - **Scripture:** Hebrews 7:26 – “For such an high priest became us, who is holy, harmless, undefiled, separate from sinners, and made higher than the heavens.”
  - Because He was without sin, the grave could not hold Him (Acts 2:27).

## Slide 7 – Q2: Righteousness by Faith
- **The Righteous Servant:** Isaiah 53:11 calls Christ the “righteous servant” who justifies many.
- **Justification Defined:** God’s legal declaration that a repentant sinner is righteous, based solely on Christ’s merits.
- **How We Are Saved:**
  - We are not saved by our own works, but “by his knowledge” (knowing and trusting Him).
  - **Scripture:** Romans 3:24 – “Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus.”
  - Christ credits His perfect life to our account and transforms our character to be like His.

## Slide 8 – Q2: Illustration
- **The Debtor and the King:**
  - A man drowning in debt faces prison and cannot pay his way out.
  - A wealthy friend pays the debt in full, setting the man free.
- **More than Payment:**
  - Christ does more than pay the debt; He adopts the debtor into the royal family.
  - He teaches the debtor how to live like the King.
  - Christ clears our past (justification) and transforms our future (sanctification).

## Slide 9 – Q3: The Goodness of God
- **Question:** What is said about the goodness of God?
- **Key Text:** Exodus 34:6 – “And the LORD passed by before him, and proclaimed, The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth.”

## Slide 10 – Q3: God’s Character Revealed
- God reveals His true character to counter Satan’s charge that God is severe.
- **Merciful:** Treating us better than we deserve.
- **Gracious:** Giving favor and power we do not earn.
- **Longsuffering:** Patient with our weaknesses.
- **Abundant in Goodness:** His resources of love are inexhaustible.
- **Key Insight:** To be changed into His image means becoming merciful and gracious through the indwelling of Christ.

## Slide 11 – Q3: Illustration
- **The King and the Rebels:**
  - A rebellion breaks out in a kingdom. The king has the power to crush the rebels instantly.
  - Instead, he visits them unarmed, offering food, medicine, and pardon.
  - He patiently waits for them to lay down their arms.
- **Spiritual Lesson:** This depicts God’s “longsuffering” and “goodness” toward a rebellious world.

## Slide 12 – Q4: Christ’s Work on Earth
- **Question:** What was the nature of Christ's work while among men?
- **Key Text:** Acts 10:38 – “How God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Ghost and with power: who went about doing good, and healing all that were oppressed of the devil; for God was with him.”

## Slide 13 – Q4: Practical Godliness
- Christ was the express image of the Father (Hebrews 1:3), demonstrating God’s attributes practically.
- **Active Benevolence:** He “went about doing good,” mingling with people to help them.
- **Conflict with Satan:** He healed those “oppressed of the devil,” destroying the enemy’s works.
- **Scripture:** 1 John 3:8 – “For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that he might destroy the works of the devil.”

## Slide 14 – Q4: Application – Sanctification
- **Sanctification Defined:** The process of becoming like Christ in character.
- **Faith Works by Love:**
  - We cannot claim Christ’s righteousness if we are indifferent to suffering.
  - **Scripture:** Galatians 5:6 – “For in Jesus Christ neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision; but faith which worketh by love.”
  - If we abide in Christ, we will do the works of Christ.

## Slide 15 – Q4: Illustration
- **The Stream and the Swamp:**
  - A stream that receives water but has no outlet becomes a stagnant swamp.
  - A stream that receives and gives flows clear and sweet.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - The Christian who receives God’s goodness but does not “go about doing good” becomes spiritually stagnant.

## Slide 16 – Q5: The Mercy of God
- **Question:** What is said of the mercy of God?
- **Key Text:** Psalms 103:8 – “The LORD is merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and plenteous in mercy.”

## Slide 17 – Q5: Plenteous Mercy
- **Slow to Anger:** God gives time for repentance.
- **Scripture:** 2 Peter 3:9 – “The Lord is not slack concerning his promise... but is longsuffering to us-ward, not willing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance.”
- **Purpose of Mercy:** It is not permission to sin, but a bridge to restoration.
- **Scripture:** Romans 2:4 – “Or despisest thou the riches of his goodness... not knowing that the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance?”

## Slide 18 – Q5: Sanctuary Connection
- **The Mercy Seat:**
  - In the sanctuary, the cover of the Ark was called the “Mercy Seat.”
  - It was placed *above* the Ten Commandments.
- **Lesson:**
  - The Law requires perfect obedience.
  - Mercy makes a way for the transgressor to be cleansed and restored to obedience through the blood of the sacrifice.

## Slide 19 – Q5: Illustration
- **The Broken Vase:**
  - A child breaks a valuable vase. The father has the right to punish.
  - Instead, he hugs the weeping child and says, “I forgive you; let us clean this up.”
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - Mercy restores the relationship so the child desires to be careful and obedient in the future.

## Slide 20 – Q6: The Compassion of God
- **Question:** Is He compassionate?
- **Key Text:** Psalms 86:15 – “But thou, O Lord, art a God full of compassion, and gracious, longsuffering, and plenteous in mercy and truth.”

## Slide 21 – Q6: Our Compassionate High Priest
- **Full of Compassion:** God feels deep pity and sorrow for our suffering.
- **Our High Priest:**
  - Christ is touched with the feeling of our infirmities.
  - **Scripture:** Hebrews 4:15 – “For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin.”
- **Boldness:** Because He understands our struggle, we can come boldly to the throne of grace for help.

## Slide 22 – Q6: Illustration
- **The Shepherd and the Sheep:**
  - When a shepherd sees a sheep caught in thorns, bleeding and scared, he does not feel anger.
  - He feels pity, gently disentangles the sheep, and carries it home.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - This is God’s compassion toward the sinner caught in the thorns of bad habits and guilt.

## Slide 23 – Q7: God’s Faithfulness
- **Question:** How did Moses represent God's faithfulness in keeping covenant with his people?
- **Key Text:** Deuteronomy 7:9 – “Know therefore that the LORD thy God, he is God, the faithful God, which keepeth covenant and mercy with them that love him and keep his commandments to a thousand generations.”

## Slide 24 – Q7: The Covenant Relationship
- **Covenant Defined:** A binding agreement between God and man.
- **New Covenant Promise:** God promises to write His law in our hearts.
  - **Scripture:** Hebrews 8:10 – “For this is the covenant that I will make... I will put my laws into their mind, and write them in their hearts.”
- **Man’s Part:** “Them that love him and keep his commandments.”
  - Obedience is the *fruit* of the covenant, not the root.
  - God is faithful to complete the work of sanctification in us (Philippians 1:6).

## Slide 25 – Q7: Illustration
- **The Faithful Marriage:**
  - In a marriage, partners pledge faithfulness “until death.”
  - God calls Himself the husband to His people (Jeremiah 3:14).
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - God never breaks His vow.
  - Even when we stumble, if we return, we find His faithfulness standing like a mighty rock.

## Slide 26 – Q8: Christ as Truth
- **Question:** Is Christ also the embodiment of truth?
- **Key Text:** John 14:6 – “Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me.”

## Slide 27 – Q8: Key Points on Truth
- **Christ is Truth:** He does not just teach truth; He *is* the Truth.
- **Freedom:**
  - **Scripture:** John 8:32 – “And ye shall know the truth, and the truth shall make you free.”
  - Embracing Christ frees us from Satan’s deceptions and the slavery of sin.
- **The Only Way:**
  - Christ is the only connector between earth and heaven (John 1:51).
  - We cannot bypass His sacrifice or mediation.

## Slide 28 – Q8: Illustration
- **The Map vs. The Guide:**
  - A map can show you the route to a destination, but it cannot help you walk it.
  - A guide goes with you, helping you over obstacles.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - Christ is not just the map (doctrine); He is the Guide (The Way) who walks with us to the Father.

## Slide 29 – Q9: The Wisdom of God
- **Question:** How is the wisdom of God represented?
- **Key Text:** Job 36:5 – “Behold, God is mighty, and despiseth not any: he is mighty in strength and wisdom.”

## Slide 30 – Q9: Strength and Wisdom
- **Perfect Understanding:** God combines infinite power with infinite intelligence.
- **Despiseth Not Any:**
  - God’s wisdom is linked to His humility.
  - He uses His wisdom to save even the humblest sinner.
- **Trusting His Plan:**
  - Because God is all-wise, His plan is better than ours.
  - **Scripture:** Romans 8:28 – “And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose.”

## Slide 31 – Q9: Illustration
- **The Master Weaver:**
  - A weaver sees the whole beautiful pattern of the tapestry from above.
  - The observer below only sees the tangled, messy threads on the backside.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - We must trust the Weaver’s wisdom even when the threads of our life seem confused and dark.

## Slide 32 – Q10: The Wisdom of Christ
- **Question:** What is said of the wisdom of Christ?
- **Key Text:** Luke 2:40 – “And the child grew, and waxed strong in spirit, filled with wisdom; and the grace of God was upon him.”

## Slide 33 – Q10: Christ Our Example
- **Growth in Wisdom:**
  - Though divine, Christ took human nature and learned as we must learn.
  - He studied Scripture, nature, and communed with His Father.
- **Available to Us:**
  - If Christ was “filled with wisdom” through the Spirit, we can be too.
  - **Scripture:** James 1:5 – “If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him.”
- **Christ is Our Wisdom:**
  - **Scripture:** 1 Corinthians 1:30 – “But of him are ye in Christ Jesus, who of God is made unto us wisdom, and righteousness, and sanctification, and redemption.”

## Slide 34 – Q10: Illustration
- **The Flower and the Sun:**
  - As a flower opens itself to the sun to receive light and color, it grows.
  - The human mind of Jesus opened to the Father to receive wisdom daily.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - We are to follow this pattern, daily opening our hearts to heaven.

## Slide 35 – Q11: The Perfect Justice of God
- **Question:** In what strong language is the perfect justice of God proclaimed?
- **Key Text:** Deuteronomy 32:4 – “He is the Rock, his work is perfect; for all his ways are judgment: a God of truth and without iniquity, just and right is he.”

## Slide 36 – Q11: Justice and the Gospel
- **The Rock:** A symbol of God’s stability and justice.
- **All His Ways are Judgment:** God is never arbitrary; He is always fair.
- **Justice at the Cross:**
  - Satan accused God of being unjust. The Cross proved God is both just and merciful.
  - **Scripture:** Romans 3:26 – “To declare, I say, at this time his righteousness: that he might be just, and the justifier of him which believeth in Jesus.”
  - God did not set aside the law; He paid its penalty Himself, establishing the law while saving the sinner.

## Slide 37 – Q11: Illustration
- **Building on Bedrock:**
  - A building built on sand shifts and cracks when the storm comes.
  - A building anchored into bedrock stands firm.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - God’s government is built on the bedrock of justice; therefore, the universe is safe in His hands.

## Slide 38 – Q12: God’s Eternity
- **Question:** How long will these attributes exist?
- **Key Text:** Psalms 102:25-27 – “Of old hast thou laid the foundation of the earth: and the heavens are the work of thy hands. They shall perish, but thou shalt endure... But thou art the same, and thy years shall have no end.”

## Slide 39 – Q12: The Unchangeable God
- **“Thou Art the Same”:** Creation may wear out, but the Creator remains.
- **Comfort for the Believer:**
  - Because God does not change, His promises do not expire.
  - **Scripture:** Malachi 3:6 – “For I am the LORD, I change not; therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed.”
- **The Everlasting Gospel:**
  - The plan of salvation is based on God’s unchangeable character.
  - **Scripture:** Revelation 14:6 – “And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth...”

## Slide 40 – Q12: Illustration
- **Clouds and the Sun:**
  - Clouds constantly change shape, blocking the sun or letting it shine.
  - The sun itself remains constant and burning.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - Our feelings and circumstances change like clouds.
  - God, the Sun of Righteousness, remains forever the same.

## Slide 41 – Q13: The Infinite God
- **Question:** Can man ever really find out God?
- **Key Text:** Romans 11:33 – “O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God: how unsearchable are his judgments, and his ways past finding out!”

## Slide 42 – Q13: Searching the Depths
- **Finite vs. Infinite:** We can know God truly, but never fully.
- **Eternity of Study:**
  - The redeemed will study God’s wisdom and the plan of salvation forever.
  - **Scripture:** Ephesians 2:7 – “That in the ages to come he might shew the exceeding riches of his grace in his kindness toward us through Christ Jesus.”
- **Humility:**
  - We must trust God’s heart even when we cannot trace His hand.
  - We walk by faith, not by sight.

## Slide 43 – Q13: Illustration
- **The Child and the Father:**
  - A small child trusts their father completely, yet understands very little of the father’s complex work or decisions.
- **Spiritual Lesson:**
  - We are the children; God is the Father.
  - We know He loves us, even when His ways are “past finding out.”
