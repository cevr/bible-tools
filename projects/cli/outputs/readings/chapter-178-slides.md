## Slide 1 – Q1: The Purpose of Creation

- **Question:** For what purpose was the earth created?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 45:18 – “For thus saith the Lord that created the heavens; God Himself that formed the earth and made it; He hath established it, He created it not in vain, He formed it to be inhabited.”

## Slide 2 – Q1: Key Points

- **Designed for Life:**
  - God is a God of order, not confusion.
  - "Not in vain" implies an empty or chaotic earth is contrary to His design.
  - The earth was created to be the home of a holy, happy race (Genesis 1:31).
- **The Character of the Creator:**
  - God is Love (1 John 4:8).
  - Love desires to share existence and happiness.
  - The earth acts as a theater to display His love through His creatures.
- **Spiritual Parallel:**
  - Just as the earth was made to be inhabited, the human heart was made to be the habitation of His Spirit.
  - God does not want our lives "void," but filled with fruits of righteousness (Ephesians 3:17).

## Slide 3 – Q1: Illustration

- **The Master Builder:**
  - A builder does not lay a foundation and walls just to let a house decay.
  - He builds so a family may live in it.
- **God’s Renovation Plan:**
  - God built this world to be the eternal home of His family.
  - Though sin has "vandalized" the house, the Architect has a plan to renovate and inhabit it again.

---

## Slide 4 – Q2: The Purpose of Man

- **Question:** Why was man made?
- **Key Text:** Psalm 8:6 – “Thou madest him to have dominion over the works of Thy hands; Thou has put all things under His feet.”

## Slide 5 – Q2: Key Points

- **Vice-Gerent:**
  - Man was created as a representative ruler (vice-gerent) of God.
  - He stood as the head of earth's creation, crowned with glory and honor.
- **True Dominion:**
  - Refers to loving authority and stewardship, not force.
  - Adam was to rule by the power of love, reflecting God’s character to the animals and garden.
- **The Image of God:**
  - To have dominion requires being like the Sovereign (Genesis 1:26).
  - Man possessed the power of choice, reason, and a moral character in harmony with God’s law.
- **King in His Sphere:**
  - As long as Adam obeyed God, nature obeyed Adam.

## Slide 6 – Q2: Illustration

- **The Appointed Governor:**
  - A king appoints a governor to rule a province.
  - As long as the governor supports the king's laws, he carries the king's authority.
  - If the governor rebels, he loses his legitimate power.
- **Application:**
  - Adam was the appointed governor of earth until his rebellion.

---

## Slide 7 – Q3: The Current State of Dominion

- **Question:** Does man now have all these things under him?
- **Key Text:** Hebrews 2:8 – “Thou hast put all things in subjection under his feet. For in that He put all in subjection under Him, He left nothing that is not put under him. But now we see not yet all things put under him.”

## Slide 8 – Q3: Key Points

- **The "Not Yet":**
  - The text gives a tragic update: the original plan has been interrupted by sin.
- **The Rebellion of Nature:**
  - When man rebelled against the Creator, creation rebelled against man.
  - Thorns, thistles, and wild beasts are evidence that the scepter of dominion has fallen (Genesis 3:17–18).
- **Loss of Self-Dominion:**
  - Man lost control of himself, becoming a slave to appetite and passion.
  - One who cannot rule his own spirit cannot truly rule the world (Proverbs 25:28).

## Slide 9 – Q3: Illustration

- **The Mutiny:**
  - If a captain mutinies against the admiral, the sailors will eventually lose respect for the captain.
  - Disorder at the top leads to chaos below.
- **The Lesson:**
  - When Adam mutinied against God, chaos entered the whole earth.

---

## Slide 10 – Q4: To Whom Dominion Was Yielded

- **Question:** When man lost his dominion, to whom did he yield it?
- **Key Text:** 2 Peter 2:19 – “For of whom a man is overcome, of the same is he brought in bondage.”

## Slide 11 – Q4: Key Points

- **The Law of Bondage:**
  - We are servants to whom we obey (Romans 6:16).
  - By obeying Satan, humanity became captives of Satan.
- **Transfer of Territory:**
  - By the laws of conquest, the victor takes the possessions of the vanquished.
  - Adam surrendered the "keys" of the earth to the enemy.
- **The Great Controversy:**
  - Earth became a rebel province occupied by forces of darkness.
  - Misery and death exist because the world is under the rule of a tyrant.

## Slide 12 – Q4: Illustration

- **The Defeated King:**
  - In ancient battles, a defeated king surrendered his crown and kingdom to the conqueror.
  - Adam represented the human race at the tree of knowledge.
  - When he fell, he surrendered the dominion to a foreign power.

---

## Slide 13 – Q5: Evidence of Satan’s Possession

- **Question:** What circumstance shows that the earth came into Satan's possession?
- **Key Text:** Luke 4:5, 6 – “And the Devil, taking Him up into a high mountain, showed unto Him all the kingdoms of the world in a moment of time. And the Devil said unto Him, All this power will I give Thee, and the glory of them; for that is delivered unto Me; and to whomsoever I will, I give it.”

## Slide 14 – Q5: Key Points

- **"Delivered unto Me":**
  - Satan claims the world was *delivered* to him (by Adam's sin).
  - He is the "prince of this world" (John 12:31).
- **The Temptation:**
  - Satan offered Christ the crown without the Cross.
  - He tempted Jesus to compromise rather than suffer.
- **Christ's Response:**
  - Jesus did not dispute that Satan currently held the world.
  - Jesus refused the offer because He came to redeem the world through righteousness, not a deal with the devil.

## Slide 15 – Q5: Illustration

- **The Thief and the Heirloom:**
  - A thief may steal a precious heirloom and hold it in his possession.
  - He does not own it by right, but he holds it hostage.
- **The Ransom:**
  - Satan is the "squatter" on God's property.
  - Christ came to pay the ransom and evict the intruder.

---

## Slide 16 – Q6: Promise of Restoration

- **Question:** Is this first dominion to be restored? And to whom is it to come?
- **Key Text:** Micah 4:8 – “And thou, O tower of the flock, the stronghold of the daughter of Zion, unto thee shall it come, even the first dominion; the kingdom shall come to the daughter of Jerusalem.”

## Slide 17 – Q6: Key Points

- **The Promise:**
  - The "first dominion" (Edenic peace) will be fully restored.
- **The Tower of the Flock:**
  - Prophetic title for the Messiah (Jesus).
  - Dominion comes *first* to Him as the Conqueror.
- **Restoration, Not Replacement:**
  - God redeems what was lost (Luke 19:10).
  - He saves both the lost man and the lost earth.
- **Daughter of Jerusalem:**
  - Refers to the Church (God's people), who are joint-heirs.

---

## Slide 18 – Q7: The Restorer

- **Question:** If man lost the dominion through sin and death, through whom will it be restored?
- **Key Text:** Hebrews 2:9 – “But we see Jesus, who was made a little lower than the angels for the suffering of death, crowned with glory and honor; that He by the grace of God should taste death for every man.”

## Slide 19 – Q7: Key Points

- **The Second Adam:**
  - Jesus succeeded where Adam failed.
  - He was "made lower" (became human) to enter the battlefield.
- **Suffering of Death:**
  - The penalty of sin is death (Romans 6:23).
  - Jesus paid the debt on Calvary to reclaim the title deed.
- **Victory:**
  - By conquering death, He stripped Satan of power (Hebrews 2:14).

## Slide 20 – Q7: Application (Righteousness by Faith)

- **Source of Restoration:**
  - We cannot win back dominion by our own works.
  - It was lost by sin; it is recovered only by Christ’s perfect righteousness.
  - **Imputed:** Reckoned to us for justification.
  - **Imparted:** Given to us for sanctification.

## Slide 21 – Q7: Illustration

- **The Family Estate:**
  - A father gambles away the family estate; the children are left homeless and penniless.
  - A wealthy, benevolent brother steps in, pays the full debt, and restores the deed to the family.
  - Jesus is that Brother.

---

## Slide 22 – Q8: The Blessing of the Meek

- **Question:** What blessing did our Saviour pronounce upon the meek?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 5:5 – “Blessed are the meek; for they shall inherit the earth.”

## Slide 23 – Q8: Key Points

- **Who are the Meek?**
  - Not weak, but strength under God's control.
  - Those who trust wholly in Christ’s righteousness, not their own.
- **Inheritance:**
  - A gift, not a wage.
  - We inherit the new earth as adopted children of the King.
- **Future Hope:**
  - The proud may seem to "own" the earth now, but their possession is temporary.
  - The meek wait for God’s vindication.

---

## Slide 24 – Q9: Earthly Prosperity

- **Question:** Who does David say have the most of earthly things?
- **Key Text:** Psalm 73:3, 7 – “For I was envious at the foolish, when I saw the prosperity of the wicked... Their eyes stand out with fatness; they have more than heart could wish.”

## Slide 25 – Q9: Key Points

- **The Envy of the Righteous:**
  - It often appears the wicked are winning (wealth, power).
  - This is a common trial of faith for God's people.
- **Deception of Appearances:**
  - Material prosperity is not a sign of God’s favor.
  - Satan often rewards his followers with temporary gain (Luke 4:6), but it is a trap.

## Slide 26 – Q9: Illustration

- **The Ox and the Calf:**
  - A fattened calf eats and rests all day, seeming to prosper more than the working ox.
  - But the calf is being prepared for slaughter.
  - The ox is preserved because it is useful to the master.

---

## Slide 27 – Q10: The Difference in Portion

- **Question:** Will there be a difference between the portion of the righteous and that of the wicked?
- **Key Text:** Psalm 37:34 – “Wait on the Lord, and keep His way, and He shall exalt thee to inherit the land: when the wicked are cut off, thou shalt see it.”

## Slide 28 – Q10: Key Points

- **"Wait on the Lord":**
  - The posture of faith: trusting God's timing rather than seizing power.
- **The Sanctuary Connection:**
  - Sin cannot exist forever.
  - Just as the "scapegoat" was separated from the camp (Leviticus 16), the wicked will be "cut off."
- **Final Outcome:**
  - The righteous will inherit the land; the wicked will be no more.

---

## Slide 29 – Q11: The Promise to Abram

- **Question:** What promise was made to Abram concerning the land?
- **Key Text:** Genesis 13:14, 15 – “And the Lord said unto Abram... Lift up now thine eyes... for all the land which thou seest, to thee will I give it, and to thy seed forever.”

## Slide 30 – Q11: Key Points

- **Personal Promise:**
  - God said, "To *thee* will I give it."
  - Abraham himself was promised the land, not just his descendants.
- **"Forever":**
  - Abraham died, yet the promise is "forever."
  - This implies a resurrection and a world where death no longer exists.
- **The Covenant:**
  - A binding agreement.
  - The foundation of the gospel: A Land and a Seed (Christ).

---

## Slide 31 – Q12: The Scope of the Promise

- **Question:** How much did this promise comprehend?
- **Key Text:** Romans 4:13 – “For the promise, that he should be the heir of the world, was not to Abraham, or to his seed, through the law, but through the righteousness of faith.”

## Slide 32 – Q12: Key Points

- **Heir of the World:**
  - Not just a strip of land in the Middle East.
  - The promise encompasses the entire renewed planet.
- **Not Through the Law:**
  - We cannot earn the New Earth by keeping the law.
  - If it depended on our performance, all would be disinherited (Romans 3:23).
- **Through Righteousness of Faith:**
  - Justification: Declared righteous by faith in Christ.
  - Sanctification: Christ living in us fits us for the land.

## Slide 33 – Q12: Illustration

- **The Ladder vs. The Vehicle:**
  - A man cannot reach the moon on a ladder of his own making (Works/Law).
  - He must be carried by a vehicle designed for the journey.
  - We are carried to the New Earth by the vehicle of Grace, entered through Faith.

---

## Slide 34 – Q13: Abraham's Ownership

- **Question:** How much of the land of Canaan did Abraham own in his lifetime?
- **Key Text:** Acts 7:5 – “And he gave him none inheritance in it, no, not so much as to set his foot on; yet He promised that He would give it to him for a possession, and to his seed after him, when as yet he had no child.”

## Slide 35 – Q13: Key Points

- **The Historical Reality:**
  - Abraham owned nothing but a burial cave. He lived as a nomad.
- **The Test of Faith:**
  - He had the *promise* (title deed) in his heart, but not the *possession* under his feet.
- **Future Fulfillment:**
  - He died "not having received the promises" (Hebrews 11:13).
  - The fulfillment is post-resurrection.

---

## Slide 36 – Q14: Abraham's Expectation

- **Question:** How much of the promised possession did Abraham expect during his lifetime?
- **Key Text:** Hebrews 11:9, 10 – “By faith he sojourned in the land of promise... for he looked for a city which hath foundations, whose builder and maker is God.”

## Slide 37 – Q14: Key Points

- **Stranger and Pilgrim:**
  - He knew this present world, marred by sin, was not his true home.
- **The City with Foundations:**
  - He looked for the New Jerusalem.
  - Tents are temporary; God’s city is permanent.
- **Sanctuary Parallel:**
  - The wilderness tabernacle was temporary; the temple was permanent.
  - Our life now is a "tabernacle" existence waiting for the solid "City of God."

---

## Slide 38 – Q15: The Seed

- **Question:** Who is the seed to whom the Abrahamic promise was made?
- **Key Text:** Galatians 3:16 – “Now to Abraham and his seed were the promises made. He saith not, And to 'seeds' as of many: but as of one, And to thy 'seed,' which is Christ.”

## Slide 39 – Q15: Key Points

- **The Singular Seed:**
  - The true Heir of the world is **Jesus Christ**.
  - He alone obeyed God perfectly and conquered Satan.
- **Legal Right:**
  - The title deed to the earth belongs legally to Christ.
  - All promises are "Yea and Amen" in Him (2 Corinthians 1:20).

---

## Slide 40 – Q16: The Heirs

- **Question:** Who are counted heirs of that promise?
- **Key Text:** Galatians 3:29 – “And if ye be Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise.”

## Slide 41 – Q16: Key Points

- **Union with Christ:**
  - We become heirs not by birth, but by being *in* Christ.
  - Righteousness by Faith makes us part of His body.
- **Joint-Heirs:**
  - Since the inheritance belongs to the Seed (Christ), those joined to Him share it.
- **Marriage Analogy:**
  - When a poor person marries a wealthy heir, they instantly share the ownership.
  - When we are joined to Christ, we become heirs of the world.

---

## Slide 42 – Q17: The Delay Explained

- **Question:** Why did not these ancient worthies receive the promise?
- **Key Text:** Hebrews 11:39, 40 – “And these all... received not the promise, God having provided some better thing for us, that they without us should not be made perfect.”

## Slide 43 – Q17: Key Points

- **God's Collective Plan:**
  - God waits so His entire family—from Adam to the last soul—can enter together.
- **"Without Us":**
  - The saints are sleeping in their graves, waiting for the resurrection.
  - They did not go immediately to receive the reward at death.
- **Made Perfect:**
  - Refers to glorification at the Second Coming.
  - We will be "caught up together" (1 Thessalonians 4:17).

---

## Slide 44 – Q18: The Day of the Lord

- **Question:** What is to become of our earth in the day of the Lord?
- **Key Text:** 2 Peter 3:10 – “But the day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night; in the which the heavens shall pass away with a great noise, and the elements shall melt with fervent heat, the earth also; and the works that are therein shall be burned up.”

## Slide 45 – Q18: Key Points

- **The Cleansing Fire:**
  - The earth must be cleansed of every trace of sin.
  - Just as water cleansed the old world, fire will cleanse the final world.
- **Sanctuary Imagery:**
  - The final act of atonement: cleansing the "camp."
  - Sin and sinners are eradicated from the universe.
- **Purification:**
  - Not annihilation of the planet, but purification of the substance.
  - The "works" of sin are burned up to prepare for re-creation.

---

## Slide 46 – Q19: What Follows the Fire

- **Question:** What will follow this great conflagration?
- **Key Text:** 2 Peter 3:13 – “Nevertheless we, according to His promise, look for new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness.”

## Slide 47 – Q19: Key Points

- **New Heavens and New Earth:**
  - From the ashes, God recreates a glorious home.
- **Wherein Dwelleth Righteousness:**
  - The defining characteristic: No rebellion, no selfishness.
  - God's law of love is written on every heart.
- **Real Home:**
  - A tangible place for resurrected people, not a ghostly existence.

## Slide 48 – Q19: Illustration

- **The Refiner's Fire:**
  - A refiner puts gold ore into fire not to destroy the gold, but to remove the dross (impurities).
  - When the fire finishes, pure gold remains.
  - God's fire burns the dross of sin, leaving the pure gold of a restored creation.

---

## Slide 49 – Q20: Peter's Source

- **Question:** To what promise did Peter refer, when saying that he looked for new heavens and a new earth?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 65:17 – “For, behold, I create new heavens and a new earth; and the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind.”

## Slide 50 – Q20: Key Points

- **Unity of Scripture:**
  - Peter confirms the Old Testament hope of a literal restored earth.
- **"Former Shall Not Be Remembered":**
  - The pain, trauma, and sting of the past will vanish.
  - The joy of the present will far outweigh former sorrows.
- **Creation:**
  - God uses the word *bara* (create)—the same power used in Genesis 1.

---

## Slide 51 – Q21: Conditions in the New Earth

- **Question:** What will be the condition of things in the new earth?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 65:25 – “The wolf and the lamb shall feed together... They shall not hurt nor destroy in all My holy mountain, saith the Lord.”

## Slide 52 – Q21: Key Points

- **Peace in Nature:**
  - The law of predation (animals killing animals) is removed.
  - The curse of sin is lifted from creation.
- **No Hurt nor Destroy:**
  - The standard of God’s kingdom is perfect safety and love.
- **Application:**
  - To live there, we must let the Spirit remove the desire to hurt or destroy (malice, anger) from our hearts *now*.

---

## Slide 53 – Q22: Demonstration of Joy

- **Question:** How will the ransomed demonstrate their joy in that place?
- **Key Text:** Isaiah 35:10 – “And the ransomed of the Lord shall return, and come to Zion with songs and everlasting joy upon their heads: they shall obtain joy and gladness, and sorrow and sighing shall flee away.”

## Slide 54 – Q22: Key Points

- **The Ransomed:**
  - Those bought back by the blood of Christ.
- **A Singing People:**
  - The burden of guilt, sickness, and death is gone forever.
- **Everlasting Joy:**
  - Unlike earthly happiness, this joy never fades.
  - God wipes away every tear (Revelation 21:4).

---

## Slide 55 – Q23: Extent of the Kingdom

- **Question:** How extensive will be the reign of Christ at that time?
- **Key Text:** Psalm 72:8 – “He shall have dominion also from sea to sea, and from the river unto the ends of the earth.”

## Slide 56 – Q23: Key Points

- **Global Dominion:**
  - Christ's kingdom is not limited to a region; it covers the globe.
  - "The earth is the Lord's, and the fullness thereof" (Psalm 24:1).
- **One King:**
  - No rival nations or political parties.
  - One Lord, and His name One (Zechariah 14:9).

---

## Slide 57 – Q24: Daniel's Prophecy

- **Question:** What does Daniel say of this kingdom?
- **Key Text:** Daniel 7:27 – “And the kingdom and dominion, and the greatness of the kingdom under the whole heaven, shall be given to the people of the saints of the Most High, whose kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and all dominions shall serve and obey Him.”

## Slide 58 – Q24: Key Points

- **Vindication:**
  - After the judgment, the kingdom is awarded to Christ and His people.
- **Given to the Saints:**
  - We are "joint-heirs" with Christ.
  - God shares His dominion with His redeemed family.
- **The High Calling:**
  - This kingdom is offered freely to all who accept the righteousness of the King.
  - We can have citizenship now by faith, and soon possess it in reality.

## Slide 59 – Q24: Illustration

- **The Soldiers' Return:**
  - When a great war ends, soldiers go home to peace.
  - We are currently on the battlefield of the Great Controversy.
  - **The Promise:** The war will end, the enemy will be defeated, and the soldiers of the cross will inherit the land of peace forever.
