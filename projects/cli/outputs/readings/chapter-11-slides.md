## Slide 1 – Q1: Christ as Divine

- **Question:** Is Christ set forth as a divine being in Holy Writ?
- **Key Text:** Hebrews 1:8, 9
    - “But unto the Son he saith, Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever: a sceptre of righteousness is the sceptre of thy kingdom. Thou hast loved righteousness, and hated iniquity; therefore God, even thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.”

## Slide 2 – Q1: Key Points (Authority & Righteousness)

- **Supreme Authority:**
    - The Father addresses the Son as "God."
    - Christ is not a created being; He is the Creator and King with a throne established forever.
- **The Sceptre of Righteousness:**
    - Christ rules by the law of God, which is the transcript of His character.
    - He does not set aside the law but establishes it.
- **Righteousness by Faith:**
    - Christ "loved righteousness, and hated iniquity."
    - **Sanctification:** The Holy Spirit implants this same nature in the believer.
    - Ezekiel 36:26, 27 – “A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh. And I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes, and ye shall keep my judgments, and do them.”
- **Definition of Iniquity:**
    - 1 John 3:4 – “Whosoever committeth sin transgresseth also the law: for sin is the transgression of the law.”

## Slide 3 – Q1: Illustration

- **The Ambassador vs. The King:**
    - A royal ambassador may speak *for* the king, but he cannot sit *on* the throne.
    - If a subject rebels against the law, only the King has the authority to pardon the rebel.
    - Only the King can pay the debt from his own treasury.
- **Application:**
    - Because Jesus is God, His sacrifice is infinite.
    - He alone can cover the sins of the whole world.

## Slide 4 – Q2: Pre-existence of Christ

- **Question:** Did Christ exist before He was manifested in the flesh?
- **Key Text:** John 17:5
    - “And now, O Father, glorify thou me with thine own self with the glory which I had with thee before the world was.”

## Slide 5 – Q2: Key Points (Eternity & Sanctuary)

- **Pre-existence:**
    - Christ was not merely a good man elevated to godhood; He existed from eternity.
    - Micah 5:2 – “But thou, Bethlehem Ephratah, though thou be little among the thousands of Judah, yet out of thee shall he come forth unto me that is to be ruler in Israel; whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting.”
- **The Covenant of Peace:**
    - The plan of salvation was a counsel between the Father and Son before creation.
    - Zechariah 6:13 – “Even he shall build the temple of the LORD; and he shall bear the glory, and shall sit and rule upon his throne; and he shall be a priest upon his throne: and the counsel of peace shall be between them both.”
- **Sanctuary Connection:**
    - The Atonement was secured in the heart of God before sin entered.
    - Revelation 13:8 – “...the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world.”

## Slide 6 – Q2: Illustration

- **The Sun and the Light:**
    - Rays of light flow from the sun.
    - Yet, as long as the sun has existed, the light has existed.
    - You cannot have the sun without the light.
- **Application:**
    - Christ is the "brightness of his glory" (Hebrews 1:3).
    - He is eternally co-existent with the Father.

## Slide 7 – Q3: The Incarnation

- **Question:** By what agency was His incarnation effected?
- **Key Text:** Luke 1:35
    - “And the angel answered and said unto her, The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee, and the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee: therefore also that holy thing which shall be born of thee shall be called the Son of God.”

## Slide 8 – Q3: Key Points (The Holy Spirit’s Work)

- **The Holy Thing:**
    - Jesus took upon Himself the seed of Abraham, yet was born without inherited guilt.
    - Galatians 4:4 – “But when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law.”
    - Hebrews 2:16 – “For verily he took not on him the nature of angels; but he took on him the seed of Abraham.”
- **Righteousness by Faith Application:**
    - Just as Christ was formed in Mary by the Holy Spirit, He must be formed in us.
    - Galatians 4:19 – “My little children, of whom I travail in birth again until Christ be formed in you.”
    - Dependence on the Spirit is the secret to the new birth.

## Slide 9 – Q3: Illustration

- **The Grafting:**
    - A gardener grafts a branch from a superior fruit tree into a wild, bitter stock.
    - The life of the good branch flows into the trunk, producing sweet fruit.
- **Application:**
    - In the incarnation, divine life was grafted into humanity.
    - 2 Peter 1:4 – “Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises: that by these ye might be partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption that is in the world through lust.”

## Slide 10 – Q4: Manifested in Flesh

- **Question:** Did His incarnation annul His divinity?
- **Key Text:** 1 Timothy 3:16
    - “And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, received up into glory.”

## Slide 11 – Q4: Key Points (Mystery of Godliness)

- **Veiled, Not Voided:**
    - Christ veiled His glory to draw near to sinners, but He never ceased to be God.
- **Justified in the Spirit:**
    - The Spirit vindicated His claim to be the Son of God through His holy life and resurrection.
    - Romans 1:4 – “And declared to be the Son of God with power, according to the spirit of holiness, by the resurrection from the dead.”
- **Sanctuary Imagery:**
    - His flesh is compared to the veil in the sanctuary.
    - Hebrews 10:20 – “By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh.”
    - The fullness of the Godhead dwelt in the temple of His body.

## Slide 12 – Q4: Illustration

- **The King in Laborer’s Clothes:**
    - Imagine a King putting on the clothes of a common laborer to work with his people.
    - He sweats like a laborer, but under the shirt beats the heart of the King.
- **Application:**
    - His rank has not changed.
    - Only His outward manifestation has changed.

## Slide 13 – Q5: Relation to the Father

- **Question:** How does Christ Himself speak of His relation to the Father?
- **Key Text:** John 10:30
    - “I and My Father are one.”

## Slide 14 – Q5: Key Points (Unity & Atonement)

- **Absolute Oneness:**
    - Unity of Essence: Sharing the same divine nature.
    - Unity of Authority: To obey the Son is to obey the Father.
- **Implication for Atonement:**
    - The Atonement was not an angry Father punishing an innocent Son.
    - God and Christ were united in the work of saving man.
    - 2 Corinthians 5:19 – “To wit, that God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them; and hath committed unto us the word of reconciliation.”

## Slide 15 – Q6: Defining "One"

- **Question:** How does He show what He means by being "one with the Father?
- **Key Text:** John 17:11
    - “And now I am no more in the world, but these are in the world, and I come to thee. Holy Father, keep through thine own name those whom thou hast given me, that they may be one, as we are.”

## Slide 16 – Q6: Key Points (Sanctification & Unity)

- **Unity of Purpose:**
    - Not just mathematical oneness, but perfect harmony of character and spirit.
- **The Goal of Sanctification:**
    - To have the "mind of Christ" (Philippians 2:5).
    - John 17:23 – “I in them, and thou in me, that they may be made perfect in one; and that the world may know that thou hast sent me, and hast loved them, as thou hast loved me.”
- **Definition of Atonement:**
    - Literally "At-one-ment."
    - The condition of being at one with God in will and character.

## Slide 17 – Q6: Illustration

- **The Musical Chord:**
    - Different notes, when played perfectly together, form one harmonious sound.
    - If one note is out of tune (sin), the harmony is broken.
- **Application:**
    - Christ tunes the human soul back to the pitch of heaven.
    - We become "one" with the Father as He is.

## Slide 18 – Q7: Manifested as Saviour

- **Question:** How was He manifested on the earth as a Saviour?
- **Key Texts:**
    - Luke 2:10, 11 – “...For unto you is born this day in the city of David a Saviour, which is Christ the Lord.”
    - John 1:14 – “And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us... full of grace and truth.”

## Slide 19 – Q7: Key Points (Grace & Truth)

- **Christ the Lord:**
    - Identified as Messiah (Christ) and Jehovah (Lord).
- **Mercy and Truth Met Together:**
    - Psalm 85:10 – “Mercy and truth are met together; righteousness and peace have kissed each other.”
    - **Grace:** Pardon for the past (Justification).
    - **Truth:** The standard of God's law (Sanctification).
    - Psalm 119:142 – “Thy righteousness is an everlasting righteousness, and thy law is the truth.”
- **Righteousness by Faith:**
    - He saves us *from* our sins, not *in* them.
    - Matthew 1:21 – “And she shall bring forth a son, and thou shalt call his name JESUS: for he shall save his people from their sins.”

## Slide 20 – Q8: Purpose of Incarnation

- **Question:** Why was He thus made known?
- **Key Text:** Hebrews 2:17
    - “Wherefore in all things it behooved him to be made like unto his brethren, that he might be a merciful and faithful high priest in things pertaining to God, to make reconciliation for the sins of the people.”

## Slide 21 – Q8: Key Points (The High Priest)

- **Necessity of Humanity:**
    - To mediate, He must touch the Father's throne (Divinity) and the sinner's infirmity (Humanity).
- **Faithful High Priest:**
    - He knows the cost of resisting temptation.
- **Reconciliation:**
    - His death paid the penalty; His life provides the power.
- **Sanctuary Truth:**
    - Jesus is alive in the Heavenly Sanctuary.
    - He is applying the benefits of His sacrifice to those who come by faith.

## Slide 22 – Q8: Illustration

- **The Bridge Guide:**
    - Crossing a dangerous chasm requires a guide who knows every loose plank.
    - You trust a guide who has crossed it before.
- **Application:**
    - Jesus became man to cross the bridge of life.
    - He faced every storm we face.
    - He turns back to guide us safely across.

## Slide 23 – Q9: The Father’s Recognition

- **Question:** How was He recognized by the Father while here?
- **Key Text:** Matthew 3:17
    - “And lo a voice from heaven, saying, This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased.”

## Slide 24 – Q9: Key Points (Acceptance)

- **Acceptance of Humanity:**
    - At the Jordan, God accepted Jesus as the second Adam, representing the race.
- **Justification by Faith:**
    - Because the Father is pleased with Jesus, He is pleased with us when we are "in Christ."
    - Ephesians 1:6 – “To the praise of the glory of his grace, wherein he hath made us accepted in the beloved.”
- **Sanctuary Type:**
    - The sacrifice must be examined and found without blemish.
    - The Father examined Christ’s life and declared it perfect.

## Slide 25 – Q10: Evidence of Divinity

- **Question:** How was the divinity of the Son of God shown?
- **Overview:**
    - The Scriptures provide a five-fold witness to His divinity.
    - Proof that He is able to save to the uttermost.

## Slide 26 – Q10: Points 1 & 2 (Creation & Miracles)

- **1. Creative Power:**
    - Colossians 1:16 – “For by him were all things created, that are in heaven, and that are in earth...”
    - **Application:** Conversion is a "new creation" (2 Corinthians 5:17). Only the Creator can re-create the heart.
- **2. Power over Nature and Death:**
    - John 11:43, 44 – “And when he thus had spoken, he cried with a loud voice, Lazarus, come forth. And he that was dead came forth...”
    - **Application:** No habit is so deadening that Christ cannot break it.

## Slide 27 – Q10: Points 3 & 4 (Life & Titles)

- **3. Power to Lay Down and Take Up Life:**
    - John 10:17 – “Therefore doth my Father love me, because I lay down my life, that I might take it again.”
    - He possesses original, unborrowed life.
- **4. Recognized as God:**
    - Hebrews 1:8 – “But unto the Son he saith, Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever...”
    - Isaiah 9:6 – “For unto us a child is born... and his name shall be called Wonderful, Counsellor, The mighty God, The everlasting Father, The Prince of Peace.”

## Slide 28 – Q10: Point 5 (Atonement & Justification)

- **5. Substitutionary Atonement:**
    - Only an infinite God could pay an infinite debt.
    - Isaiah 53:11 – “He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied: by his knowledge shall my righteous servant justify many; for he shall bear their iniquities.”
- **Justification:**
    - God's legal declaration that a sinner is accounted righteous.
    - Romans 3:24 – “Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus.”
    - He bears our iniquities; we receive His righteousness.

## Slide 29 – Q11: Paul’s Testimony

- **Question:** How does Paul speak of Christ's relation to the Father?
- **Key Text:** Colossians 2:9
    - “For in him dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily.”

## Slide 30 – Q11: Key Points (Fulness in Christ)

- **The Fulness:**
    - Not a spark or emanation, but the *entirety* of Deity.
- **Bodily:**
    - Even now, a Man on the throne of heaven holds this fullness.
- **Righteousness by Faith:**
    - Colossians 2:10 – “And ye are complete in him, which is the head of all principality and power.”
    - Because we are "in Christ," we have access to all His power to obey and overcome.

## Slide 31 – Q11: Illustration

- **The Diver:**
    - A diver in the ocean is surrounded by the fullness of the water.
    - He does not contain the ocean, but the ocean contains him.
- **Application:**
    - When we are "in Christ," we are surrounded by the fullness of the Godhead.
    - Everything God has is available to us through Him.

## Slide 32 – Q12: Our Privilege

- **Question:** What is our privilege in view of what this precious Saviour has done?
- **Key Text:** Hebrews 4:15, 16
    - “For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin. Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need.”

## Slide 33 – Q12: Key Points (Throne of Grace)

- **Touched with Infirmities:**
    - Jesus feels our struggles and knows the pull of temptation.
- **Without Sin:**
    - He is our Substitute (dying for us) and Example (living within us).
- **Two Gifts at the Throne:**
    1. **Mercy:** Forgiveness for past guilt (Justification).
    2. **Grace to Help:** Power for the present struggle (Sanctification).
- **"Time of Need":**
    - The moment of temptation. We must come *before* we fall.

## Slide 34 – Q12: Illustration

- **The Child and the Judge:**
    - A child who broke a window fears the judge.
    - But if the judge is his own father, who paid for the window, fear turns to confidence.
- **Application:**
    - We come "boldly" not with arrogance, but with trust in the Father's love.
    - Christ, our Brother and our God, is on the throne.
