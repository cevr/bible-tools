## Deeper Truths

## Practical Application

### Recognizing Envy in Ourselves

Envy often disguises itself. Ask honestly:

- Do I feel discomfort when others are praised or promoted?
- Am I secretly pleased when a rival fails?
- Do I find fault with those who succeed where I have not?
- Do I minimize others' accomplishments or attribute them to unfair advantage?

If so, envy has found lodging in the heart.

### The Only Remedy

Human effort cannot uproot envy. We may suppress its outward expression, but the root remains. Only Christ can give us a new heart:

> _"A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh."_ (Ezekiel 36:26)

This is righteousness by faithâ€”not struggling to feel loving, but receiving Christ's love as a gift. When His life flows through us, we can genuinely rejoice in another's blessing, for we see all good as coming from the same gracious Father.

### Daily Surrender

Victory over envy requires:

1. **Honest confession**: Acknowledge envious thoughts as sin, not mere weakness
2. **Daily surrender**: Give the heart to Christ each morning before temptation comes
3. **Active blessing**: Pray specifically for those you are tempted to envy
4. **Gratitude**: Cultivate thankfulness for your own blessings rather than comparison with others
5. **Eternal perspective**: Remember that earthly advantages are temporary; character alone endures
