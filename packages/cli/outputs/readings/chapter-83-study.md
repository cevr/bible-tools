## Deeper Truths

### How Can Any Power Exalt Itself Above God?

[Q] **"How could any earthly power possibly exalt itself above the Almighty Creator?"**

The only way to truly exalt oneself **above** God is to:

1. **Change some portion of God's law**
2. **Require obedience to the changed law instead of God's original**

- **God's name**: "The LORD thy God"

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Central Issue: Authority

The great controversy between Christ and Satan has always centered on one fundamental question: **Whose authority will be obeyed?**

In Eden, Satan's temptation challenged God's word: "Hath God said?" (Genesis 3:1). The serpent suggested that God's command could be modified, reinterpreted, or set aside.

In these last days, the same battle rages. The Sabbath becomes the testing truth precisely because it answers the question: Will I obey God's explicit command, or will I follow human tradition?

> _"In vain they do worship me, teaching for doctrines the commandments of men."_ (Matthew 15:9)

### Sanctuary Connection

In the Most Holy Place of the heavenly sanctuary, where Christ now ministers as our High Priest, stands the ark containing the ten commandmentsâ€”the foundation of God's government (Revelation 11:19). The law that the earthly power has "thought to change" remains unchanged in heaven.

During the investigative judgment, now taking place, each case is measured against this unchanging standard:

> _"Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters."_ (Revelation 14:7)

This first angel's message echoes the very language of the fourth commandment, calling the world back to worship of the Creator on His appointed day.

### Righteousness by Faith and Sabbath Keeping

[Q] **"Doesn't keeping the Sabbath make salvation a matter of works rather than faith?"**

Never! True Sabbath keeping flows from a heart made new by Christ. We do not keep the Sabbath to _earn_ salvationâ€”that is impossible (Ephesians 2:8-9). Rather, we keep it because:

1. **We love God and desire to obey Him** (John 14:15)
2. **Christ's righteousness within us produces obedience** (Philippians 2:13)
3. **The Sabbath is a sign of sanctification**â€”God's work in us, not our work for Him (Ezekiel 20:12)

The Sabbath is not our work for God; it is our rest in His completed workâ€”both creation and redemption. We cease from our labors as God ceased from His, trusting wholly in Christ's merits rather than our own efforts.

## Practical Application

This prophetic truth calls us to:

### 1. Search the Scriptures Personally

Like the Bereans, we must examine "whether those things were so" (Acts 17:11). No church tradition, however ancient, can override a plain "Thus saith the Lord."

### 2. Choose Allegiance Consciously

Every Sabbath, we declare by our actions whose authority we acknowledge. There is no neutral ground.

> _"Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey?"_ (Romans 6:16)

### 3. Trust God for Consequences

Sabbath keeping may bring inconvenience, misunderstanding, or even persecution. But God's promise stands:

> _"Them that honour me I will honour."_ (1 Samuel 2:30)

### 4. Rest in Christ's Power

We keep the Sabbath not by self-effort but by the indwelling life of Christ. He who commanded the Sabbath also empowers its keeping.
