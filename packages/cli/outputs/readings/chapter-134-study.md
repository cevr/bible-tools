## Practical Application

This truth calls us to:

1. **Prioritize wisdom above all earthly pursuits.** Career advancement, financial security, and social status are secondary. Ask yourself daily: "Am I growing in the wisdom that comes from God?"

2. **Ask God for wisdom in faith.** Not hesitantly, not doubting, but confidently claiming His promise (James 1:5). Make this a regular part of your prayer life: "Lord, give me wisdom for this decision, this relationship, this challenge."

3. **Cultivate the fear of the Lord.** Spend time contemplating God's holiness, His majesty, His power over all creation. Let awe and reverence grow in your heart. This is not optional mysticismâ€”it is the very foundation of wisdom.

4. **Obey what you already know.** Understanding increases through obedience (Psalm 111:10). If you neglect known duty, you cannot expect greater light. Walk in the light you have, and more light will come.

5. **Surrender to the renewing of your mind.** Allow the Holy Spirit access to your thoughts, attitudes, and assumptions. Read Scripture not merely for information but for transformation.

6. **Reject worldly wisdom.** The counsel of the ungodly, the philosophy of secularism, the values of popular cultureâ€”these must be tested by Scripture and, where they conflict, rejected. "Cease, my son, to hear the instruction that causeth to err from the words of knowledge" (Proverbs 19:27).

7. **Let your face shine.** As you receive wisdom from above, let it manifest in your countenance and conduct. Others should see in you the peace, gentleness, and purity that only heaven can give.
