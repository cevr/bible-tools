## Core Truths

### The Promise of Elijah's Coming

God declared through Malachi that before "the great and dreadful day of the Lord," He would send Elijah the prophet (Malachi 4:5). This prophecy carried profound significance for Israel, as Elijah had never died but was translated to heaven (2 Kings 2:11).

> _"Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the Lord: and he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the children, and the heart of the children to their fathers, lest I come and smite the earth with a curse."_ (Malachi 4:5â€“6)

### John the Baptist: A Partial Fulfillment

Jesus Himself identified John the Baptist as the Elijah promised in Malachi's prophecy:

The "Elijah message" is not about a single individual but about a specific work: calling God's people to decisive commitment and preparing them for a divine visitation.

## Deeper Truths

### The Elijah Spirit and Work

What characterized Elijah's ministry? At Mount Carmel, facing a backslidden nation, Elijah delivered a piercing challenge:

> _"How long halt ye between two opinions? if the LORD be God, follow him: but if Baal, then follow him."_ (1 Kings 18:21)

- A call to decisionâ€”no more halting between opinions
- A return to the worship of the true God

### The Day of the Lord Still Future

The "great and dreadful day of the Lord" prophesied by Malachi was still future in Peter's time:

## Practical Application

### What the Elijah Message Demands of Us Today

The spirit of Carmel demands decision. We cannot serve God and mammon (Matthew 6:24). Every compromise with worldliness, every divided loyalty, every postponed commitment weakens the soul for the final crisis. Now is the time to settle, by God's grace, that we will follow the Lord wholly.
