## Core Truths

### The Divine Call to Purity

Paul's simple injunction to Timothy encapsulates the Christian standard: _"Keep thyself pure"_ (1 Timothy 5:22). This is not mere outward conformity, but a comprehensive purification that reaches to the depths of one's being.

The apostle expands this principle with earnest appeal:

> _"Having therefore these promises, dearly beloved, let us cleanse ourselves from all filthiness of the flesh and spirit, perfecting holiness in the fear of God."_ (2 Corinthians 7:1)

Notice the completeness of this commandâ€”**all** filthiness, both of **flesh** (physical habits) and **spirit** (mental and moral defilement). The Christian who desires to be wholly the Lord's cannot pick and choose which polluting influences to abandon.

### The Body as God's Temple

The solemn warning of 1 Corinthians 3:17 reveals how seriously God regards the care of our bodies:

> _"If any man defile the temple of God, him shall God destroy; for the temple of God is holy, which temple ye are."_

Under the old covenant, severe penalties attended any who profaned the sanctuary. > _"What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your own? For ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God's."_ (1 Corinthians 6:19â€“20)

## Deeper Truths

### Separation from Defilement

> _"Wherefore come out from among them, and be ye separate, saith the Lord, and touch not the unclean thing; and I will receive you."_ (2 Corinthians 6:17)

God's people are called to be distinct from the world in their habits. The promise attached to this separation is precious: **"I will receive you."** The Father longs to draw near to His children, but defilement creates a barrier. Just as the high priest could not enter the Most Holy Place while ceremonially unclean, so polluting habits hinder our access to God's fullest blessing.

### The Testimony of Science Regarding Tobacco

While Scripture gives principles, modern science confirms their wisdom. Tobacco, unknown in biblical lands, emerged from the customs of pagan peoples who had no knowledge of the true God.

Its effects touch nearly every organ systemâ€”the blood, heart, brain, nerves, and respiratory system all suffer injury. The gardener uses tobacco oil to kill noxious insects; the Hottentot uses it to slay serpentsâ€”and yet millions introduce this same poison into bodies designed to be temples of the Holy Spirit.

Our bodies are the palace God has prepared for His Spirit. When we defile them with tobacco, we show contempt for the gift and grieve the Giver.

### The Subtler Dangers: Tea, Coffee, and Stimulants

Many who would never touch alcohol or tobacco freely partake of tea and coffee, supposing these to be harmless. Yet scientific investigation reveals that these common beverages contain harmful substances that undermine health.

- **Theine** (caffeine) belongs to the same class of poisons as strychnine
- **Tannin** interferes with digestion and irritates the stomach lining
- Even "moderate" use establishes a tolerance that masks accumulating harm

[DYKðŸ”Ž] Dr. Bock of Leipzig attributed much of the "nervousness and peevishness" of modern times to tea and coffee consumption. The stimulant creates artificial energy, then leaves the user depletedâ€”leading to irritability, anxiety, and digestive complaints that "react upon the brain."

[Q] **"But I only use these things moderately. Surely that's acceptable?"**

The argument for "moderate" use of harmful substances has been used to defend every intemperate practice. Yet the essential difference between liberal and moderate use of a poison is simply that one causes great harm and the other causes less. Would we counsel a friend to take only small doses of arsenic? Total abstinence from all substances that injure the body is the only position consistent with Scripture's call to purity and with glorifying God in our bodies.

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary and Bodily Purity

The sanctuary message illuminates why God cares so deeply about what we put into our bodies. In the earthly tabernacle:

- **The outer court** represented the bodyâ€”where sacrifices were offered and cleansing took place
- **The holy place** represented the mind and character being transformed through communion with God (the bread, lamp, and incense)
- **The Most Holy Place** represented the deepest union with God, where His law resided in the ark

Just as the sanctuary had to be cleansed of all defilement for the priest to minister effectively, so our bodies must be purified for the Holy Spirit to work unhindered. The investigative judgment now proceeding in heaven's sanctuary calls for a corresponding cleansing of God's people on earthâ€”a cleansing that includes our physical habits (Daniel 8:14; 1 John 3:3).

### Righteousness by Faith and Victory Over Appetite

The appetite is one of Satan's most powerful weapons. In Eden, he tempted Eve through appetite; in the wilderness, he tempted Christ the same way. Every victory over appetite is a testimony to the power of Christ working in human weakness.

But here is the gospel's promise: we need not fight these battles in our own strength. Christ overcame every temptation "in the likeness of sinful flesh" (Romans 8:3), and His victory is ours by faith. When we surrender our habitsâ€”even deeply entrenched onesâ€”to Jesus, He provides supernatural power to overcome.

> _"I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me."_ (Philippians 4:13)

This is not legalism but love responding to love. We abandon defiling habits not to earn salvation but because we have been "bought with a price" and long to honor the One who gave everything for us.

### The Hope That Purifies

The apostle John connects our hope of Christ's return with personal purification:

## Practical Application

### Steps to Freedom

1. **Acknowledge the problem honestly** before God. Confess any enslaving habit as sin against your body-temple (1 John 1:9).

2. **Claim Christ's victory** by faith. He overcame appetite in the wilderness; His strength is available to you (Hebrews 4:15â€“16).

3. **Remove temptation** from your environment. Do not test your willpower by keeping harmful substances within reach (Romans 13:14).

4. **Replace harmful habits** with healthful ones. Fill the void with prayer, Scripture study, physical activity, and wholesome refreshment.

5. **Seek support** from fellow believers. Accountability strengthens resolve (Ecclesiastes 4:9â€“10).

6. **Trust the process**. Victory may come gradually rather than instantly, but each day of faithfulness builds strength for the next.

### Questions for Self-Examination

- Am I glorifying God in what I eat and drink?
- Would I be comfortable if Jesus returned while I was engaged in this practice?
- Am I treating my body as a purchased possession belonging to Christ?
