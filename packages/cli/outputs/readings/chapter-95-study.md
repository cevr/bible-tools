## Core Truths

### The Danger of Human Tradition

> _"But in vain they do worship Me, teaching for doctrines the commandments of

The Pharisees had devised a tradition called "Corban" that allowed men to neglect

making _"the commandment of God of none effect by your tradition"_ (Matthew
15:6).

**The principle is clear**: Any traditionâ€”however ancient, however widely

### Two Plantings: Divine and Human

When the disciples reported that the Pharisees were offended by Christ's rebuke,
Jesus answered with a solemn principle:

> _"Every plant which My heavenly Father hath not planted, shall be rooted up."_
> (Matthew 15:13)

This raises a vital question for every religious institution and practice: **Who
planted it?**

**The seventh-day Sabbath** was planted by God Himself at creation:

> _"For in six days the Lord made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them
> is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the Lord blessed the Sabbath day, and
> hallowed it."_ (Exodus 20:11)

Three divine acts established the Sabbath:

1. **God rested** on the seventh day (Genesis 2:2)
2. **God blessed** the seventh day (Genesis 2:3)
3. **God sanctified** (set apart as holy) the seventh day (Genesis 2:3)

No other day in all of Scripture receives this threefold distinction.

**The Sunday institution**, by contrast, claims a different origin. Note this
remarkable admission from Roman Catholic sources:

> God?_

> commandment, instead of the ancient Sabbath."_
>

[Q] **"But doesn't the New Testament teach that Sunday replaced the Sabbath?"**

Scripture records no such change. Jesus kept the Sabbath (Luke 4:16). The
apostles kept it decades after the resurrection (Acts 13:14, 42, 44; 16:13;
17:2; 18:4). The change came gradually through human authority, not divine
command. When we compare Matthew 15:13 with this historical admission, the
conclusion is unavoidable: what man has planted, God will uproot.

## Deeper Truths

### A Last-Day Blessing for Sabbath-Keepers

Isaiah 56 contains a remarkable prophecy that speaks directly to our time:

> _"Thus saith the Lord, Keep ye judgment, and do justice: for My salvation is

Notice the **time setting**: "My salvation is near to come." Peter confirms this
refers to the last days:

> _"Who are kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation ready to be

### The Sabbath Promise Extends to All Nations

Some object that the Sabbath was only for Jews. But Isaiah explicitly includes

> _"Also the sons of the stranger, that join themselves to the Lord, to serve
> Him, and to love the name of the Lord, to be His servants, every one that
> keepeth the Sabbath from polluting it, and taketh hold of My covenant; even
> them will I bring to My holy mountain, and make them joyful in My house of
> prayer."_ (Isaiah 56:6-7)

And what will these faithful Gentile Sabbath-keepers receive?

> _"Even unto them will I give in Mine house and within My walls a place and a

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary Connection

The Sabbath finds its deepest meaning in the sanctuary:

- **The Sabbath points to the Creator**â€”and the Most Holy Place contains the ark
  with God's law, including the Sabbath commandment (Revelation 11:19)
- **The Sabbath is a sign of sanctification** (Ezekiel 20:12)â€”the very work
  Christ performs as our High Priest in the heavenly sanctuary

  commandmentsâ€”including the Sabbath (Ecclesiastes 12:13-14; Revelation 14:7, 12)

The Sabbath is not merely a day; it is **a test of loyalty** in the great

### Untempered Mortar: The Wall That Will Fall

Ezekiel describes religious teachers who hide their eyes from God's Sabbaths and

> stormy wind shall rend it."_ (Ezekiel 13:11)

When do these hailstones fall?

> _"Hast thou seen the treasures of the hail, which I have reserved against the
> time of trouble, against the day of battle and war?"_ (Job 38:22-23)

Under the **seventh plague**, great hailstones fall upon those who have clung to
false religious systems (Revelation 16:17-21). Every wall built on human
tradition rather than divine truth will collapse.

### The Third Angel's Message

The final warning to the world centers on commandment-keeping:

> _"And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man

### Sabbath in Eternity

The Sabbath is not a temporary institution to pass away. It will endure through
all eternity:

> _"For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I will make, shall remain
> before Me, saith the Lord, so shall your seed and your name remain. And it
> shall come to pass, that from one new moon to another, and from one Sabbath to
> another, shall all flesh come to worship before Me, saith the Lord."_ (Isaiah
> 66:22-23)

What began in Eden will continue in the earth made new. The Sabbath is the
eternal bond between Creator and creation, a weekly celebration of His power, His
love, and His finished work.
