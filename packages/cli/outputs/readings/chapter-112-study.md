### The Problem with the Immortal Soul Theory

2. **Why would Paul disclaim any desire for this state?** If death meant instant heavenly bliss, why would he say, "not for that we would be unclothed"?

3. **Why would Paul groan for the redemption of the body?** If the soul is already in glory, what urgent need exists for bodily resurrection?

4. **How could the "house from heaven" be said to be "eternal in the heavens" if believers already possess immortal souls?** This house is _future_â€”we do not yet have it except by faith and promise.

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Resurrectionâ€”God's Answer to Death

### Sanctuary Connections

In the earthly sanctuary, the high priest bore the names of all Israel upon his heart when he entered the Most Holy Place on the Day of Atonement (Exodus 28:29). The people waited outside, their fate bound up with his ministry within.

Paul's hopeâ€”and oursâ€”is not a disembodied, ghostly existence, but a bodily resurrection at Christ's glorious appearing.

### Righteousness by Faith and the Resurrection Hope

Paul's confidence in the resurrection rested not on any inherent immortality, but on his union with Christ:

> _"He that hath the Son hath life."_ (1 John 5:12)
