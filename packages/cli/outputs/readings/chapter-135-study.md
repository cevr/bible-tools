> _"Submit yourselves therefore to God."_ (James 4:7)

## Deeper Truths

## Christ Our Example

How was submission perfectly exemplified? In Christ Himself:

> _"He humbled Himself, and became obedient unto death, even the death of the cross."_ (Philippians 2:8)

## Submission to Human Authorities

### Civil Government

Scripture extends the principle of submission to earthly authorities:

> _"Put them in mind to be subject to principalities and powers, to obey magistrates."_ (Titus 3:1)

> _"Let every soul be subject unto the higher powers. For there is no power but of God: the powers that be are ordained of God. Whosoever therefore resisteth the power, resisteth the ordinance of God."_ (Romans 13:1-2)

> _"Submit yourselves to every ordinance of man for the Lord's sake... for so is the will of God."_ (1 Peter 2:13-15)

Why does God require this? Because civil government is part of God's ordained order. Even imperfect governments serve divine purposes in restraining evil and maintaining social order.

### The Vital Exception

[Q] **"Does this mean Christians must obey government even when it commands sin?"**

Peter himself answers this critical question:

> _"We ought to obey God rather than men."_ (Acts 5:29)

> _"Whether it be right in the sight of God to hearken unto you more than unto God, judge ye."_ (Acts 4:19)

When human law conflicts with divine law, God's authority takes precedence. The three Hebrew worthiesâ€”Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednegoâ€”exemplified this principle. When commanded to worship an idol, they respectfully but firmly refused:

> _"Be it known unto thee, O king, that we will not serve thy gods, nor worship the golden image which thou hast set up."_ (Daniel 3:18)

They submitted to the consequence (the fiery furnace) while refusing to sin against God. This is faithful submission properly understood.

### The True Scope of Civil Obedience

What does obedience to civil authority actually mean in practice?

> _"For this, Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not covet; and if there be any other commandment, it is briefly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself."_ (Romans 13:9)

Civil law, at its best, reflects moral lawâ€”the second table of the Ten Commandments dealing with our duty to fellow humans. Christians are to be model citizens, fulfilling civic duties and demonstrating that godliness produces the most law-abiding, helpful, and trustworthy members of society.

## Submission in Relationships

### Mutual Submission Among Believers

> _"Likewise, ye younger, submit yourselves unto the elder. Yea, all of you be subject one to another, and be clothed with humility."_ (1 Peter 5:5)

### Marriage: Mutual Honor, Not Domination

The wife is called to a specific expression of submission:

> _"Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands."_ (Colossians 3:18)

Titus expands what this means practically: _"To be sober, to love their husbands, to love their children, to be discreet, chaste, keepers at home, good, obedient to their own husbands."_ (Titus 2:4-5)

[Q] **"Does wifely submission permit husbands to dominate and mistreat their wives?"**

Absolutely not. The same passage immediately constrains husbands:

> _"Husbands, love your wives, and be not bitter against them."_ (Colossians 3:19)

> _"Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave Himself for it."_ (Ephesians 5:25)

A husband who truly loves as Christ loves does not dominateâ€”he serves, protects, and sacrifices. Christ's love was self-giving, not self-serving. Any interpretation that justifies abuse or tyranny misses the mutual respect Scripture establishes.

Furthermore, wifely submission has a qualifier:

> _"Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, **as it is fit in the Lord**."_ (Colossians 3:18)

Submission is "fit" only within the boundaries of righteousness. A wife is never required to follow a husband into sin.

### Children and Parents

> _"Children, obey your parents in all things: for this is well pleasing unto the Lord."_ (Colossians 3:20)

This submission is part of God's order for the home, training children in the way of godly authority before they can fully understand divine authority.

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### Submission: The Heart of the Great Controversy

The great controversy began when Lucifer refused submission to God. He exalted himself, claiming equality with the Most High (Isaiah 14:12-14). Prideâ€”the refusal to submitâ€”is the original sin and the root of every sin since.

In contrast, Christ demonstrated perfect submission even unto death. The Father's plan of salvation required the Son's willing obedience. In Gethsemane, we hear the ultimate prayer of submission:

> _"Not My will, but Thine, be done."_ (Luke 22:42)

Every human being faces this same choice: Will I submit to God's authority, or assert my own? The redeemed are those who, like Christ, choose the Father's will over self-will.

### The Sanctuary and Submission

In the earthly sanctuary, submission was demonstrated at the altar of sacrifice in the outer court. The sinner brought an animal, laid hands upon its head, confessed sins, and then took its life. This pointed forward to Christ's ultimate submission at Calvary.

In the Holy Place, the priest daily ministeredâ€”maintaining the lamps, offering incense, presenting the shewbread. Each service required careful obedience to God's specific instructions. Careless deviation brought death, as Nadab and Abihu discovered (Leviticus 10:1-2).

In the Most Holy Place, on the Day of Atonement, the high priest entered with utmost reverence and precision. This foreshadowed our Lord's ministry in the heavenly sanctuary, where He now intercedes for us. Those who will be found faithful when Christ completes His work are those who have learned full submission to God.

[DYKðŸ”Ž] The Hebrew word for "atonement" (_kaphar_) includes the idea of covering and reconciliation. Through submission to God's plan of salvation, we are brought back into harmonyâ€”"at-one-ment"â€”with our Creator.

### Righteousness by Faith and Submission

Here is the vital connection: **true submission to God IS righteousness by faith in action.**

We cannot submit in our own strength. The carnal mind is "not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be" (Romans 8:7). Self cannot crucify self. Only as Christ dwells in us by faith can genuine submission become possible.

When we surrender to Godâ€”confessing our inability and claiming Christ's powerâ€”His Spirit works within us both to will and to do of His good pleasure (Philippians 2:13). The righteousness of the law is then "fulfilled in us who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit" (Romans 8:4).

This is not legalism. Legalism is trying to obey God in human strength to earn favor. Righteousness by faith is receiving Christ's power to obey because we already have His favor through grace.

## Practical Application

What does a life of submission look like in daily practice?

### In Your Relationship with God:

- **Morning surrender**: Begin each day with the prayer, "Lord, Thy will be done in me today"
- **Scripture meditation**: Let God's Word shape your thinking rather than worldly influences
- **Instant obedience**: When the Spirit convicts, respond without argument or delay
- **Thought governance**: Practice bringing wandering or rebellious thoughts captive to Christ

### In Your Relationships:

- **Honor authority**: Submit to government, employers, and church leaders where not contrary to God's law
- **Practice humility**: Consider others' interests, not only your own (Philippians 2:4)
- **Guard your tongue**: Speak only what builds up and honors God
- **In marriage**: Husbandsâ€”love sacrificially; wivesâ€”honor respectfully
- **As parents**: Exercise loving authority that reflects God's character
- **As children**: Obey cheerfully, recognizing parents as God's representatives

### When Faced with Conflict:

- Distinguish between God's clear commands and human preferences
- Be willing to suffer rather than sin
- Maintain respectful demeanor even when you cannot comply
- Trust God with the consequences of faithful obedience
