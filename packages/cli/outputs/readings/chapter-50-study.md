# Reading 50: The First Angel's Message

## Introduction

In the closing chapters of earth's history, God sends a final, urgent appeal to humanity through three angels bearing heaven's last messages of mercy. The first of these messages announces the arrival of a solemn hourâ€”"the hour of His judgment is come"â€”and calls all people to worship the Creator. ## Core Truths

### God Has Appointed a Day of Judgment

The judgment is not a vague, uncertain future event. Scripture declares with absolute certainty that God has fixed a definite time:

> _"Because He hath appointed a day, in the which He will judge the world in righteousness by that man whom He hath ordained."_ (Acts 17:31)

**Judgment**: God's examination and evaluation of every human life, determining eternal destiny based on one's response to Christ and His truth (Ecclesiastes 12:14; 2 Corinthians 5:10).

This truth teaches us that:

- God is orderly and purposeful in all His works
- History is moving toward a divine climax
- Every soul must give account to the Creator

### God Reveals His Plans to His Servants

The Almighty does not act in secret, leaving humanity unprepared:

> _"Surely the Lord God will do nothing, but He revealeth His secret unto His servants the prophets."_ (Amos 3:7)

This principle runs throughout Scripture. God warned Noah of the flood, Abraham of Sodom's destruction, and Joseph of the coming famine. Before every major divine intervention, heaven sends warning through faithful messengers.

[Q] **"If God is loving, why would He judge the world?"**

Judgment is actually an expression of God's love and justice. Without judgment, sin would go uncorrected, the wicked would forever oppress the righteous, and questions about God's character would remain unanswered. The judgment vindicates those who have trusted in Christ while demonstrating to the universe that God has dealt fairly with every soul. It is mercy's last appeal before mercy's door closes (Psalm 96:10-13; Revelation 15:3-4).

## The Threefold Message of Revelation 14

### The First Angel: The Everlasting Gospel and the Judgment Hour

> _"And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people, saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to Him; for the hour of His judgment is come: and worship Him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters."_ (Revelation 14:6-7)

### The Three Angels Work in Succession

The first angel does not complete his work alone:

## Deeper Truths

### The Mighty Angel and the Little Book (Revelation 10)

John beholds a scene of extraordinary significance:

The critical detail is the **little book open**. This book was once sealed but is now opened.

> _"But thou, O Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end: many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased."_ (Daniel 12:4)

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary Connection

The first angel's message is inseparable from the sanctuary truth. The announcement "the hour of His judgment is come" points directly to Christ's ministry in the Most Holy Place of the heavenly sanctuary.

In the earthly sanctuary:

- **Outer court**: The sacrifice of the lamb (representing Calvaryâ€”justification)
- **Holy place**: Daily ministry of the priest (representing Christ's ongoing intercessionâ€”sanctification)
- **Most Holy Place**: The Day of Atonement, judgment, final cleansing (representing the investigative judgment and the blotting out of sinsâ€”glorification and sealing)

The first angel announces that we have entered the antitypical Day of Atonement. This is the most solemn period in earth's historyâ€”a time when every case is being decided for eternity.

The people gathered in solemn assembly, afflicting their souls and examining their hearts while the high priest ministered within the veil. No one knew when the high priest would emergeâ€”but when he did, the fate of every soul had been determined. So it is today. ### God Permits Disappointment to Test Faith

The study notes reveal a profound principle: God permitted His people to misunderstand the nature of the event to occur in 1844. They expected Christ to return; instead, He began His final work of judgment in the heavenly sanctuary.

This was not the first time God's people experienced such disappointment:

- The disciples expected Jesus to establish an earthly kingdom (Acts 1:6; Luke 24:19-21)
- Israel expected a short journey to Canaan but wandered forty years
- Elijah expected all Israel to repent after Carmel but fled in discouragement

[Q] **"Doesn't the 1844 disappointment prove the Adventist movement was in error?"**

No more than the disciples' disappointment proved Jesus was not the Messiah. The disciples fulfilled Zechariah 9:9 by their hosannas, even while misunderstanding Christ's mission. Their disappointment came not from error in the prophecy, but in their understanding of its fulfillment. Similarly, the Millerites correctly identified the time (the 2300 days ending in 1844) but misunderstood the event. The sanctuary was indeed "cleansed"â€”not the earth by fire, but the heavenly sanctuary through the commencement of the investigative judgment.

God tests faith through disappointment. Those who trust His word more than their understanding emerge stronger. Those who demand that God fulfill their expectations fall away.

### Righteousness by Faith and the Judgment Message

How can sinners face the judgment with confidence?

> _"For He hath made Him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in Him."_ (2 Corinthians 5:21)

The first angel's message includes the "everlasting gospel." This gospel is not merely pardon for past sins but the power of God to transform the believer into the image of Christ. Through faith:

- Christ's righteousness is **imputed** (counted as ours)â€”we stand justified before the judgment bar
- Christ's righteousness is **imparted** (worked into our character)â€”we are being sanctified, prepared to stand before a holy God
- Christ's life within enables victory over every known sin

The judgment holds no terror for those who are abiding in Christ. Their names are covered by His blood, their characters are being transformed by His grace, and they welcome His coming as their redemption.

## Practical Application

### What Does This Message Mean for Us Today?

1. **We live in the judgment hour.** Every day, eternal destinies are being decided. This should impress us with the solemnity of our time and the urgency of preparation.

2. **We are called to worship the Creator.** In an age of evolution, materialism, and false worship, the Sabbath stands as a sign of loyalty to the Creator God. Revelation 14:7 echoes the fourth commandment precisely because the final controversy centers on worship.

3. **We must proclaim this message.** The angel flies in the midst of heaven, visible to all. God's people are His messengers, commissioned to give this warning to "every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people."

4. **We need not fear the judgment.** Those who are in Christ have an Advocate with the Father (1 John 2:1). The judgment is good news for God's peopleâ€”it vindicates their faith, clears their names, and prepares the way for their eternal reward.

5. **We must live with eternal perspective.** The harvest is ripening. The three angels complete their work, and then the Son of man appears on the cloud. Is your life reflecting the urgency of this hour?
