# Speaking Notes: Not Justified by the Law

---

## Slide 1 – Introduction: Not Justified by the Law

We come today to one of the most important truths in all of Scripture—a truth that, if misunderstood, leads either to despair or to presumption. If we get this wrong on one side, we will try to earn our way to heaven and fail. If we get it wrong on the other side, we will cheapen grace into a license for sin.

The truth is simply this: **The law reveals sin but cannot remove it—only Christ's grace justifies, yet grace never licenses disobedience.**

- Read Romans 3:20 – "Therefore by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in His sight: for by the law is the knowledge of sin."

This verse contains both the problem and the purpose. The law cannot justify—but the law does something essential: it gives us the knowledge of sin.

So let us understand first why the law cannot save us, and then discover what can.

---

## Slide 2 – The Law Cannot Make Anyone Righteous

Notice the language: "no flesh." This is universal. It doesn't say "most people" or "especially wicked people"—it says _no flesh_. This applies to the moral person, the religious person, the Jew under the covenant, the Gentile trying to live by conscience. No one is justified by law-keeping.

Why not? Because the law's purpose is **diagnostic, not therapeutic**.

- Think of it this way: the law diagnoses the disease; it does not cure it.
- It points to sin; it cannot remove sin.

The word "justified" here means _declared righteous_—like a verdict in a courtroom. The law is the standard by which the court operates, but the law is not the judge who acquits. The law can only show what righteousness looks like; it cannot produce righteousness in a sinful heart.

This raises an important question—if the law can't justify, why do we have it?

---

## Slide 3 – Why Have the Law If It Cannot Justify?

Here is the answer: **Without diagnosis, no one seeks the Physician.**

If you don't know you're sick, you won't go to the doctor. The law shows us our desperate need. Christ meets that need.

- Read Galatians 3:24 – "Wherefore the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith."

The law is the schoolmaster, the tutor, the one who leads us by the hand and says, "You cannot do this on your own. Come to Christ." The law drives us to the Savior.

So the law has a vital purpose—it reveals sin.

---

## Slide 4 – The Law Reveals Sin

Paul gives his own testimony:

- Read Romans 7:7 – "I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet."

Before Paul understood the law, coveting seemed harmless—just a private desire, hurting no one. But then the tenth commandment came: "Thou shalt not covet." And suddenly Paul saw that the law reaches beyond outward actions to inward motives. The law is spiritual, searching the heart.

This is what makes God's law different from human laws. Human law punishes the thief; God's law exposes the covetous heart that wanted to steal before the hand ever moved. The law shows us sin where we thought there was none.

And this is exactly what we need—an honest diagnosis.

---

## Slide 5 – Illustration: The Mirror and the Fountain

Think of a mirror. A mirror shows the dirt on your face—but it cannot wash it off. You look in the mirror, see the smudge, and then you go to the water.

The law is the mirror. It shows us our sin with perfect clarity. But we cannot wash in the mirror. We must go to the fountain.

- Reference Zechariah 13:1 – "In that day there shall be a fountain opened to the house of David and to the inhabitants of Jerusalem for sin and for uncleanness."

That fountain is Christ. His blood cleanses what the mirror only reveals.

So the law is not our enemy—it is the honest friend who tells us the truth so we will seek the One who can help.

And this diagnosis applies to everyone.

---

## Slide 6 – All the World Guilty Before God

- Read Romans 3:19 – "Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God."

Notice: "every mouth may be stopped." No defense. No excuse. No argument.

The Jew had the written law on tablets of stone. The Gentile had the law written on the conscience (Romans 2:14–15). Both had enough light to condemn them. Both fell short. Both need grace.

This is not about one group being worse than another. This is about the entire human race standing silent before a holy God, with nothing to say in their own defense.

And this is the point—if any human being could be saved by law-keeping, it would mean something devastating about Calvary.

---

## Slide 7 – If the Law Could Justify, Christ Died in Vain

- Read Galatians 2:21 – "I do not frustrate the grace of God: for if righteousness come by the law, then Christ is dead in vain."

Think about that. If you could earn your way to heaven by keeping the commandments, then Jesus did not need to die. His death was unnecessary. His agony in Gethsemane was wasted. The cross was a tragic mistake.

But we know better. We know the cross was no accident. The very fact that Christ had to die tells us that no amount of law-keeping could save us. The debt was too great. The disease was too deep. Only the blood of God's own Son could pay the price.

But someone might say, "If the law can't save us, then the law must be the problem. The law must be bad." Is that true?

---

## Slide 8 – The Law Is Holy, Just, and Good

- Read Romans 7:12 – "Wherefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good."

The law is not the problem. The law reflects God's own character—His holiness, His justice, His goodness. The law tells us what love looks like in action. It shows us how a sinless being would live.

- **Holy** — it comes from a holy God.
- **Just** — it is a fair standard, the same for all.
- **Good** — it was designed for human flourishing.

The law is not flawed. The law is perfect. The problem lies elsewhere.

---

## Slide 9 – The Problem Is Our Sinful Nature

- Read Romans 8:7 – "Because the carnal mind is enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be."

Here is the problem: the carnal mind, the unregenerate heart, is at war with God's law. It is not merely _unwilling_ to keep the law—it is _unable_ to keep it. "Neither indeed can be."

Paul defends the law: "Is the law sin? God forbid" (Romans 7:7). The law didn't create the problem; the law exposed the problem. The disease is indwelling sin, not the diagnostic tool that revealed it.

So how can sinful man ever be made right with a holy God?

---

## Slide 10 – Justification: Freely by His Grace

Here is the answer:

- Read Romans 3:24 – "Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus."

Three words unlock this verse:

- **Freely** — the Greek word means "without cause," "as a gift." There is nothing in us that caused God to justify us.
- **Grace** — unmerited favor. We did not earn it. We cannot earn it.
- **Redemption** — a ransom paid, a release from bondage. Someone paid the price to set us free.

This is the gospel: justification is a gift, flowing from grace, made possible by Christ's redemption.

---

## Slide 11 – The Source, Ground, and Means of Justification

Let's be clear about how this works:

- The **source** is God's grace—it originates in His heart of love.
- The **ground** is Christ's redemption—His blood paid the price.
- The **means** is faith—we receive it by trusting in Christ.

Now someone might ask: if justification is free, what did it cost?

- Read 1 Peter 1:18–19 – "Forasmuch as ye know that ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold... but with the precious blood of Christ."

Free to us. Infinite cost to heaven. The gift cost the Giver everything.

---

## Slide 12 – Illustration: The Bankrupt Debtor

Imagine a bankrupt debtor who owes millions but cannot pay a single cent. He stands before the court with empty hands and no defense.

Then a wealthy friend steps forward. He pays the entire debt—every cent. He hands the debtor a receipt that says, "Paid in full."

The debtor walks out free. Not because of his works. Not because he made payments. But because another paid on his behalf. That is justification by grace.

- Reference Romans 6:23 – "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."

The wages are what we earned—death. The gift is what we could never earn—eternal life.

But now a question arises. And Paul knew it would arise.

---

## Slide 13 – Grace Does Not License Sin

If we are saved by grace and not by works, then why not just keep sinning? If the debt is paid, why not run up more debt?

Paul anticipates this:

- Read Romans 6:15 – "What then? shall we sin, because we are not under the law, but under grace? God forbid."

"God forbid"—literally, "May it never be!" This is Paul's strongest rejection. The very thought is abhorrent.

And then he explains why with a principle about slavery.

---

## Slide 14 – Two Masters, Two Outcomes

- Read Romans 6:16 – "Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey; whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness?"

You have a choice of masters, and each master leads to a different destination:

- Serve sin → death
- Serve righteousness → life

Grace does not remove the choice; grace empowers a new choice. The person who has been set free from sin's penalty does not run back to sin's slavery. Grace produces obedience—not excuses from it.

---

## Slide 15 – Grace Teaches Us to Live Godly

- Read Titus 2:11–12 – "For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men, teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world."

Notice: grace _teaches_ us. Grace is not passive. Grace is not permission to sin. Grace is a teacher with a curriculum: deny ungodliness, deny worldly lusts, live soberly, righteously, and godly.

Grace that doesn't transform is grace misunderstood. If someone claims to be saved by grace but lives unchanged, they have not understood what grace is. True grace changes lives.

And this brings us to one of the most precious promises in all of Scripture.

---

## Slide 16 – No Condemnation in Christ Jesus

- Read Romans 8:1 – "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit."

"No condemnation." What words could be sweeter to a guilty sinner? The verdict is in, and for those in Christ, it is acquittal.

But notice the qualifiers:

- **"In Christ Jesus"** — this is union with Him, not just mental agreement. It is abiding in the Vine.
- **"Walk not after the flesh"** — the daily direction of life matters. This is not a single decision but a way of living.
- **"But after the Spirit"** — the Holy Spirit empowers this new walk.

---

## Slide 17 – The Righteousness of the Law Fulfilled in Us

- Read Romans 8:4 – "That the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit."

Here is something remarkable: the very righteousness the law demands is _fulfilled in us_—not by our striving, but by the Spirit's work.

The word "walk" here means lifestyle, conduct—the ongoing direction of your life. And this walk is not sinless perfection achieved by human effort. It is Christ's life lived out in the believer.

---

## Slide 18 – Victory Through Abiding, Not Striving

- Reference Galatians 2:20 – "I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me: and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God, who loved me, and gave himself for me."

"Not I, but Christ." This is the secret. Victory comes not by trying harder but by trusting deeper. Christ lives His life through us as we surrender to Him.

- Reference John 15:4–5 – "Abide in me, and I in you... without me ye can do nothing."

The branch does not strain to produce fruit. The branch abides in the vine, and fruit comes naturally. So it is with the Christian life. We abide. He produces. This is victory through abiding, not striving.

So how do law and grace fit together?

---

## Slide 19 – The Harmony of Law and Grace

They are not enemies. They are partners in God's plan of salvation:

- **Law diagnoses** — grace heals.
- **Law condemns** — grace justifies.
- **Law demands righteousness** — grace provides righteousness.
- **Law reveals God's standard** — grace enables meeting that standard.

Both come from the same God. Both serve His purpose. The law shows us our need; grace meets that need. The law reveals what we cannot do; grace does in us what we cannot do ourselves.

---

## Slide 20 – Faith Establishes the Law

- Read Romans 3:31 – "Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law."

Faith does not destroy the law. Faith establishes it. How? Because faith brings us to Christ, and Christ writes His law on our hearts (Hebrews 8:10). Grace does not lead us away from obedience—grace leads us into obedience, empowered from within.

The person who truly understands grace will love God's law, because the law reveals the character of the One who saved them.

---

## Slide 21 – Appeal: Receive Justification as a Gift

Let me ask you today: Where do you stand?

The law has done its work. It has shown us all guilty—every mouth stopped, no defense, no excuse. We cannot save ourselves. No amount of trying will pay the debt.

But there is a way forward—only one way: **the blood of Christ, freely offered**.

- If you have been trying to earn salvation, stop. Receive justification as a gift.
- If you have been abusing grace as an excuse for sin, stop. Let grace teach you to live godly.

Walk in the Spirit today. Let grace transform you, not just excuse you.

---

## Slide 22 – Closing Texts

I leave you with these two verses—memorize them, claim them, live in their truth:

- **Romans 3:24** – "Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus."

This is how you are saved—freely, by grace, through Christ.

- **Romans 8:1** – "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit."

This is how you live—in Christ, walking by the Spirit, free from condemnation.

May God write these truths on your heart today.
