## Deeper Truths

### The Principle of Full Restitution

Scripture establishes a comprehensive system of responsibility for losses caused to others:

**Through theft or negligence of entrusted goods:**

> _"If the thief be not found, then the master of the house shall be brought unto the judges, to see whether he have put his hand unto his neighbor's goods."_ (Exodus 22:8)

**Through personal carelessness:**

> _"If fire break out, and catch in thorns, so that the stacks of corn, or the standing corn, or the field be consumed therewith; he that kindled the fire shall surely make restitution."_ (Exodus 22:6)

**Through intentional harm:**

> _"If a man shall cause a field or vineyard to be eaten, and shall put in his beast, and shall feed in another man's field; of the best of his own field, and of the best of his own vineyard, shall he make restitution."_ (Exodus 22:5)

Notice the graduated principle: for intentional harm, restitution comes from **"the best"** of one's own goods. The more deliberate the wrong, the more complete the restoration required.

This is not punishment for punishment's sake, but training in responsibility. So our heavenly Father trains His children: when we cause loss to others, love requires that we make it right, even at personal cost.

### Borrowed Goods and Personal Responsibility

> _"And if a man borrow aught of his neighbor, and it be hurt, or die, the owner thereof being not with it, he shall surely make it good."_ (Exodus 22:14)

**Borrowing creates obligation.** The borrower assumes responsibility for the condition of the borrowed item. If damaged while in our careâ€”regardless of faultâ€”we bear the loss. This principle cuts against the modern tendency to borrow carelessly or return things in worse condition than received.

### The Sin of Usury

> _"Thou shalt not lend upon usury to thy brother, usury of money, usury of victuals, usury of anything that is lent upon usury."_ (Deuteronomy 23:19)

**Usury**: Exorbitant or oppressive interest charged on loans, especially to the poor or desperate.

God's law distinguished between legitimate business lending and predatory practices that exploit the needy. The Scriptures consistently condemn those who profit from others' desperation (Psalm 15:5; Ezekiel 18:13).

## Practical Application

### Examining Our Financial Lives

This study calls us to honest self-examination:

1. **Do I pay promptly?** When services are rendered, do I delay payment unnecessarily? Do employees or contractors wait for what is owed them?

2. **Do I borrow responsibly?** When I borrow, do I treat others' property with greater care than my own? Do I return items in the same or better condition?
