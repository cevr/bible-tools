## Deeper Truths

### Seven Manifestations of Selfishness

Scripture reveals how selfishness shows itself in human conduct. Recognizing these manifestations helps us identify the enemy within:

**1. Lovers of Self**

> _"For men shall be lovers of their own selves, covetous."_ (2 Timothy 3:2)

Self-love is the first and most fundamental expression of selfishness. It places "I" at the center of the universe where only God belongs.

**2. Pleasing Ourselves**

> _"We then that are strong ought to bear the infirmities of the weak, and not to please ourselves."_ (Romans 15:1)

The selfish heart constantly asks, "What do I want? What makes me comfortable? What satisfies my preferences?"

**3. Seeking Our Own**

> _"Even as I please all men in all things, not seeking mine own profit, but the profit of many, that they may be saved."_ (1 Corinthians 10:33)

This is selfishness in actionâ€”the relentless pursuit of personal advantage regardless of others.

**4. Greedy Gain**

> _"Yea, they are greedy dogs which can never have enough... they all look to their own way, every one for his gain, from his quarter."_ (Isaiah 56:11)

[ILL] Consider a child with a plate of cookies who, when asked to share, hides them behind his back. Though he cannot possibly eat them all, he cannot bear for another to have what he considers "his." So is the covetous heartâ€”never satisfied, always grasping, incapable of generous joy.

**5. Seeking Undue Precedence**

> _"Grant that these my two sons may sit, the one on thy right hand, and the other on the left, in thy kingdom."_ (Matthew 20:21)

**6. Neglect of the Poor**

> _"But whoso hath this world's good, and seeth his brother have need, and shutteth up his bowels of compassion from him, how dwelleth the love of God in him?"_ (1 John 3:17)

Selfishness blinds us to the needs of others. It builds walls around our abundance while brothers and sisters suffer want.

**7. Performing Duty for Reward**

> _"The heads thereof judge for reward, and the priests thereof teach for hire, and the prophets thereof divine for money."_ (Micah 3:11)

Even religious service can be poisoned by selfishness when we serve God for what we can getâ€”reputation, income, influence, or heavenly rewardâ€”rather than from love.

[Q] **"But didn't Jesus promise rewards to His followers? Isn't it right to be motivated by heaven?"**

There is a difference between _receiving_ reward and _serving for_ reward. A child who obeys his father out of love may receive an inheritance, but he does not obey _in order to_ receive it. The selfish heart calculates: "What will I get?" The loving heart asks: "How can I please my Father?" Jesus promises rewards to those whose hearts are right, but the reward-seeking motive itself reveals a heart still governed by self.

### Selfishness Destroys Christian Fellowship

> _"There should be no schism in the body; but that the members should have the same care one for another. And whether one member suffer, all the members suffer with it; or one member be honoured, all the members rejoice with it."_ (1 Corinthians 12:25, 26)

### Selfishness Is Contrary to Love

> _"Charity envieth not; charity vaunteth not itself, is not puffed up, doth not behave itself unseemly, seeketh not her own, is not easily provoked, thinketh no evil."_ (1 Corinthians 13:4, 5)

Notice how many descriptions of love are simply the _absence_ of selfish traits:

- **Envieth not** (does not covet what others have)
- **Vaunteth not itself** (does not boast of self)
- **Is not puffed up** (does not have an inflated self-view)
- **Seeketh not her own** (does not pursue self-interest)

Love and selfishness are mutually exclusive. Where one reigns, the other cannot exist.

## Practical Application

### The Divine Prescription

> _"Let no man seek his own."_ (1 Corinthians 10:24)

> _"Look not every man on his own things, but every man also on the things of others."_ (Philippians 2:4)

These are not merely good suggestionsâ€”they are the pattern of the kingdom of heaven. How do we apply them practically?

**1. Daily Surrender**
Each morning, before engaging with the world, bring your self-life to the cross. Pray, "Lord, I cannot conquer selfishness. Take my heart, for I cannot give it. Keep it pure, for I cannot keep it for Thee. Mold me, fashion me, raise me into a pure and holy atmosphere, where the rich current of Thy love can flow through my soul."

**2. Look for Opportunities to Serve**
Selfishness is overcome not merely by _not doing_ selfish things, but by actively doing loving things. Ask each day: "Whom can I serve? What need can I meet? How can I bear another's burden?"

**3. Rejoice in Others' Blessings**
One of selfishness's most subtle manifestations is envyâ€”resenting others' success, gifts, or blessings. Practice rejoicing when others are honored, promoted, or blessed. This crucifies self.

**4. Give Generously**

> _"Give, and it shall be given unto you."_ (Luke 6:38)

Generosity breaks the grip of covetousness. Give of your time, your resources, your attention. Give when it costs you something. Give to those who cannot repay.

**5. Examine Your Motives**
Regularly ask yourself: "Why am I doing this? Is it for God's glory, for others' good, or for my own advancement?" Honest self-examination, in the light of God's word and Spirit, exposes hidden selfishness.

### The Cost of Discipleship

> _"If any man will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross, and follow me."_ (Matthew 16:24)
