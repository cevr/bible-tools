## Core Truths

### What Is Backsliding?

Backsliding is more than occasional failure; it is a spiritual regressionâ€”a turning away from the living God to whom we once drew near. The prophet Jeremiah describes its bitter consequences:

**Backsliding** is defined as: A gradual or sudden departure from close fellowship with God, marked by loss of reverent fear (awe and love), disobedience, and spiritual coldness (Hosea 11:7; Revelation 2:4).

Notice three elements in this passage:

- **Self-inflicted correction**: The backslider's own sin becomes their teacher through painful consequences
- **Bitterness of fruit**: What seemed sweet in departing from God turns bitter
- **Root cause identified**: Forsaking the Lord and losing the fear (reverent awe) of God

### The Universal Tendency

Scripture reveals a sobering truth about human nature, even among God's professed people:

> _"And My people are bent to backsliding from Me."_ (Hosea 11:7)

The word "bent" suggests an inclination, a leaning, a gravitational pull. Like water flowing downhill, our fallen nature naturally drifts away from God unless actively resisted. This is why Paul warned, "Wherefore let him that thinketh he standeth take heed lest he fall" (1 Corinthians 10:12).

## Deeper Truths

### How God Regards Backsliding

The Lord does not view backsliding with cold indifference. His heart is that of a wounded spouse:

> _"Surely as a wife treacherously departeth from her husband, so have ye dealt treacherously with Me, O house of Israel, saith the Lord."_ (Jeremiah 3:20)

This imagery reveals:

- **Covenant relationship**: God sees His people as bound to Him in sacred covenant, as intimate as marriage
- **Personal grief**: Backsliding wounds the heart of God
- **Treachery, not mere weakness**: The departure is a betrayal of love and trust

[Q] **"Isn't this picture of God as a wounded husband too emotional? Is God really affected by our choices?"**

Scripture consistently portrays God as having genuine feelings toward His creatures. "In all their affliction He was afflicted" (Isaiah 63:9). Christ wept over Jerusalem (Luke 19:41). The Holy Spirit can be grieved (Ephesians 4:30). While God is not subject to the instability of human emotions, He genuinely loves us and is moved by our choices. This is not weakness but the depth of infinite love.

### A Specific Area of Backsliding: Robbing God

When Israel asked, "Wherein shall we return?" God pointed to a specific area of departure:

> _"Will a man rob God? Yet ye have robbed Me. But ye say, Wherein have we robbed Thee? In tithes and offerings."_ (Malachi 3:7â€“8)

This reveals that backsliding can manifest in practical, measurable waysâ€”particularly in our stewardship. The tithe is holy unto the Lord (Leviticus 27:30), and withholding it is identified as robbery. But notice God's gracious remedy:

> _"Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse... and prove Me now herewith, saith the Lord of hosts, if I will not open you the windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it."_ (Malachi 3:10)

### Why Some Disciples Left Jesus

Even during Christ's earthly ministry, some who followed Him turned back:

> _"From that time many of His disciples went back, and walked no more with Him."_ (John 6:66)

The context reveals why: Jesus had just taught the difficult doctrine that He was the bread from heaven and that eating His flesh and drinking His blood (receiving Him fully by faith) was essential for eternal life (John 6:53â€“58). The teaching was too demanding, too humbling, too contrary to their expectations.

[Q] **"Does this mean once someone backslides, they were never truly saved?"**

Not necessarily. Scripture shows genuine believers can drift away (Hebrews 2:1), fall from grace (Galatians 5:4), and need restoration (Galatians 6:1). The key question is not whether one ever stumbled but whether one returns. Peter denied Christ but was restored. Judas betrayed Christ and refused restoration. The difference was in their response to the Spirit's conviction.

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Law in the Heartâ€”The Safeguard Against Sliding

> _"The law of his God is in his heart; none of his steps shall slide."_ (Psalm 37:31)

This verse reveals the divine antidote to backsliding: not merely knowing the law externally but having it written in the heart through the new covenant (Hebrews 8:10). When God's law becomes our delight rather than our burden, when obedience flows from love rather than fear of punishment, our steps become stable.

This is righteousness by faith at its practical core. The same Christ who justifies us by His blood sanctifies us by His indwelling Spirit. His righteousness is not merely **imputed** (credited to our account) but **imparted** (worked into our character). This internal transformation is our only security against backsliding.

## Practical Application

### The Path of Return

For the backslider, God outlines a clear path home:

**Praying** involves:

- Daily, earnest communion with God
- Specific prayer against known weaknesses
- Dependence on divine strength rather than human resolve
