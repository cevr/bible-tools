# Reading 177: Subjects of the Kingdom

## Introduction

## Core Truths

### The Call to Overcome

God's plan for His children is not merely forgiveness but complete victory over sin. Paul's appeal is direct and urgent:

> _"Be not overcome of evil, but overcome evil with good."_ (Romans 12:21)

The apostle John identifies precisely what we must conquer:

> _"For all that is in the world, the lust of the flesh, and the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life, is not of the Father, but is of the world."_ (1 John 2:16)

These three categoriesâ€”**the lust of the flesh** (bodily appetites), **the lust of the eyes** (covetousness), and **the pride of life** (self-exaltation)â€”encompass every temptation humanity faces. ## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Character of God's People

Before conversion, the Gentiles were in a pitiable condition:

> _"At that time ye were without Christ, being aliens from the commonwealth of Israel, and strangers from the covenants of promise, having no hope, and without God in the world."_ (Ephesians 2:12)

But through Christ, _"ye who sometimes were far off are made nigh by the blood of Christ"_ (Ephesians 2:13). The dividing wall is broken down; Jew and Gentile become one new man in Christ (Ephesians 2:14â€“15).

God's remnant people in the last days are described with unmistakable clarity:

> _"Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus."_ (Revelation 14:12)

Two marks identify the true Israel of the end time:

### Sanctuary Connection

The promises to overcomers are deeply rooted in sanctuary imagery:

- **White raiment** (Revelation 3:5) recalls the priestly garmentsâ€”Christ's righteousness covering the believer
- **Pillar in God's temple** (Revelation 3:12) speaks of permanent standing in the heavenly sanctuary

The overcomer's experience is one of entering by faith into the heavenly sanctuary, standing in Christ's righteousness before the Father, and receiving the verdict of acquittal in the investigative judgment. Those whose names remain in the book of life (Revelation 3:5) are those who have overcome by the blood of the Lamb and the word of their testimony (Revelation 12:11).

## Practical Application

### What Does Overcoming Look Like Today?

1. **Daily surrender**: Each morning, commit your will to Christ. _"I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me"_ (Galatians 2:20).

2. **Feed on the Word**: The "hidden manna" is spiritual nourishment from Scripture. _"Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God"_ (Matthew 4:4).

3. **Maintain living faith**: Faith is not a one-time decision but a daily dependence. Pray for faith. _"Lord, I believe; help thou mine unbelief"_ (Mark 9:24).

4. **Resist specific temptations**: Identify your particular battlefieldâ€”is it lust of the flesh, lust of the eyes, or pride of life? Bring these to Christ specifically, claiming His victory.

5. **Keep the commandments by faith**: God's law reveals His character and our duty. We keep it not to earn salvation but because Christ keeps it in us. _"I delight to do thy will, O my God: yea, thy law is within my heart"_ (Psalm 40:8).

6. **Hold fast your confession**: _"Let us hold fast the profession of our faith without wavering; for he is faithful that promised"_ (Hebrews 10:23).

### Marks of True Israelites

Ask yourself:

- Am I trusting in religious heritage, church membership, or any human merit? Or am I resting wholly on Christ's righteousness?
- Am I walking in the faith of Abrahamâ€”obedient, trusting, forward-looking?
- Am I keeping God's commandments, not as a means of salvation, but as the fruit of a living connection with Christ?
