# Reading 111: Departing and Being with Christ

### What Was the "Gain" in Dying?

Many assume Paul meant immediate conscious bliss in heaven. But notice the
context: Paul explains his struggle by saying Christ would be _magnified_

[Q] **"But doesn't 'to depart and be with Christ' prove immediate presence with

This is a fair question, and we must let Paul interpret Paul. Notice that
Philippians 1:23 does not specify _when_ or _how_ the departed believer would be
with Christ. Paul addresses this explicitly elsewhere.

## Deeper Truths

### Paul's Own Teaching on When Believers Meet Christ

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### Satan's Ancient Lie

The doctrine of immediate consciousness after death traces back to Eden:

> _"And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die."_ (Genesis
> 3:4)

God said, "Thou shalt surely die" (Genesis 2:17). Satan contradicted, "Ye shall
not surely die." This lieâ€”that the soul is inherently immortal and continues
conscious after deathâ€”opened the door to spiritualism, purgatory, prayers to
saints, and communication with "the dead."

The biblical truth that the dead sleep until the resurrection protects believers
from these deceptions.

### The Sanctuary Connection

In the earthly sanctuary, the high priest entered the Most Holy Place on the Day
of Atonementâ€”not to bring the dead into God's presence, but to cleanse the
record of sins for the living and sleeping saints alike. Christ's ministry in
the heavenly sanctuary prepares a people to meet Him at His coming:

> _"So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many; and unto them that look
> for him shall he appear the second time without sin unto salvation."_ (Hebrews
> 9:28)

The reward is given at Christ's appearing, not at death. This preserves the
integrity of judgmentâ€”no one receives their final destiny before the verdict is
rendered (Revelation 22:12; 2 Corinthians 5:10).

## Practical Application

This truth calls us to:

1. **Hope in the resurrection**, not in death itself. Our comfort is not that
   loved ones are "looking down" but that they rest securely until Jesus calls
   them forth (John 5:28â€“29).

2. **Live urgently for Christ**. Like Paul, we should desire both to serve
   faithfully now and to see Christ at His coming. Neither death nor life should
   be wasted.

3. **Guard against spiritualistic deception**. If the dead are unconscious, any
   "spirit" claiming to be a departed loved one is a demonic deception. "Beloved,
   believe not every spirit, but try the spirits" (1 John 4:1).

4. **Comfort the grieving with truth**. We "sorrow not, even as others which
   have no hope" (1 Thessalonians 4:13). Our loved ones in Christ are safe in
   His keeping, awaiting the glad reunion.
