## Practical Application

### How to Test a Shepherd

Every believer has both the right and responsibility to examine religious teaching:

1. **Does he exalt Christ as the only way of salvation?** (John 14:6; Acts 4:12)

2. **Does he feed the flock with Scripture, not human philosophy?** (2 Timothy 4:2; Isaiah 8:20)

3. **Does he uphold God's law as the standard of Christian living?** (Malachi 2:7; Matthew 5:17â€“19)

4. **Does he warn of coming judgment and the nearness of Christ's return?** (Ezekiel 33:7; Acts 20:26â€“27)

5. **Does he open the prophetic word rather than declaring it "sealed"?** (Revelation 1:3; Daniel 12:4, 10)

6. **Does his life exemplify the truth he preaches?** (Titus 2:7; 1 Timothy 4:12)

> _"To the law and to the testimony: if they speak not according to this word, it is because there is no light in them."_ (Isaiah 8:20)

This is the infallible test. No eloquence, no popularity, no miracle can substitute for agreement with God's Word.

### What If You Discover a Shepherd Is False?

- **Do not follow error out of loyalty or tradition.** Jesus said, "Let them alone: they be blind leaders of the blind. And if the blind lead the blind, both shall fall into the ditch" (Matthew 15:14).

- **Seek out those who faithfully teach God's Word.** There are still shepherds who have not bowed the knee to Baal.

- **Study Scripture for yourself.** The Bereans were "more noble" because they searched the Scriptures daily to prove what they heard (Acts 17:11).

- **Pray for discernment.** "If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God" (James 1:5).
