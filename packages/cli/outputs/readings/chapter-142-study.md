## Core Truths

### All That Is Devoted to God Is Sacred

The foundation of reverence begins with understanding that everything set apart for God's service takes on a holy character:

> _"Every devoted thing is most holy unto the Lord."_ (Leviticus 27:28)

**Devoted** (Hebrew: _cherem_): Something irrevocably given to God, separated from common use, and belonging entirely to Him.

This principle extends beyond physical objects to places, times, and even our own bodies when consecrated to His service. What we dedicate to God is no longer ours to treat carelessly.

### Christ's Example of Holy Jealousy

Jesus demonstrated the proper attitude toward His Father's house in the cleansing of the temple:

> _"And He taught, saying unto them, Is it not written, My house shall be called of all nations the house of prayer? but ye have made it a den of thieves."_ (Mark 11:17)

The gentle Saviour who blessed little children and forgave sinners was moved to righteous action when God's house was desecrated. This was not anger out of controlâ€”it was holy jealousy for His Father's honor.

Notice what Christ did:

- **Cast out** those buying and selling
- **Overthrew** the tables of money changers

Jesus acted to restore what worship was meant to be: communion between heaven and earth.

## Deeper Truths

### A House of Prayer for All Nations

Some might argue that Christ's words applied only to the Jewish temple, now destroyed. But the prophecy He quoted extends far beyond ancient Jerusalem:

> _"Even them will I bring to My holy mountain, and make them joyful in My house of prayer: their burnt offerings and their sacrifices shall be accepted upon Mine altar; for Mine house shall be called a house of prayer for all people."_ (Isaiah 56:7)

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary Connection

This teaching connects directly with the sanctuary message. God's presence dwelt in the earthly tabernacle and temple, and His glory filled the Most Holy Place. The same God who required reverence in the earthly sanctuary now ministers in the heavenly sanctuary:

- **Outer court** (sacrifice): Christ's blood was shed to make reconciliation possible
- **Holy place** (daily ministry): Christ intercedes for His people continually
- **Most Holy Place** (judgment): Christ now ministers in the final phase of atonement

If reverence was required in the earthly type, how much more in our worship that connects with the heavenly reality? When we gather for worship, we join the angels in the courts above. The writer of Hebrews reminds us:

> _"Wherefore we receiving a kingdom which cannot be moved, let us have grace, whereby we may serve God acceptably with reverence and godly fear."_ (Hebrews 12:28)

### Why Irreverence Matters in the Great Controversy

In the cosmic conflict between Christ and Satan, one central issue is whether God's law and character deserve honor. Satan has consistently sought to diminish reverence for God and all that pertains to Him.

Notice that God linked Sabbath observance directly with sanctuary reverence:

> _"Ye shall keep My sabbaths, and reverence My sanctuary: I am the Lord."_ (Leviticus 19:30)

Both the Sabbath and the sanctuary point to God as Creator and Redeemer. Both call us to step out of the common and into the sacred. Irreverence toward either reflects a diminished view of God Himself.

[Q] **"But doesn't God care more about the heart than outward behavior?"**

He cares about bothâ€”because outward behavior reveals and shapes the heart. The Preacher counsels:

> _"Keep thy foot when thou goest to the house of God."_ (Ecclesiastes 5:1)

This expression means to walk carefully, deliberately, with conscious attention. True heart worship expresses itself in reverent conduct. And careless conduct, over time, erodes heart reverence. The two cannot be separated.

### The Danger of Polluting God's House

God warned through Jeremiah of the consequences when His people brought "abominations" into His house:

> _"For the children of Judah have done evil in My sight, saith the Lord: they have set their abominations in the house which is called by My name, to pollute it."_ (Jeremiah 7:30)

Israel's casual attitude toward the sanctuary reflected their casual attitude toward God Himselfâ€”and eventually led to their rejection as His chosen people.

[ILL] A pure spring of water can be polluted graduallyâ€”a little sediment here, some debris thereâ€”until what was once crystal clear becomes unfit to drink. So irreverence creeps into God's house little by little: casual conversation that could wait, business conducted during sacred hours, entertainment replacing worship, until the very atmosphere that should draw souls to heaven becomes indistinguishable from the world.

## Practical Application

### How to Cultivate Reverence

David provides our model:

> _"But as for me, I will come into Thy house in the multitude of Thy mercy: and in Thy fear will I worship toward Thy holy temple."_ (Psalm 5:7)

Notice David's approach:

1. **Personal resolve**: "As for me"â€”regardless of what others do
2. **Humble dependence**: "In the multitude of Thy mercy"â€”not presuming on his own worthiness
3. **Holy fear**: "In Thy fear"â€”not terror, but profound respect for who God is

The psalmist declares:

> _"God is greatly to be feared in the assembly of the saints, and to be had in reverence of all them that are about Him."_ (Psalm 89:7)

### Specific Applications for Today

**Before worship:**

- Prepare your heart through prayer and Scripture
- Arrive early enough to quiet your mind
- Remember that Christ has promised to meet with you

**During worship:**

- Give full attention to God, not to phones or distractions
- Participate actively in praise, prayer, and study
- Maintain a spirit of expectationâ€”God desires to speak to you

**Regarding God's house:**

- Reserve business discussions for appropriate times and places
- Guard against casual conversation that belongs outside
- On the Sabbath especially, let the house of God be devoted to its sacred purpose
