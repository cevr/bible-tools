# Bible Study: The Judgment

## Introduction

The judgment of God is one of the most solemn and yet most comforting truths in all of Scripture. Far from being an arbitrary or capricious event, the judgment reveals God's perfect justice, His transparent government, and His earnest desire that all should be saved. Through the judgment, God vindicates His character before the universe, demonstrates the fairness of every decision, and prepares a people to dwell eternally in His presence. This study traces the judgment through its various phasesâ€”investigative, millennial, and executiveâ€”showing how each phase fulfills God's eternal purpose of restoring righteousness to His creation.

## Deeper Truths

### The Purpose of the Pre-Advent Judgment

Why must there be an investigative judgment before Christ returns? Because at His coming, there is no time for deliberationâ€”everything happens instantaneously.

> "The dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed, in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump" (1 Corinthians 15:52).

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary and the Judgment

The investigative judgment is the antitype of the Day of Atonement (Leviticus 16). In the earthly sanctuary:

- **Daily ministry** (holy place): Sins were confessed and symbolically transferred to the sanctuary through the blood of the sacrifice.
- **Yearly cleansing** (most holy place): On the Day of Atonement, the high priest entered the most holy place to cleanse the sanctuary from the accumulated sins.

In the heavenly sanctuary:

- **Christ's daily ministry**: Since His ascension, Christ has applied His blood on behalf of repentant sinners, forgiving their sins.
- **Christ's final atonement**: Beginning in 1844 (see Daniel 8:14), Christ entered upon the final phase of His ministry in the most holy placeâ€”the investigative judgmentâ€”to blot out the sins of the righteous and prepare a people for His coming.

### Righteousness by Faith and the Judgment

Some fear that an investigative judgment teaches salvation by works. But the judgment actually demonstrates the power of righteousness by faith.

The judgment examines whether we have genuinely accepted Christâ€”whether His righteousness has been **imputed** to cover our guilt and **imparted** to transform our character. The evidence sought is not perfect human performance but genuine faith that works by love.

> "But wilt thou know, O vain man, that faith without works is dead?" (James 2:20).

Living faith produces fruit. The judgment reveals whether our profession was genuine or merely nominal. Those found in Christâ€”those who by faith have allowed Him to live out His life in themâ€”are accepted. Their sins are blotted out, and their names are retained in the book of life.

Since the dead are to be judged out of the things written in the books, it is impossible that the sins of men should be blotted out until after the judgment at which their cases are to be investigated" (_The Great Controversy_, p. 485).

### The Announcement of the Judgment

The investigative judgment's commencement is announced to the world through the first angel's message:

> "Fear God, and give glory to Him; for the hour of His judgment is come: and worship Him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters" (Revelation 14:7).

This message has been proclaimed since 1844, calling people to:

The judgment message is not designed to frighten but to prepare. God wants a people ready to meet Him, cleansed from sin, clothed in Christ's righteousness, reflecting His character.

## Practical Application

> "For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ; that every one may receive the things done in his body, according to that he hath done, whether it be good or bad" (2 Corinthians 5:10).
