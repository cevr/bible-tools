# Bible Study: The Two Resurrections

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary and Resurrection Hope

The sanctuary service illuminates the resurrection truth. On the Day of

> _"So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many; and unto them that look

> 9:28)

The righteous dead wait in their graves as ancient Israel waited for the high
priest to emerge. When Christ completes His mediatorial work in heaven's Most

### Victory Over Death Through Christ

The resurrection is possible only because Christ conquered death:

> _"I am he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore,

Christ holds the keys! Death has no independent power. The grave cannot hold
those for whom Christ died and rose again. His resurrection is the guarantee of
ours:

> _"But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the firstfruits of them
> that slept."_ (1 Corinthians 15:20)

### The Great Controversy Settled

The two resurrections bring the great controversy to its final resolution. The

## Practical Application

Understanding the two resurrections calls us to:

1. **Live with eternal perspective**. This life determines which resurrection we
   will experience. Every day is preparation for eternity.

2. **Grieve with hope**. When loved ones who knew Christ die, we sorrow not as
   those who have no hope (1 Thessalonians 4:13). The separation is temporary;
   the reunion will be eternal.

3. **Reject spiritualism's deceptions**. The dead know nothing (Ecclesiastes
   9:5). Any "spirit" claiming to be a departed loved one is a demonic
   deception. This truth protects us from Satan's masterful delusions in the
   last days.

4. **Rest in Christ's finished work**. Immortality is not inherent in manâ€”it is
   the gift of God through Christ (Romans 6:23; 2 Timothy 1:10). We cannot earn
   it; we receive it by faith in the One who holds the keys of death.

5. **Share the blessed hope**. The resurrection message brings comfort to the
   grieving and urgency to the careless. It is the heart of the gospel: _"If in
   this life only we have hope in Christ, we are of all men most miserable. But
   now is Christ risen"_ (1 Corinthians 15:19â€“20).
