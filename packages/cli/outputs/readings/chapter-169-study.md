# Reading 169: Promises for the Children

## Deeper Truths

### The Value of a Wise Child

The world often measures worth by age, wealth, or position. God's economy

> _"Better is a poor and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no

## Practical Application

### For Children

**Begin each day with prayer.** Talk to Jesus as you would to your kindest
friend. Ask Him for wisdom, for help to obey your parents cheerfully, and for
power to do what is right.

**Honour your parents in practical ways:**

- Obey promptly and respectfully
- Speak kindly, even when correcting seems unfair
- Help without being asked
- Remember that honouring parents is honouring God

**Stay teachable.** The wise child receives correction gladly. When you are
admonished, listen carefully rather than making excuses. This humble attitude is
precious to God.

**Come to Jesus daily.** He is never too busy for you. He longs to take you in
His arms of love and bless you.

### For Parents and Teachers

Teach children that God has made specific promises _for them_. These are not
adult concepts scaled downâ€”they are heaven's direct address to young hearts.
Help them memorize these promises as anchors for their faith.

Never forbid children from coming to Christ or suggest they must wait until they
are older to truly know Him. Jesus rebuked the disciples for this very attitude.
