### The Standard for Saints

God's people are called to a higher standard:

> _"But fornication, and all uncleanness, or covetousness, let it not be once
> named among you, as becometh saints; neither filthiness, nor foolish talking,
> nor jesting, which are not convenient: but rather giving of thanks."_
> (Ephesians 5:3, 4)

The phrase "not be once named among you" means these sins should be so foreign
to the Christian that they are not even a topic of conversation or humor among
believers. Coarse jesting and suggestive humor defile the soul and grieve the
Holy Spirit.

[Q] **"Isn't this standard impossibly strict? Everyone struggles with impure
thoughts."**

It is true that all have struggledâ€”"all have sinned, and come short of the glory
of God" (Romans 3:23). But the gospel does not merely forgive past sins; it
provides power for present victory. Christ came "to save His people _from_ their
sins" (Matthew 1:21), not merely _in_ their sins. The same Christ who said,
"Neither do I condemn thee," also said, "Go, and sin no more" (John 8:11). The
standard is high because our Saviour is mighty.

## Deeper Truths

### The Power of Thought and Association

The principle of sowing and reaping governs the thought-life:

> _"Be not deceived; God is not mocked: for whatsoever a man soweth, that shall
> he also reap. For he that soweth to his flesh shall of the flesh reap
> corruption; but he that soweth to the Spirit shall of the Spirit reap life
> everlasting."_ (Galatians 6:7, 8)

Every impure thought sown in the heart produces a harvest. The imagination is
the seedbed of action. No one can long keep himself from vicious acts whose
thoughts dwell, without restraint, upon unchaste subjects. **Purity of life
depends upon purity of mind.**

Association plays a decisive role:

> _"Be not deceived: evil communications corrupt good manners."_ (1 Corinthians
> 15:33)

[ILL] A piece of white cloth laid in a dusty room will not remain white for
long. Though the cloth does not choose to become soiled, mere exposure to the

conversation and impure influences cannot remain uncontaminated. One soon
receives the mold of frequent companionship, whether the companion be good or
bad.

The believer is therefore instructed:

> _"And have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather
> reprove them. For it is a shame even to speak of those things which are done
> of them in secret."_ (Ephesians 5:11, 12)

Even speaking of the details of vice gives sin a kind of familiarity that
diminishes horror and emboldens transgression.

### The Source of Words and Deeds

Jesus traced all speech to its fountain:

> _"O generation of vipers, how can ye, being evil, speak good things? for out
> of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh."_ (Matthew 12:34)

If the heart is defiled, the mouth will reveal it. The words we speak, the humor
we enjoy, the entertainment we consumeâ€”all these are diagnostic of the heart's
condition.

[Q] **"What about literature, media, and entertainment? How strict should we
be?"**

The principle is clear:

> _"Let no corrupt communication proceed out of your mouth, but that which is
> good to the use of edifying, that it may minister grace unto the hearers."_
> (Ephesians 4:29)

What is true of speech is equally true of what we read, watch, and listen to.
Whatever enters the mind through the eye or ear shapes the character. Parents
must be vigilant guardians of their children's reading and associations. A bad
book, a corrupt film, or degrading music may do damage that years cannot fully
repair. "One wave of moral unhealth will fever and blast the soul."

[DYKðŸ”Ž] In the final judgment, even the secrets of the heart will be brought to
light: _"For God shall bring every work into judgment, with every secret thing,
whether it be good, or whether it be evil"_ (Ecclesiastes 12:14). Nothing is
hidden from Him with whom we have to do (Hebrews 4:13).

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary and the Cleansing of the Heart

The earthly sanctuary teaches a vital lesson: the blood of the sacrifice was not
merely offered in the outer court (where the animal died), but was carried

> _"For the word of God is quick, and powerful, and sharper than any twoedged

### Righteousness by Faith and Victory Over Sin

How can the impure become pure? Not by human effort alone:

> _"Can the Ethiopian change his skin, or the leopard his spots? then may ye

When Christ lives within, His purity becomes ours. The mind is filled with His

### Lessons from Sacred History

[DYKðŸ”Ž] Social impurity was the chief cause of the Flood (Genesis 6:1-13). It
was the principal reason for the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah (Genesis
19:1-11). God destroyed 24,000 Israelites at one time because of their laxness

yielded (Genesis 41:38-44). This is a lesson for all: the momentary pleasure of
sin is nothing compared to the eternal weight of glory reserved for those who
overcome.

## Practical Application

### Guarding the Mind

The prescription for mental purity is clear:

> _"Finally, brethren, whatsoever things are true, whatsoever things are honest,
> whatsoever things are just, whatsoever things are pure, whatsoever things are
> lovely, whatsoever things are of good report; if there be any virtue, and if
> there be any praise, think on these things."_ (Philippians 4:8)

This truth calls us to:

1. **Understanding**: The mind is the citadel of the soul. What we allow in
   shapes who we become.
2. **Response**: We must actively choose what enters our mindsâ€”guarding eyes,
   ears, and associations.
3. **Growth**: By feeding the mind on Scripture, prayer, and pure associations,
   we cultivate a character fit for heaven.

### Practical Steps

- **Daily Scripture**: Fill the mind with God's Word each morning before the
  world's influences intrude.
- **Prayer for purity**: Ask God daily to cleanse the thoughts and guard the
  imagination.
- **Selective associations**: Avoid those who indulge in impure conversation;
  seek companions who lift the soul toward God.
- **Media vigilance**: Refuse entertainment that stimulates impure thoughts.
  When the marks of the enemy appear, "away with it."
- **Immediate rejection**: When impure thoughts arise, reject them instantly.
  Entertain them not. The first look may be involuntary; the second is sin.
- **Incessant mental occupation**: Idleness is the breeding ground of
  temptation. Keep the mind engaged in useful, uplifting pursuits.
