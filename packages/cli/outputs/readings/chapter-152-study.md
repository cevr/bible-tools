True righteousness can only come by faith because faith is the acknowledgment that we cannot save ourselves. Pride says, "I can do it myself." Faith says, "Without Christ I can do nothing" (John 15:5).

## Practical Application

This truth calls us to honest self-examination:

**1. Recognize pride's subtle forms**

- Taking credit for abilities that are God's gifts
- Looking down on those less educated, talented, or spiritual
- Resisting correction or instruction
- Comparing ourselves favorably to others
- Being unable to admit wrong or say "I'm sorry"
- Demanding recognition or feeling hurt when overlooked

**2. Cultivate humility actively**

- Begin each day acknowledging complete dependence on God
- Thank God specifically for abilities and accomplishments rather than taking credit
- Seek to serve rather than to be served
- Listen more than you speak
- Receive correction as a gift rather than an insult
- Rejoice in others' successes without jealousy

**3. Pursue Christ's example**

> _"Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus: Who, being in the form of God, thought it not robbery to be equal with God: But made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant."_ (Philippians 2:5-7)

Christ, who alone had reason for pride, chose the path of humility. If the Creator of the universe washed His disciples' feet, what room have we for self-exaltation?
