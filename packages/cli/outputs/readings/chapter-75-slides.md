## Slide 1 – Introduction: Not Justified by the Law

- Theme: The law reveals sin but cannot remove it—only Christ's grace justifies, yet grace never licenses disobedience.
- Key Text: Romans 3:20 – "Therefore by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in His sight: for by the law is the knowledge of sin."

---

## Slide 2 – The Law Cannot Make Anyone Righteous

- "No flesh" means this applies universally to all humanity.
- The law's purpose is diagnostic, not therapeutic.
- It diagnoses the disease of sin; it does not cure it.
- It points to sin; it cannot remove sin.
- "Justified" means declared righteous, acquitted in court—the law is the standard, not the acquitting judge.

---

## Slide 3 – Why Have the Law If It Cannot Justify?

- Without diagnosis, no one seeks the Physician.
- The law shows our need; Christ meets our need.
- Key Text: Galatians 3:24 – "Wherefore the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith."

---

## Slide 4 – The Law Reveals Sin

- The law exposes hidden transgression.
- Key Text: Romans 7:7 – "I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet."
- Paul's testimony: coveting seemed harmless until the law exposed its true nature.
- The tenth commandment reaches inward motives—the law is spiritual, searching the heart (Romans 7:14).

---

## Slide 5 – Illustration: The Mirror and the Fountain

- A mirror shows dirt on your face—it does not wash it off.
- You see the smudge, then you go to the water.
- The law is the mirror; Christ is the fountain.
- Key Text: Zechariah 13:1 – "In that day there shall be a fountain opened to the house of David and to the inhabitants of Jerusalem for sin and for uncleanness."

---

## Slide 6 – All the World Guilty Before God

- The law speaks to everyone, not just the Jews.
- Key Text: Romans 3:19 – "Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God."
- Every mouth is stopped—no defense, no excuse.
- The Jew had the written law; the Gentile had conscience (Romans 2:14–15).
- Both fall short; both need grace.

---

## Slide 7 – If the Law Could Justify, Christ Died in Vain

- The law's purpose is to drive all humanity to Christ.
- Key Text: Galatians 2:21 – "I do not frustrate the grace of God: for if righteousness come by the law, then Christ is dead in vain."

---

## Slide 8 – The Law Is Holy, Just, and Good

- The law is not the problem.
- Key Text: Romans 7:12 – "Wherefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good."
- Holy: reflects God's own character.
- Just: a fair, equitable standard.
- Good: designed for human flourishing.

---

## Slide 9 – The Problem Is Our Sinful Nature

- The carnal mind cannot keep the law.
- Key Text: Romans 8:7 – "Because the carnal mind is enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be."
- Paul defends the law: "Is the law sin? God forbid" (Romans 7:7)—the problem is indwelling sin, not the law.

---

## Slide 10 – Justification: Freely by His Grace

- How can man be justified?
- Key Text: Romans 3:24 – "Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus."
- Freely: without cause, as a gift.
- Grace: unmerited favor.
- Redemption: ransom paid, release from bondage.

---

## Slide 11 – The Source, Ground, and Means of Justification

- Source: God's grace.
- Ground: Christ's redemption.
- Means: faith (Romans 3:25).
- Free to us—but at infinite cost to heaven.
- Key Text: 1 Peter 1:18–19 – "Forasmuch as ye know that ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold... but with the precious blood of Christ."

---

## Slide 12 – Illustration: The Bankrupt Debtor

- A bankrupt debtor cannot pay a single cent.
- A wealthy friend pays the entire debt and hands over the receipt: "Paid in full."
- The debtor now stands free—not by his works, but by another's gift.
- Key Text: Romans 6:23 – "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."

---

## Slide 13 – Grace Does Not License Sin

- Objection anticipated: "If saved by grace, why not keep sinning?"
- Key Text: Romans 6:15 – "What then? shall we sin, because we are not under the law, but under grace? God forbid."
- Paul's answer: the slavery principle.

---

## Slide 14 – Two Masters, Two Outcomes

- Key Text: Romans 6:16 – "Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey; whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness?"
- Sin leads to death.
- Obedience leads to righteousness.
- Grace produces obedience—not excuses from it.

---

## Slide 15 – Grace Teaches Us to Live Godly

- Key Text: Titus 2:11–12 – "For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men, teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world."
- Grace that doesn't transform is grace misunderstood.

---

## Slide 16 – No Condemnation in Christ Jesus

- Key Text: Romans 8:1 – "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit."
- "In Christ Jesus": union with Him, not mere intellectual assent.
- "Walk not after the flesh": a daily choice, Spirit-empowered living.
- "But after the Spirit": the indwelling Spirit enables obedience.

---

## Slide 17 – The Righteousness of the Law Fulfilled in Us

- Key Text: Romans 8:4 – "That the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit."
- "Walk" means conduct or lifestyle—an ongoing, habitual direction of life.
- This is not sinless perfection by human effort.
- It is Christ's life lived out in the believer.

---

## Slide 18 – Victory Through Abiding, Not Striving

- Key Text: Galatians 2:20 – "I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me: and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God, who loved me, and gave himself for me."
- Key Text: John 15:4–5 – "Abide in me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit of itself, except it abide in the vine; no more can ye, except ye abide in me. I am the vine, ye are the branches... without me ye can do nothing."

---

## Slide 19 – The Harmony of Law and Grace

- Law diagnoses; grace heals.
- Law condemns; grace justifies.
- Law demands righteousness; grace provides righteousness.
- Law reveals God's standard; grace enables meeting that standard.
- Both are from the same God—they are not in conflict.

---

## Slide 20 – Faith Establishes the Law

- Key Text: Romans 3:31 – "Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law."
- Grace brings us to obedience, not away from it.

---

## Slide 21 – Appeal: Receive Justification as a Gift

- The law has done its work—it shows us guilty, mouths stopped.
- Only one way forward: Christ's blood, freely offered.
- Receive justification as a gift today.
- Walk in the Spirit—let grace teach obedience, not excuse disobedience.

---

## Slide 22 – Closing Texts

- "Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus." (Romans 3:24)
- "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit." (Romans 8:1)
