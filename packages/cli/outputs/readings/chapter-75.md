# Reading 75: Not Justified by the Law

What is salvation — and why does it matter that we get the answer right?

---

## What Is Salvation?

- Ask ten people "what is salvation?" and you'll get ten answers
  - Forgiveness? Relationship with God? Knowing God? Going to heaven?
  - All true — but incomplete
- To understand salvation we must first answer: **what are we being saved from?**
- Humanity faces **two problems:**
  1. **We have sinned** — therefore we must die (Romans 6:23)
  2. **We cannot stop sinning** — our nature is broken (Romans 8:7)
- Most people focus on problem #1. God is focused on problem #2.

[Q] **Which problem do you think about more — the penalty of sin, or the power of sin?**
→ The answer reveals how we understand the gospel

---

## What Is Sin?

- "I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet" (Romans 7:7)
- "By the law is the knowledge of sin" (Romans 3:20)
- Sin is the transgression of the law (1 John 3:4) — but what is the law?
- **Luke 10:27** — love God with all your heart, love your neighbor as yourself
  - The law is a description of **love**
- "God is love" (1 John 4:8) — so the law is a description of **God's character**
- "The law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good" (Romans 7:12)
  - Holy — reflects God's own nature
  - Just — fair, equitable
  - Good — designed for human flourishing

[→] Sin isn't just breaking rules. It's a departure from the character of God.

---

## The Law Is a Standard, Not a Solution

- "Therefore by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in His sight" (Romans 3:20)
- "No flesh" — universal. No exceptions.
- The law diagnoses disease; it does not cure it
- The law points to sin; it cannot remove sin

[ILL] A child waits in line for a ride. When it's his turn, he sees a sign: "You must be this tall to enter."
He's too short. Can he ask the sign to fix the problem?
The sign doesn't make you taller. It only tells you where you stand.
→ The law is the sign. It reveals the standard — it cannot lift you to meet it.

- "Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God" (Romans 3:19)
- Every mouth stopped — no defense, no excuse, Jew and Gentile alike
- The law was never meant to save — it was meant to drive us to the One who can (Galatians 3:24)

---

## What Was Lost: The Image of God

- To understand why God targets problem #2, go back to the beginning
- "God created man in His own image, in the image of God created He him" (Genesis 1:27)
- After sin: "Adam... begat a son in his own likeness, after his image" (Genesis 5:3)
  - Not God's image — Adam's fallen image
- **What does "image" mean?**

- **Exodus 33–34** — Moses asked God: "Show me Thy glory" (Exodus 33:18)
  - God understood: Moses wanted to see what He looked like — His likeness
  - God said: "Thou canst not see My face: for there shall no man see Me, and live" (Exodus 33:20)
  - Yet God did show him something:
  - "The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth, keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin" (Exodus 34:6-7)
  - God equated His **glory** with His **character** — and His **image** with His **name**

- **Jesus confirms this:**
  - "Who being the brightness of His glory, and the express image of His person" (Hebrews 1:3)
  - Philip: "Lord, show us the Father" — Jesus: "He that hath seen Me hath seen the Father" (John 14:8-9)
  - **Image = Character**

[→] What was lost in Eden was not just innocence. It was the image of God — His character reproduced in humanity.

---

## God's Real Target: The Root Cause

- God is not primarily interested in fixing problem #1 (the penalty)
- He wants to fix problem #2 (the power — the broken nature)
- Because the root cause of sin is our **image** — our character
- "The carnal mind is enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be" (Romans 8:7)
  - Not just "doesn't obey" — **cannot** obey
  - The flesh doesn't need improvement. It needs replacement.

[Q] **If God only forgave sin without changing our nature, what would happen?**
→ We'd keep sinning. Forgiveness without transformation is a revolving door.

---

## Justification: Forgiveness and Power

- "Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus" (Romans 3:24)
  - **Freely** (dōrean) — without cause, as a gift
  - **Grace** (charis) — unmerited favor
  - **Redemption** (apolutrōsis) — ransom paid, release from bondage
- But justification is not only legal pardon — it includes **power**

- **Mark 2: The Paralytic**
  - A man who cannot walk — his will to move doesn't matter, his body won't respond
  - Jesus says: "Son, thy sins be forgiven thee" (Mark 2:5) — then: "Arise, and take up thy bed, and go thy way into thine house" (Mark 2:11)
  - Forgiveness AND power to walk — given **immediately**
  - And notice: Jesus told him **where to go** — home

- **John 8: The Woman Caught in Adultery**
  - "Neither do I condemn thee: go, and sin no more" (John 8:11)
  - Not condemnation. Not permission. Forgiveness with a new direction.

[→] Justification is the moment God forgives and empowers — simultaneously

---

## Sanctification: Going Home

- The paralytic was told to walk home — that **journey** is sanctification
- "God hath from the beginning chosen you to salvation through sanctification of the Spirit and belief of the truth" (2 Thessalonians 2:13)
  - Salvation **through** sanctification — not apart from it
- Sanctification is the process of the image of God being restored in us

- "What then? shall we sin, because we are not under the law, but under grace? God forbid" (Romans 6:15)
- "Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey; whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness?" (Romans 6:16)
- "The grace of God that bringeth salvation... teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world" (Titus 2:11-12)

- **Walking in the Spirit:**
  - "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit" (Romans 8:1)
  - "That the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit" (Romans 8:4)
  - The righteousness of the law **fulfilled in us** — not by us, but in us by the Spirit

[Q] **If the paralytic decided halfway home that he didn't want to go — what then?**
→ He can walk. He has the power. But if he chooses not to go home, he loses what was given.
→ Sanctification is not optional. It is the path of salvation itself.

---

## The New Heart: God's Solution to Problem #2

- God's answer to the broken image is not a patched-up version of the old
- "I will put My law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts" (Jeremiah 31:33)
- "A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh. And I will put My Spirit within you, and **cause** you to walk in My statutes" (Ezekiel 36:26-27)
  - Not "help you try harder" — **cause** you to walk
  - This is God fixing problem #2 at the root

| Problem                | Symptom          | God's Solution                                     |
| ---------------------- | ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| We have sinned         | Death penalty    | Justification — forgiveness through Christ's blood |
| We cannot stop sinning | Broken character | Sanctification — new heart, His law written within |

- "Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law" (Romans 3:31)
  - Faith doesn't abolish the standard. It enables us to meet it — by a new nature, not by old effort.

---

## Appeal

- The law has done its work — it shows us guilty, every mouth stopped
- But God is not content to merely pardon the guilty
- He wants to restore what was lost in Eden: His image in humanity
- Justification is the gift — immediate, free, complete
- Sanctification is the journey home — empowered by the same grace
- Will you let Him write His law on your heart today?

_"Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus."_ (Romans 3:24)

_"A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you."_ (Ezekiel 36:26)
