# Bible Study: The Powers That Be

## Introduction

The Christian's relationship to civil government presents one of the most
practical and profound questions of faith: How do we honor earthly authorities
while maintaining supreme allegiance to God? Scripture reveals that civil
government is indeed ordained of God for the good of societyâ€”yet there exists a

## Core Truths

### God's Ordination of Civil Government

The apostle Paul makes a remarkable declaration regarding earthly powers:

> _"Let every soul be subject unto the higher powers. For there is no power but
> of God: the powers that be are ordained of God."_ (Romans 13:1)

This statement establishes a foundational truth: civil government is not a human
invention but a divine institution. God has ordained governmental authority for
the preservation of order and the restraint of evil in a fallen world.

**Ordained**: This means "appointed" or "established by decree." God has set up
civil authority as part of His providential care for humanity.

The proper sphere of civil government is clearly defined:

> _"For rulers are not a terror to good works, but to the evil... for he is the
> minister of God, a revenger to execute wrath upon him that doeth evil."_
> (Romans 13:3â€“4)

Government's God-given role is to:

- **Restrain evil** through the administration of justice
- **Protect the innocent** from those who would do harm
- **Maintain civil order** for the welfare of society

### The Christian's Duty to Civil Authority

Scripture gives clear instruction regarding our responsibility to government:

> _"Put them in mind to be subject to principalities and powers, to obey
> magistrates, to be ready to every good work."_ (Titus 3:1)

> _"Submit yourselves to every ordinance of man for the Lord's sake: whether it

- **Pay taxes honestly**: _"Render therefore to all their dues: tribute to whom

- **Show proper respect**: _"Honour all men... Honour the king"_ (1 Peter 2:17)
- **Obey just laws**: _"To obey magistrates, to be ready to every good work"_
  (Titus 3:1)

This submission is not based on the worthiness of rulers but on recognition of
God's ordering of society. Even when governing authorities are corrupt or
unjust, Christians are called to be model citizens in all matters that do not
conflict with God's higher law.

## Deeper Truths

### Two Distinct Spheres of Authority

Christ Himself established the principle that distinguishes between civil and
religious authority:

> _"Render therefore unto Caesar the things which are Caesar's; and unto God the
> things that are God's."_ (Matthew 22:21)

This profound statement reveals that there are two separate realms:

1. **Caesar's realm**: Civil mattersâ€”taxes, public order, civic duties
2. **God's realm**: Religious mattersâ€”worship, conscience, faith, obedience to
   divine law

No. Romans 13 defines the _legitimate_ sphere of civil authorityâ€”punishment of
evil and protection of good in _civil_ matters. When government steps outside

### The Sanctuary Principle of Government

In the sanctuary service, we see God's ideal pattern of governance. The priests
served in sacred things, teaching the people God's law (Malachi 2:7). Civil
rulers administered justice in civil matters. When King Uzziah attempted to burn
incense in the templeâ€”a priestly functionâ€”God struck him with leprosy (2
Chronicles 26:16â€“21).

This separation illustrates a vital truth: **God has never authorized civil
government to enforce religious observance**. The mingling of church and state
corrupts both institutions and inevitably leads to persecution of those who
follow conscience rather than civil decree.

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Higher Power Above All Powers

Solomon, the wisest of earthly kings, recognized a supreme authority above all
human government:

> _"If thou seest the oppression of the poor, and violent perverting of judgment
> and justice in a province, marvel not at the matter: for he that is higher
> than the highest regardeth; and there be higher than they."_ (Ecclesiastes
> 5:8)

God observes every act of injustice. No tyrant's decree escapes His notice. The
persecutor may triumph for a season, but there is One who "regardeth"â€”who takes
careful note of every wrong done to His children.

### Religion: The Realm of God Alone

**Religion**: The recognition of God as an object of worship, love, and
obedience; man's personal relation of faith and duty to his Creator.

By its very nature, religion is **between the soul and God**. No human authority
can stand between a person and their Maker:

> _"And call no man your father upon the earth: for one is your Father, which is
> in heaven."_ (Matthew 23:9)

> _"Neither be ye called masters: for one is your Master, even Christ."_
> (Matthew 23:10)

[Q] **"Doesn't the state have authority to enforce religious observances for the
good of society?"**

Christ's words make clear that worship belongs to God alone: _"Thou shalt
worship the Lord thy God, and Him only shalt thou serve"_ (Matthew 4:10). When
civil government attempts to enforce religious practice, it usurps God's
prerogative and puts itself in the place of God (2 Thessalonians 2:3â€“4). The
history of religious persecutionâ€”from pagan Rome to the medieval church to
modern totalitarian statesâ€”demonstrates the evil fruit of such presumption.

### Accountability to God Alone in Matters of Faith

> _"So then every one of us shall give account of himself to God."_ (Romans
> 14:12)

Since every soul must personally answer to God for their faith and practice, no
human authority can lawfully compel conscience:

> _"Who art thou that judgest another man's servant? to his own master he
> standeth or falleth."_ (Romans 14:4)

The believer is Christ's purchased possession:

> _"Ye are bought with a price; be not ye the servants of men."_ (1 Corinthians
> 7:23)

This does not mean Christians are rebellious or anarchical. In civil matters,
they are the most loyal citizens. But in matters of faith, worship, and
conscience, they acknowledge only one Masterâ€”the Lord Jesus Christ.

### The Great Controversy and Religious Liberty

The conflict between God's government and Satan's rebellion centers on the
question of allegiance. Satan has always sought to compel worshipâ€”whether
through Nebuchadnezzar's image, Darius's decree, Rome's imperial cult, or the
religious persecutions of the Dark Ages.

God's government, by contrast, operates on the principle of love and free
choice. He invites: _"Come now, and let us reason together"_ (Isaiah 1:18). He
appeals: _"Choose you this day whom ye will serve"_ (Joshua 24:15). He never
compels.

When civil government attempts to enforce religious observance, it reflects
Satan's principle of coercion rather than God's principle of liberty. This is
why prophecy reveals that in the last days, a union of church and state will
