## Deeper Truths

### The Cross: Center of God's Work

[Q] **"Why does Scripture emphasize 'It is finished' at the cross rather than at the resurrection?"**

The cross was the moment of complete payment. Christ's resurrection confirmed His victory, but Calvary was where the price was paid. Every drop of the bitter cup was drained; every sin of humanity was borne. The resurrection declares that the Father accepted the sacrifice, but the cross is where the Lamb "finished" the work of atonement (Hebrews 9:26; 10:12).

[ILL] Consider a prisoner awaiting execution. The governor signs a full pardon, and the document is delivered to the prison. The moment the pardon is signed, the prisoner's legal standing is changedâ€”though he may not yet walk free. So it was at Calvary: Christ signed our pardon in His own blood. The resurrection opened the prison door, but the pardon was "finished" at the cross.

### The Seventh Plague: Probation's End

When the seventh angel pours out his vial and the voice from the throne cries, _"It is done"_ (Revelation 16:17), human probation has forever closed. Christ leaves the Most Holy Place, and mercy no longer pleads for the impenitent. This solemn "It is done" announces that all decisions are finalâ€”the righteous sealed, the wicked confirmed in rebellion.

This is not arbitrary judgment but the inevitable result of choices made. God waits long, but there comes a moment when every heart is fixed.

> _"He that is unjust, let him be unjust still... and he that is holy, let him be holy still."_ (Revelation 22:11)

### The New Earth: Conflict Eternally Ended

The final "It is done" (Revelation 21:6) announces what the redeemed have longed for: sin is no more, death is no more, the curse is no more. God Himself will dwell with His people:

> _"He will dwell with them, and they shall be His people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God."_ (Revelation 21:3)
