# Reading 144: The Ordinance of Humility

## Introduction

On the night of His betrayal, Jesus instituted a sacred ordinance that strikes at the very root of human pride and self-exaltation. The foot-washing service, often called the ordinance of humility, was not merely a cultural courtesy but a divinely appointed ceremony carrying profound spiritual lessons for every believer until Christ returns. In this act, the King of Glory knelt as a servant, forever defining the character of His kingdom.

## Core Truths

### Christ's Longing for Fellowship

Jesus approached His final Passover with intense anticipation: _"With desire I have desired to eat this Passover with you before I suffer"_ (Luke 22:15). The original Greek conveys a superlative longingâ€”literally, "with desiring I have desired." Our Saviour earnestly sought this final time of intimate fellowship with His disciples before Calvary.

### The Spirit of the World vs. The Spirit of Christ

While Jesus contemplated the cross, the disciples were consumed with self-advancement:

> _"The kings of the Gentiles exercise lordship over them; and they that exercise authority upon them are called benefactors. But ye shall not be so: but he that is greatest among you, let him be as the younger; and he that is chief, as he that doth serve."_ (Luke 22:25, 26)

The contrast could not be more stark:

- **Worldly greatness**: Exercising authority, seeking titles, demanding service
- **Kingdom greatness**: Serving others, taking the lowest place, seeking no recognition

Christ declared His own position plainly: _"I am among you as he that serveth"_ (Luke 22:27). The Creator of the universe defined His mission not in terms of power received but service rendered.

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Issue of Authority and Service

The great controversy between Christ and Satan is fundamentally about the character of God's government. Satan claimed God was arbitrary and self-serving. Jesus came to reveal that divine authority is exercised through humble service, not domination.

When Christ knelt to wash feet, He demonstrated the principle that governs heaven itself: _"Whosoever will be great among you, let him be your minister; and whosoever will be chief among you, let him be your servant"_ (Matthew 20:26, 27).

This principle will characterize God's redeemed people throughout eternity. The ordinance of humility trains us now for the spirit we will carry forever.

### Sanctuary Connections

The foot-washing ordinance beautifully illustrates the believer's ongoing relationship with Christ's mediatorial ministry:

- **Outer court (justification)**: The initial "bath"â€”complete cleansing through acceptance of Christ's sacrifice
- **Holy place (sanctification)**: The ongoing "foot washing"â€”daily cleansing as we walk in a defiling world, maintained through Christ's continual intercession
- **Preparation for the Most Holy Place**: The self-examination and reconciliation that prepares God's people for the final work of judgment and sealing

Just as the priests washed at the laver before ministering in the sanctuary, believers today are to come to the Lord's Supper with hearts examined and cleansedâ€”feet washed, as it wereâ€”ready to commune with their Lord.

### Service to "the Least"

Christ links this ordinance to a broader principle of kingdom service:

> _"Inasmuch as ye have done it unto one of the least of these My brethren, ye have done it unto Me."_ (Matthew 25:40)

Every act of humble service rendered to the lowliest believer is received by Christ as service to Himself. The foot-washing ceremony teaches us to see Christ in every brother and sister, regardless of their social standing, education, or apparent importance.

## Practical Application

### What This Ordinance Requires of Us

**Before participating:**

- Examine your heart for cherished grudges or unconfessed wrongs
- Seek reconciliation with any you have offended or who have offended you
- Approach with willingness both to serve and to be served

**During the ordinance:**

- Allow the physical act to prompt spiritual reflection
- Pray for the one whose feet you wash
- Receive the service of another with humility, not proud reluctance like Peter

**After participating:**

- Carry the spirit of humble service into daily life
- Look for opportunities to serve "the least" of Christ's brethren
- Remember that happiness is promised to those who _do_ these things, not merely understand them

### Breaking Down Pride

The ordinance of humility strikes at the root of:

- **Pride of position**: We kneel before those we might consider "beneath" us
- **Pride of independence**: We allow others to serve us, acknowledging our need
- **Pride of self-righteousness**: We confess that we need continual cleansing
- **Pride of appearance**: We handle the dusty, common feet of our fellow believers

[Q] **"Isn't foot washing merely a cultural practice that doesn't apply today?"**

While foot washing served a practical purpose in sandal-wearing cultures, Christ elevated it to a spiritual ordinance with permanent significance. The practical need may have diminished in modern settings, but the spiritual lessons remain vital:

- We still need humility
- We still need reconciliation with one another
- We still need preparation for communion with Christ
- We still need reminders that greatness in God's kingdom is measured by service

If anything, in our age of self-promotion and status-seeking, we need this ordinance more than ever.

## Appeal

_"If ye know these things, happy are ye if ye do them."_ (John 13:17)

Christ has given us both the example and the command. He who was equal with God knelt as a servant. He who could command legions of angels washed the feet of His betrayer. He who sustains the universe by the word of His power girded Himself with a towel.

Will you follow His example? Will you lay aside your garments of pride and self-importance? Will you allow Christ to wash away not only the guilt of sin but also the daily defilement of walking in a sinful world?

The promise is sure: happiness awaits those who know these things and do them. Not happiness as the world givesâ€”dependent on circumstancesâ€”but the deep, settled blessedness of those who walk in the footsteps of Jesus.

May we each come to the ordinance of humility with hearts prepared to serve and to be served, knowing that in this sacred act, we have communion with our Lord and preparation for eternal fellowship in His kingdom.
