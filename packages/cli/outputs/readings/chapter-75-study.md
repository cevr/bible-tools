# Reading 75: Not Justified by the Law

The law reveals sin but cannot remove it—only Christ's grace justifies, yet grace never licenses disobedience.

---

## The Law's Limitation: It Cannot Justify

- **Core truth** — law cannot make anyone righteous
  - "Therefore by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in His sight" (Romans 3:20)
  - "no flesh" = universal; applies to all humanity
- **Why not?** — law's purpose is diagnostic, not therapeutic
  - Diagnoses disease; does not cure it
  - Points to sin; cannot remove sin

[DYK🔎] "justified" (dikaioō) = declared righteous, acquitted in court — law is the standard, not the acquitting judge

[Q] **If the law can't justify, why have it?**
→ Without diagnosis, no one seeks the Physician
→ Law shows need; Christ meets need

---

## The Law's Function: Revealing Sin

- **Exposes hidden transgression**
  - "I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet" (Romans 7:7)
  - "by the law is the knowledge of sin" (Romans 3:20, last clause)
- **Paul's testimony** — coveting seemed harmless until law exposed its nature
  - Tenth commandment reaches inward motives
  - [→] Law is spiritual, searching the heart (Romans 7:14)

[ILL] Mirror shows dirt on face — doesn't wash it off.
You see the smudge, then go to the water.
→ Law = mirror; Christ = fountain (Zechariah 13:1)

---

## Universal Scope: All the World Guilty

- **Not just Jews** — law speaks to everyone
  - "Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God" (Romans 3:19)
- **Every mouth stopped** — no defense, no excuse
  - Jew had written law; Gentile had conscience (Romans 2:14-15)
  - Both fall short; both need grace
- **Purpose** — drive all humanity to Christ
  - "the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith" (Galatians 3:24)

[→] If law could justify anyone, Christ died in vain (Galatians 2:21)

---

## The Law's Character: Holy, Just, Good

- **The law is not the problem**
  - "Wherefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good" (Romans 7:12)
- **Holy** — reflects God's own character
- **Just** — fair, equitable standard
- **Good** — designed for human flourishing
- **The problem** — our sinful nature cannot keep it
  - "the carnal mind is enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be" (Romans 8:7)

[DYK🔎] Paul defends the law in Romans 7 — "Is the law sin? God forbid" (v. 7) — problem is indwelling sin, not the law

[TANGENT] sanctuary connection: law in ark of covenant, under mercy seat — justice met by mercy through blood

---

## Justification: Freely by Grace

- **How then can man be justified?**
  - "Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus" (Romans 3:24)
- **Three key words:**
  - **Freely** (dōrean) = without cause, as a gift
  - **Grace** (charis) = unmerited favor
  - **Redemption** (apolutrōsis) = ransom paid, release from bondage
- **Source** — God's grace
- **Ground** — Christ's redemption
- **Means** — faith (Romans 3:25)

[Q] **If justification is free, what did it cost?**
→ Free to us; infinite cost to heaven
→ "redeemed... with the precious blood of Christ" (1 Peter 1:18-19)

[ILL] Bankrupt debtor — cannot pay a single cent.
Wealthy friend pays entire debt, hands receipt: "Paid in full."
Debtor now stands free — not by his works, but by another's gift.
→ "the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord" (Romans 6:23)

---

## Grace Does Not License Sin

- **Objection anticipated** — "If saved by grace, why not keep sinning?"
  - "What then? shall we sin, because we are not under the law, but under grace? God forbid" (Romans 6:15)
- **Paul's answer** — slavery principle
  - "Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey; whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness?" (Romans 6:16)
- **Two masters, two outcomes:**
  - Sin → death
  - Obedience → righteousness
- **Grace produces obedience** — not excuses from it
  - "the grace of God that bringeth salvation... teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world" (Titus 2:11-12)

[→] Grace that doesn't transform is grace misunderstood

---

## Walking Free: No Condemnation in Christ

- **Condition for freedom**
  - "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit" (Romans 8:1)
- **"In Christ Jesus"** — union with Him, not mere intellectual assent
- **"Walk not after the flesh"** — daily choice, Spirit-empowered living
- **"But after the Spirit"** — the indwelling Spirit enables obedience
  - "that the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit" (Romans 8:4)

[DYK🔎] "walk" (peripateō) = conduct, lifestyle — present tense implies ongoing, habitual direction of life

[Q] **Is this sinless perfection by human effort?**
→ No — it is Christ's life lived out in the believer
→ "I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me" (Galatians 2:20)
→ Victory through abiding, not striving (John 15:4-5)

---

## The Harmony of Law and Grace

- Law **diagnoses** — grace **heals**
- Law **condemns** — grace **justifies**
- Law **demands** righteousness — grace **provides** righteousness
- Law reveals **God's standard** — grace enables **meeting that standard**

| Law's Role            | Grace's Role       |
| --------------------- | ------------------ |
| Shows sin             | Forgives sin       |
| Cannot change heart   | Creates new heart  |
| Points to need        | Meets the need     |
| Condemns transgressor | Justifies believer |

- **Both from same God** — not in conflict
  - "Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law" (Romans 3:31)

[TANGENT] sanctuary: brazen altar (grace/blood) leads into holy place where law is enshrined — grace brings us to obedience, not away from it

---

## Appeal

- The law has done its work — it shows us guilty, mouths stopped
- Only one way forward: Christ's blood, freely offered
- Receive justification as a gift today
- Walk in the Spirit — let grace teach obedience, not excuse disobedience

_"Being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus."_ (Romans 3:24)

_"There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit."_ (Romans 8:1)
