## Biblical Principles for Eating and Drinking

### The Overarching Principle

> _"Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God."_ (1 Corinthians 10:31)

This comprehensive principle covers every aspect of life, but particularly our diet. The question to ask before eating or drinking anything is not merely "Is this lawful?" but "Does this glorify God?"

### Lawful vs. Expedient

Paul teaches an important distinction:

> _"All things are lawful for me, but all things are not expedient: all things are lawful for me, but all things edify not."_ (1 Corinthians 10:23)

**Expedient**: Profitable, advantageous, contributing to one's good or the good of others.

**Edify**: To build up, strengthen, or improve (from Latin _aedificare_, to build).

The mature Christian asks not only "Is this permitted?" but "Is this wise? Does this build up my body, my spiritual life, my influence for good?"

### God's Original Diet

In Eden, before sin entered, God prescribed the ideal human diet:

> _"And God said, Behold, I have given you every herb bearing seed, which is upon the face of all the earth, and every tree, in the which is the fruit of a tree yielding seed; to you it shall be for meat."_ (Genesis 1:29)

The original diet consisted of fruits, grains, and nutsâ€”plant foods in their natural state. This was the diet of Paradise, and it sustained humanity for the long-lived generations before the Flood.

[DYKðŸ”Ž] The longest-lived people in Scriptureâ€”those who lived 800-900+ yearsâ€”lived before the Flood on the original plant-based diet. After the Flood, when flesh foods were permitted, human lifespans dropped dramatically within just a few generations (compare Genesis 5 with Genesis 11).

### The Permission of Flesh Food and Clean/Unclean Distinctions

After the Flood, God permitted flesh food but with significant restrictions:

> _"Whatsoever parteth the hoof, and is cloven footed, and cheweth the cud, among the beasts, that shall ye eat."_ (Leviticus 11:3)

The distinction between "clean" and "unclean" animals predates the Mosaic law:

> _"Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens, the male and his female; and of beasts that are not clean by two, the male and his female."_ (Genesis 7:2)

Noah knew this distinction before Sinaiâ€”it was not merely a ceremonial Jewish law but a principle rooted in creation.

### The Swine Specifically Forbidden

Among unclean animals, the swine receives particular attention:

> _"And the swine, because it divideth the hoof, yet cheweth not the cud, it is unclean unto you: ye shall not eat of their flesh, nor touch their dead carcass."_ (Deuteronomy 14:8)

The pig appears to meet one criterion (cloven hoof) but fails the other (chewing the cud). It is a scavenger by nature, consuming refuse, carrion, and waste. Its flesh harbors parasites and disease-causing organisms that cooking does not always destroy.

[DYKðŸ”Ž] Modern science confirms what Scripture declared millennia ago: pork is associated with higher rates of parasitic infection (trichinosis, tapeworm), hepatitis E virus, and various food-borne illnesses compared to clean meats. The swine's digestive system does not filter toxins as effectively as ruminant animals that chew the cud.

### The Reason for Dietary Restrictions

Why did God restrict Israel's diet?

> _"For thou art a holy people unto the Lord thy God, and the Lord hath chosen thee to be a peculiar people unto Himself, above all the nations that are upon the earth. Thou shalt not eat any abominable thing."_ (Deuteronomy 14:2-3)

**Holy**: Set apart, consecrated to sacred use.

**Peculiar**: From Latin _peculium_, meaning "private property"â€”God's treasured possession.

God's people were to be distinct from surrounding nations in every way, including their diet. These distinctions were not arbitrary ceremonial rules but wise provisions for the health and holiness of His people.

The prophet Daniel demonstrated the connection between diet and spiritual faithfulness:

Daniel recognized that eating the king's rich food and wine would "defile" him. His choice of simple plant food ("pulse"â€”vegetables and grains) resulted in superior physical health and mental clarity (Daniel 1:15, 17, 20).

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### Health and the Sanctuary

The sanctuary teaches us about God's plan for complete restoration. Consider:

- **Outer Court (Altar of Sacrifice)**: Christ's death provides forgivenessâ€”but He died also to free us from the _power_ of sin (Romans 6:6-7), including sins against the body
- **Holy Place (Table of Showbread)**: The bread represented Christ, the Bread of Life, who sustains usâ€”both spiritually and in wisdom for physical living
- **Most Holy Place (Preparation for Translation)**: The final generation will be so closely connected with God that their bodies will be transformed without seeing death (1 Corinthians 15:51-52)

Those preparing for Christ's return must have bodies and minds fitted for heaven. Gross habits and defiled temples cannot receive the seal of God or stand in His presence.

### Righteousness by Faith and Health

True health reform is never legalism or self-righteousness. It flows from:

1. **Gratitude**: We love Him who first loved us (1 John 4:19)
2. **Cooperation**: We work with God's power, not our own willpower (Philippians 2:12-13)
3. **Faith**: We trust that God's ways are best, even when appetite protests (Proverbs 3:5-6)
4. **Sanctification**: The Holy Spirit transforms our desires as we surrender (Romans 12:2)

The same Christ who forgives our sins also empowers victory over harmful habits. We cannot overcome by human effort, but "I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me" (Philippians 4:13).

### The Prophetic Warning

Isaiah's prophecy connects diet directly to Christ's return:

> _"For behold, the Lord will come with fire, and with His chariots like a whirlwind, to render His anger with fury, and His rebuke with flames of fire... They that sanctify themselves, and purify themselves in the gardens behind one tree in the midst, eating swine's flesh, and the abomination, and the mouse, shall be consumed together, saith the Lord."_ (Isaiah 66:15-17)

This passage describes people who attempt self-sanctification ("sanctify themselves") while continuing in practices God has forbidden. At Christ's coming, the distinction between those who truly honor God and those who merely profess to do so will be revealed.

[Q] **"Wasn't the distinction between clean and unclean foods abolished in the New Testament?"**

Some point to Peter's vision in Acts 10 or Paul's statements in Romans 14 and Colossians 2. But careful study shows:

- Peter's vision was about _people_, not foodâ€”God was teaching him not to call Gentiles "unclean" (Acts 10:28, 34-35)
- Romans 14 addresses _scruples_ over meat offered to idols, not the clean/unclean distinction
- Colossians 2:16 concerns _feast days_ ("meat and drink" in the context of holy days), not daily diet

Nowhere does the New Testament declare swine or other unclean animals fit for food. The principles of health established at creation and clarified in Scripture remain valid for those who desire to glorify God in their bodies.

## Practical Application

### What This Means for Daily Living

1. **Recognize your body as sacred**: You are not your own; you belong to God by creation and redemption

2. **Choose foods that glorify God**: Ask not "What do I crave?" but "What will best maintain this temple for God's service?"

3. **Return to Eden's diet where possible**: Fruits, vegetables, grains, nuts, and legumes remain God's ideal; these support optimal health

4. **Avoid unclean foods**: The biblical distinctions between clean and unclean animals remain valid principles of health

5. **Practice temperance in all things**: Even good foods can be harmful in excess; self-control is a fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23)

6. **Depend on Christ for power**: Victory over appetite comes not through willpower but through surrender to Christ (John 15:5)

### Steps Toward Change

For those convicted about health but struggling with long-standing habits:

1. **Pray for desire**: Ask God to change your wants, not just your willpower
2. **Learn why**: Understanding the reasons strengthens resolve
3. **Make gradual changes**: Replace harmful items with healthier alternatives
4. **Find support**: Share your journey with fellow believers
5. **Trust the process**: God completes what He begins (Philippians 1:6)
