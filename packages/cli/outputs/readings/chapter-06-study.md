# Bible Study: Creation and the Creator

### The Identity of the Creator

Scripture opens with a declaration that answers humanity's deepest questions about origins:

> _"In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth."_ (Genesis 1:1)

## Deeper Truths

### The Creation of Mankind

The creation of man stands apart from all other creative acts:

> _"And the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul."_ (Genesis 2:7)

While other creatures were spoken into existence, God _formed_ man with His own hands, like a potter carefully shaping clay. Then came the most intimate actâ€”God breathed into Adam's nostrils. This was not merely the impartation of oxygen but the gift of life from the very breath of the Almighty.

### The Image of God

> _"So God created man in His own image, in the image of God created He him; male and female created He them."_ (Genesis 1:27)

Notice that _bara_ appears three times in this single verseâ€”the only place in Genesis 1 where this word is repeated. This triple emphasis signals the unique significance of humanity's creation.

[Q] **"What does it mean to be made in God's image? Does God have a physical form like ours?"**

The "image of God" (_imago Dei_) refers primarily to:

- **Moral likeness**: The capacity for righteousness, holiness, and love
- **Rational capacity**: The ability to think, reason, and make moral choices
- **Creative ability**: The power to create, appreciate beauty, and exercise dominion
- **Relational nature**: The capacity for fellowship with God and with one another
- **Dominion**: Authority to represent God as stewards over creation (Genesis 1:26)

When sin entered, this image was marred but not destroyed. The plan of salvation aims at nothing less than the complete restoration of God's image in humanity (Colossians 3:10; 2 Corinthians 3:18).

### Man's Original Dominion and Home

God crowned humanity with authority and responsibility:

> _"Thou madest him to have dominion over the works of Thy hands; Thou hast put all things under his feet."_ (Psalm 8:6)

### The Test of Loyalty

In Eden stood two special treesâ€”the tree of life and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil:

> _"Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat: but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die."_ (Genesis 2:16-17)

The tree was not a trap but a giftâ€”the gift of freedom. Love cannot exist without choice. God did not create robots programmed for obedience but beings capable of genuine love, which requires the genuine possibility of rejection. The tree represented:

- **The principle of free will**: Without the possibility of disobedience, obedience has no moral value
- **The boundary of creaturely dependence**: There are realms of knowledge that belong to God alone
- **The opportunity for faith**: Trusting God's word even when the reason is not fully understood

Notice the generosity of God's command: "Of _every_ tree of the garden thou mayest _freely_ eat"â€”only one was withheld. God gave everything; He asked for trust in one small matter.

## Practical Application

### We Are His Workmanship

> _"For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus unto good works, which God hath before ordained that we should walk in them."_ (Ephesians 2:10)

The word "workmanship" (_poiÄ“ma_) echoes Romans 1:20â€”we are God's poem, His masterpiece. This truth calls us to:

1. **Identity**: Know that you are not an accident of nature but a deliberate creation of infinite Love. Your existence has meaning and purpose.

2. **Worship**: Give wholehearted worship to the Creator alone. Let the Sabbath become not merely a day of rest but a celebration of relationship with your Maker.

3. **Stewardship**: Care for your body, mind, and the natural world as sacred trusts from the Creator.

4. **Transformation**: Surrender daily to the re-creative power of Christ. The same word that spoke worlds into existence can speak peace, purity, and power into your life.

5. **Brotherhood**: Treat every human being as a fellow creature made in God's image, regardless of external differences.

### Hope in the New Creation

> _"For behold, I create new heavens and a new earth: and the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind."_ (Isaiah 65:17)

The God who created Eden will create Eden restored. The power that brought light out of darkness at the beginning will banish all darkness forever in the end. Creation points backward to our origin and forward to our destiny.

[Q] **"If God could create a perfect world that fell into sin, how do we know the new earth won't fall too?"**
