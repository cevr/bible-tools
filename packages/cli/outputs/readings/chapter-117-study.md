## Practical Application

### How to Stand Firm

**1. Ground yourself in Scripture.** Know what the Bible teaches about the state
of the dead. The dead know nothing; they sleep until the resurrection. Any
spirit claiming to be a deceased person is a deceiving spirit.

**2. Test everything by "the law and the testimony."** Does the teaching uphold
all of God's commandments, including the Sabbath? Does it harmonize with the
whole counsel of Scripture? If not, reject it, no matter how impressive the
accompanying signs.

**3. Do not be moved by miracles.** Supernatural power is not proof of divine
origin. Satan can work miracles. Our standard is God's Word, not the
spectacular.

**4. Know how Christ will return.** His coming will be:

- Visible to all: _"Every eye shall see him"_ (Revelation 1:7)
- Audible: With a shout, the voice of the archangel, and the trumpet of God (1 Thessalonians 4:16)
- Glorious: _"As the lightning cometh out of the east, and shineth even unto the west"_ (Matthew 24:27)
- Preceded by the resurrection of the righteous dead (1 Thessalonians 4:16)

Any "Christ" who appears locally, walks among people, or whose coming does not
match this description is an impostor.

**5. Walk daily with God.** Those who live in fellowship with Christ, who study
His Word and commune with Him in prayer, will recognize the voice of the
Stranger. Jesus said, _"My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow
me"_ (John 10:27).

[Q] **"What if spiritualism is presented in a Christian context, with talk of
Jesus and heaven?"**

Satan is a master of disguise. He will use Christian language and appeal to
religious sentiment. But his teachings will always, somewhere, contradict
Scripture. He may speak of love while undermining God's law. He may promise
peace while denying Christ's true work in the sanctuary. Test every spirit. If
it contradicts one clear teaching of Scripture, it is not from God.
