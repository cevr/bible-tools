## Core Truths

### The Commandment's Reach

The ninth commandment forbids more than outright perjury. It guards the sacred ground of human reputation and relationships:

> _"Thou shalt not bear false witness against thy neighbor."_ (Exodus 20:16)

Every slander, half-truth, exaggeration, and malicious insinuation falls under this divine prohibition. Our neighbor's good name is a sacred trust, and God holds us accountable for how we speak of others.

### The Tongue's Terrible Power

James presents a sobering paradox about human speech:

> _"Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not so to be."_ (James 3:10)

The same tongue that praises God on Sabbath morning can wound a brother by Sabbath afternoon. This inconsistency reveals a heart problem that mere self-discipline cannot cure:

> _"The tongue can no man tame; it is an unruly evil, full of deadly poison."_ (James 3:7, 8)

[Q] **"If no man can tame the tongue, are we then helpless? Is victory impossible?"**

Notice carefully: James says "no _man_ can tame" the tongue. Human willpower, education, and self-control are insufficient. But what is impossible with man is possible with God (Luke 18:27). The very impossibility of self-reformation points us to our need of a Savior who can do in us what we cannot do for ourselves.

### The Heart Connection
