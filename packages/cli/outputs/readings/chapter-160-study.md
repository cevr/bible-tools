## Core Truths

### What Hypocrisy Is

**Hypocrisy**: The practice of professing beliefs, feelings, or virtues that one
does not actually hold or practice; religious pretense (Matthew 23:28).

> _"Beware ye of the leaven of the Pharisees, which is hypocrisy."_ (Luke 12:1)

Christ called hypocrisy "leaven" for a profound reason. Leaven works silently,
invisibly, and pervasivelyâ€”a tiny amount eventually affects the entire lump of
dough. So hypocrisy, once admitted into the heart, gradually corrupts the entire
character and spreads to influence others.

### The Heart of the Problem

> _"This people draweth nigh unto Me with their mouth, and honoreth Me with
> their lips; but their heart is far from Me."_ (Matthew 15:7, 8)

The essence of hypocrisy is **disconnection between profession and heart**. The
Pharisees said the right words, performed the right rituals, and appeared

This diagnosis reveals something crucial: **God examines the heart, not merely
the outward appearance** (1 Samuel 16:7). A religion that satisfies human
observers may be utterly worthless before the Searcher of hearts.

### How Hypocrisy Manifests

**1. Modifying God's Commands for Self-Interest**

> _"Thus have ye made the commandment of God of none effect by your tradition."_
> (Matthew 15:6)

The Pharisees invented a loophole called "Corban" (Mark 7:11)â€”a person could
declare their money "devoted to God" and thereby excuse themselves from
supporting aging parents, while still using that money for their own purposes.
They honored God's law with their lips but evaded its claims upon their hearts
and wallets.

[Q] **"But weren't the Pharisees sincere in their traditions?"**

Some traditions began with good intentions, but the problem arose when human
customs were elevated above God's clear commands. Sincerity in following
tradition does not excuse us when that tradition contradicts Scripture. Isaiah's
warning still applies: _"In vain they do worship Me, teaching for doctrines the
commandments of men"_ (Matthew 15:9). True worship submits human reasoning to
divine revelation, not the reverse.

**2. Religious Performance for Human Applause**

> _"They love to pray standing in the synagogues and in the corners of the
> streets, that they may be seen of men."_ (Matthew 6:5)

The hypocrite's prayers are directed horizontallyâ€”toward human observersâ€”rather
than vertically toward God. Jesus said such people "have their reward": the
fleeting admiration of men, which is all they sought and all they will receive.

**3. Judging Others While Ignoring Personal Sin**

> _"Thou hypocrite, first cast out the beam out of thine own eye."_ (Matthew

## Deeper Truths

### Hypocrisy's Destructive Power

> _"A hypocrite with his mouth destroyeth his neighbor."_ (Proverbs 11:9)

Hypocrisy is not a victimless sin. The pretender's inconsistency causes others
to stumble. How many have turned from Christianity because they witnessed
professed believers living contrary to their profession? The hypocrite becomes a
stumbling block, causing the weak to fall and giving unbelievers excuse to
reject the gospel.

### Even Apostles Were Not Immune

> was carried away with their dissimulation."_ (Galatians 2:13)

Paul withstood Peter "to the face, because he was to be blamed" (Galatians
2:11). This teaches us that hypocrisy must be confronted even in respected
leaders, and that no one's spiritual reputation makes them immune to this sin.

The note in our original reading is important: **Ignorance is not hypocrisy. One
can only be a hypocrite when acting something he knows he is not.** Peter knew
betterâ€”he had received the vision at Joppa and witnessed the Spirit fall on
Cornelius's household (Acts 10). His withdrawal was not ignorance but

### The Hypocrite's Doomed Hope

> _"So are the paths of all that forget God; and the hypocrite's hope shall
> perish."_ (Job 8:13)

The hypocrite builds on sand. Whatever spiritual confidence he possesses is
false security. In the judgment, when all masks are removed and all pretense
stripped away, the hypocrite's hope will vanish like morning mist.

[Q] **"Isn't this too harsh? Doesn't everyone struggle with consistency?"**

There is a difference between the sincere believer who struggles with weakness
yet honestly desires to grow, and the hypocrite who deliberately maintains a
false image while having no intention of genuine transformation. God is patient
with struggling saints (Psalm 103:13-14) but will not be mocked by those who use
religion as a cloak for sin (Galatians 6:7). The question is not whether we have
arrived at perfection, but whether our hearts are honestly set toward God.

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### Hypocrisy in the Great Controversy

In the cosmic conflict, Satan himself is the original hypocrite. He appeared as
a loyal angel while harboring rebellion; he posed as concerned for the universe
while seeking his own exaltation. His masterwork is creating a counterfeit
religionâ€”one that has the form of godliness while denying its power (2 Timothy
3:5).

The Pharisees, though claiming to honor God, had actually become agents of the
enemy. Their religion preserved external forms while evacuating spiritual power.
They tithed mint and anise while neglecting justice, mercy, and faith (Matthew
23:23). This pattern appears throughout history wherever form replaces power and
ritual replaces relationship.

### Sanctuary Light on Hypocrisy

In the earthly sanctuary, worshippers brought sacrifices to the outer courtâ€”but
the sacrifice was worthless if offered without genuine contrition. The brazen
altar demanded the death of a substitute, representing surrender of the sinful
self.

The hypocrite offers the outward sacrifice while withholding the heart. But God
has always desired truth in the inward parts (Psalm 51:6). The sanctuary service
taught that **outward ritual without inward reality is meaningless**.

In the Most Holy Place ministry now being conducted in heaven, Christ our High
Priest searches the heart. The investigative judgment is a revelation of who has
genuinely surrendered to Christ and who has merely professed His name. _"The
Lord seeth not as man seeth; for man looketh on the outward appearance, but the
Lord looketh on the heart"_ (1 Samuel 16:7).

### The Cure: Righteousness by Faith

The only remedy for hypocrisy is **genuine heart transformation through faith in
Christ**. The Pharisees attempted righteousness through external conformityâ€”and
produced only elaborate pretense. True righteousness comes when Christ's life is
imparted to the believer, creating a new heart that loves what God loves and
hates what God hates (Ezekiel 36:26-27).

[ILL] A wax apple may look identical to a real apple on the outsideâ€”perhaps even
more perfect in appearance. But cut it open, and there is no flesh, no juice, no
seeds, no life. External religion can mimic genuine Christianity in appearance,
but it lacks the living sap of Christ's indwelling presence. Righteousness by
faith produces fruit from within; hypocrisy merely paints fruit on the outside.

## Practical Application

### David's Resolution

> _"I have not sat with vain persons, neither will I go in with dissemblers."_
> (Psalm 26:4)

David understood that hypocrisy is contagious. He chose his companions
carefully, avoiding those whose lives were performances rather than genuine
devotion. We must likewise be discerning about whose influence we invite into
our lives.

### The Character of True Wisdom

> _"But the wisdom that is from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, and
> easy to be entreated, full of mercy and good fruits, without partiality, and
> without hypocrisy."_ (James 3:17)

Notice the progression: **first pure**â€”genuine wisdom begins with internal
reality, not external appearance. Such wisdom is "without hypocrisy"â€”it is the
same in private as in public, the same in thought as in word.

### Practical Steps Away from Hypocrisy

1. **Pray for self-knowledge**: Ask God to reveal any areas where your
   profession exceeds your practice (Psalm 139:23-24)

2. **Cultivate private devotion**: The cure for performance-based religion is a
   genuine secret life with God (Matthew 6:6)

3. **Welcome correction**: The hypocrite resents rebuke; the honest heart
   receives it as a gift (Proverbs 9:8-9)

4. **Confess inconsistencies honestly**: Rather than maintaining the mask, admit
   struggles to God and trusted believers (James 5:16)

5. **Depend on Christ for transformation**: Stop trying to manufacture
   righteousness and receive it by faith (Philippians 3:9)

### The Solemn Warning

> _"And shall cut him asunder, and appoint him his portion with the hypocrites:
> there shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth."_ (Matthew 24:51)

This warning comes in the context of Christ's return. The unfaithful servant
professed to believe in his master's coming but lived as though that coming
would never occur. His destiny is "with the hypocrites"â€”that is, with those
whose profession contradicted their practice.

[Q] **"How can I know if I'm being a hypocrite without realizing it?"**

Ask yourself these questions: Do I pray as earnestly in private as I appear to
in public? Does my behavior change when no one is watching? Do I excuse in
myself what I condemn in others? Is my primary concern what God thinks, or what
people think? Am I depending on my religious performances, or on Christ's
merits? Honest answers to these questions, sought in prayer, will reveal the
heart's true condition.
