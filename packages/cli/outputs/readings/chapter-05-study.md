### Christ Expounded in All the Scriptures

## Deeper Truths

### The Scarlet Thread from Eden to Eternity

The first promise of redemption appears immediately after sin entered:

> _"And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel."_ (Genesis 3:15)

Here, in Eden's shadow, God promised that the "seed of the woman" would crush the serpent's head, though suffering a wound in the process. This is the _protevangelium_â€”the first gospel. Every subsequent revelation of Christ builds upon this foundation.

**The Promise to Abraham:**

> _"In thy seed shall all the nations of the earth be blessed."_ (Genesis 22:18)

Paul explains who this "seed" is:

> _"Now to Abraham and his seed were the promises made. He saith not, And to seeds, as of many; but as of one, And to thy seed, which is Christ."_ (Galatians 3:16)

### Prophecies of Christ's Life, Death, and Resurrection

The Old Testament provides astonishing detail about the Messiah's life:

**His Birthplace:**

> _"But thou, Bethlehem Ephratah, though thou be little among the thousands of Judah, yet out of thee shall He come forth unto Me that is to be Ruler in Israel; whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting."_ (Micah 5:2)

**The Price of Betrayal:**

**His Dying Words:**

These very words fell from Jesus' lips as He hung upon the cross (Matthew 27:46; Luke 23:46). The Psalms recorded Christ's agony before it occurred.

**His Resurrection:**

> _"For Thou wilt not leave My soul in hell; neither wilt Thou suffer Thine Holy One to see corruption."_ (Psalm 16:10)

### Christ's Kingdom and Second Coming

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### Christ as the Center of the Great Controversy

The battle between Christ and Satan began before this earth existed, but it has always centered on the Person and work of Christ. Satan's strategy throughout history has been to obscure, distort, or deny the truth about the Redeemer. When Scripture testifies of Christâ€”His deity, His sacrifice, His righteousness, His ministryâ€”it exposes Satan's lies and reveals the character of God.

The serpent's head was bruised at Calvary (Genesis 3:15), yet the full crushing awaits the final judgment. Every Old Testament type and prophecy pointed forward to the cross; every New Testament teaching points back to it and forward to His coming kingdom.

### Christ in the Sanctuary

The entire sanctuary service testified of Christ:

- The **sacrificial lamb** represented _"the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world"_ (John 1:29)
- The **high priest** foreshadowed Christ, our _"great high priest, that is passed into the heavens"_ (Hebrews 4:14)
- The **bread** on the table symbolized Christ, the _"bread of life"_ (John 6:35)
- The **light** of the candlestick represented Christ, _"the light of the world"_ (John 8:12)
- The **incense** ascending pictured Christ's intercession (Hebrews 7:25; Revelation 8:3-4)
- The **Most Holy Place** pointed to Christ's final work of atonement in the heavenly sanctuary (Hebrews 9:11-12, 24)

### Righteousness by Faith Throughout Scripture

From Genesis to Revelation, salvation has always been by grace through faith in the promised Redeemer:

- **Abel** offered a lamb _by faith_ (Hebrews 11:4; Genesis 4:4)
- **Abraham** _"believed God, and it was counted unto him for righteousness"_ (Romans 4:3; Genesis 15:6)
- **Moses** esteemed _"the reproach of Christ greater riches than the treasures in Egypt"_ (Hebrews 11:26)

The Old Testament saints were saved by looking _forward_ to Christ; we are saved by looking _back_ to the same Saviour. The method has always been faith, and the object has always been Christ.
