## Core Truths

### The Certainty of Christ's Return

The promise of Christ's second coming rests not on speculation but on the word of our Saviour Himself:

> _"In My Father's house are many mansions: if it were not so, I would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you. And if I go and prepare a place for you, I will come again, and receive you unto Myself; that where I am, there ye may be also."_ (John 14:2, 3)

This is not a vague hope but a definite appointment. Jesus spoke these words to comfort hearts that would soon be pierced with grief at His crucifixion. The same lips that said, "It is finished" on Calvary, declared, "I will come again." Both promises are equally certain.

**Justification**: God's legal declaration that a repentant sinner is righteous because of Christ's merits (Romans 3:24; 5:1).

### The Disciples' Questions

When Christ foretold the destruction of Jerusalem's magnificent templeâ€”so glorious that its stones gleamed white and gold in the sunlightâ€”the disciples were astonished. Privately, they asked Him a threefold question:

> _"Tell us, when shall these things be? and what shall be the sign of Thy coming, and of the end of the world?"_ (Matthew 24:3)

In His answer, Jesus blended prophecies concerning:

1. **The destruction of Jerusalem** (fulfilled in A.D. 70)
2. **The signs preceding His second advent**
3. **The end of the world**

This interweaving is characteristic of Bible prophecy, where near and distant events often appear together, like mountain peaks seen from a distance that seem close together but are actually separated by great valleys.

### The "Not Yet" of History

Jesus warned against premature excitement:

> _"Ye shall hear of wars and rumours of wars: see that ye be not troubled: for all these things must come to pass, but the end is not yet."_ (Matthew 24:6)

### The Abomination of Desolation and the Great Tribulation

Jesus directed His hearers to Daniel's prophecy:

> _"When ye therefore shall see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, stand in the holy place, (whoso readeth, let him understand)."_ (Matthew 24:15)

Luke's parallel passage clarifies this: "When ye shall see Jerusalem compassed with armies, then know that the desolation thereof is nigh" (Luke 21:20). This was fulfilled when Roman armies surrounded Jerusalem in A.D. 70. Christians who heeded Christ's warning fled to Pella and were preserved.

But the "great tribulation" that followed extended far beyond Jerusalem:

> _"For then shall be great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be."_ (Matthew 24:21)

### The 1,260 Years of Papal Supremacy

Daniel's prophecy foretold that a persecuting power would "wear out the saints of the Most High" for "a time and times and the dividing of time" (Daniel 7:25). Revelation 12:6 interprets this as 1,260 days. Applying the prophetic principle of "each day for a year" (Ezekiel 4:6), we arrive at 1,260 literal years.

**Historicist interpretation**: The method of prophetic interpretation that sees prophecy fulfilled progressively through history, rather than entirely in the past (preterism) or entirely in the future (futurism).

This period of papal supremacy began in A.D. 538, when the last of the three Arian kingdoms opposing Rome fell, and the emperor Justinian's decree establishing the pope as "head of all the holy churches" could be fully enforced. It ended in 1798, when General Berthier of Napoleon's army took Pope Pius VI captive, and the papal government received a "deadly wound" (Revelation 13:3).

## The Signs in the Heavens

Jesus declared that signs in the sun, moon, and stars would mark the approach of His coming:

> _"Immediately after the tribulation of those days shall the sun be darkened, and the moon shall not give her light, and the stars shall fall from heaven."_ (Matthew 24:29)

Mark's account specifies: "In those days, _after_ that tribulation" (Mark 13:24). This means the signs would occur within the 1,260-year period (before 1798), but after the persecution had ceased (after 1773). Let us examine the historical record.

Between 1773 and 1798, the sun was indeed darkened in a most remarkable manner:

God distributes His signs according to His wisdom. The Old World witnessed the rise and fall of prophetic kingdoms, the papal captivity of 1798, and the gathering of nations for Armageddon. The New World witnessed the Dark Day and the falling of the stars. Should those in one hemisphere reject the testimony of those in another? ## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Sanctuary and Christ's Return

**Atonement**: The work by which God and man are brought into oneness through Christ's sacrifice and priestly ministry (Leviticus 16; Romans 5:10â€“11).

Christ's promise, "I go to prepare a place for you... I will come again," connects His ascension to heaven with His return. What has He been doing during the intervening centuries? The sanctuary message reveals the answer:

- **Outer Court (Cross)**: Christ offered Himself as our sacrifice, providing justification through His blood.
- **Holy Place (Intercession)**: For centuries, Christ has been ministering in the heavenly sanctuary, applying the benefits of His sacrifice to penitent believers.
- **Most Holy Place (Judgment)**: Since 1844, at the close of the 2,300-day prophecy of Daniel 8:14, Christ has been engaged in the investigative judgmentâ€”the work of determining who, among the professed people of God, are truly His.

The signs in the heavens (1780, 1833) came just before the transition to this final phase of Christ's ministry. They were heaven's announcement that the end was drawing near.

### The Great Controversy in the Signs

Satan has worked throughout history to obscure the meaning of these signs. Naturalistic explanations are offered for the Dark Day; the star-fall is dismissed as a meteor shower (which it was, but prophetically significant). Yet for those with eyes to see, the evidence is overwhelming. God spoke through nature at the time prophecy specified.

[ILL] A man sets his watch by a certain clock, trusting it to be accurate. When the clock strikes the hour, he acts accordingly. So God has given us a prophetic clock. When the sun darkened in 1780, precisely within the window Scripture specified, it was as though heaven's clock struck the hour. We may ignore it, but we cannot deny it.

### Righteousness by Faith and Readiness

The moral condition of the world at Christ's coming will mirror the days of Noah:

> _"As the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be. For as in the days that were before the flood they were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark."_ (Matthew 24:37, 38)

There is nothing sinful in eating, drinking, or marrying. The sin was in being so absorbed in these things that the people ignored Noah's warning. They "knew not until the flood came, and took them all away" (Matthew 24:39).

True readiness for Christ's coming is not mere intellectual assent to prophetic truth. It is a heart transformed by graceâ€”Christ's righteousness both imputed (covering our past sins) and imparted (changing our present character). > _"Therefore be ye also ready: for in such an hour as ye think not the Son of man cometh."_ (Matthew 24:44)

[Q] **"If we cannot know the day and hour, why study the signs?"**

Jesus said we cannot know the "day and hour" (Matthew 24:36), but He also said, "When ye shall see all these things, know that it is near, even at the doors" (Matthew 24:33).
