# Bible Study: The Perpetuity of the Law

## Core Truths

### The Law as the Standard of Judgment

Every human being must one day stand before the bar of heaven:

> _"For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ; that every one may receive the things done in his body, according to that he hath done, whether it be good or bad."_ (2 Corinthians 5:10)

But by what standard will God judge? James answers plainly:

> _"So speak ye, and so do, as they that shall be judged by the law of liberty."_ (James 2:12)

> _"Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God."_ (Romans 3:19)

### Universal Sinfulness Exposed

Scripture leaves no room for human pride:

> _"For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God."_ (Romans 3:23)

> _"What then? are we better than they? No, in no wise: for we have before proved both Jews and Gentiles, that they are all under sin."_ (Romans 3:9)

The law is God's mirrorâ€”it shows us our true condition. Without it, we would not know what sin is (Romans 7:7). The law does not save, but it reveals our desperate need for a Saviour.

## Deeper Truths

### Faith Establishes the Law

[Q] **"Doesn't faith in Christ abolish the law? Aren't Christians 'free from the law'?"**

Paul anticipated this question and answered it emphatically:

> _"Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law."_ (Romans 3:31)

By providing the only means by which fallen humanity can be brought into harmony with that law through Christ's righteousness.

The converted heart delights in God's standard:

> _"For I delight in the law of God after the inward man."_ (Romans 7:22)

But the unconverted, carnal mind is fundamentally opposed:

> _"Because the carnal mind is enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be."_ (Romans 8:7)

Here is the great dividing line: the carnal heart rebels against God's law; the renewed heart loves it. This transformationâ€”from rebellion to delightâ€”is the very work of the new covenant, where God promises: _"I will put my laws into their mind, and write them in their hearts"_ (Hebrews 8:10).

When asked which commandment was greatest, Jesus summarized the entire law in two principles:

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### The Law, the Sanctuary, and the Final Conflict

The law of God is central to the sanctuary message. In the earthly tabernacle, the Ten Commandments rested within the ark of the covenant in the Most Holy Placeâ€”beneath the mercy seat, where the blood of atonement was sprinkled. This arrangement reveals a profound truth: **mercy and law are not enemies; they meet together in Christ.**

In the heavenly sanctuary, John beheld the same reality:

> _"And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament."_ (Revelation 11:19)

The law remains in the very throne room of the universe. It is the foundation of God's government, the standard of the investigative judgment now proceeding, and the focal point of Satan's final assault.

### The Remnant and the Commandments

Revelation describes the last-day people of God:

> _"And the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ."_ (Revelation 12:17)

Notice what provokes Satan's wrath: **commandment-keeping faith.** The remnant church does not earn salvation by obedience, but their faith is demonstrated by obedience. They have received Christ's righteousness by faith, and that faith works by love (Galatians 5:6), producing the fruit of obedience.

[Q] **"Isn't this salvation by works? Isn't emphasizing commandment-keeping legalism?"**

Consider carefully: Scripture never separates saving faith from obedience. True faith always produces corresponding works (James 2:17-26). The question is not whether we will obey, but _how_ and _why_:

- **Legalism** is self-effort to earn God's favour by outward compliance.
- **Righteousness by faith** is Christ living in the believer, producing from within what the law requires without.

> _"For this is the love of God, that we keep his commandments: and his commandments are not grievous."_ (1 John 5:3)

To the heart renewed by grace, obedience is not a burden but a delightâ€”the natural outflow of love for a loving God.

## Practical Application

### How Do We Know We Truly Know God?

John provides a sobering test:

> _"He that saith, I know him, and keepeth not his commandments, is a liar, and the truth is not in him."_ (1 John 2:4)

Strong words! But they are words of love, warning us against self-deception. A profession of faith without corresponding obedience is worthlessâ€”not because obedience saves, but because genuine faith inevitably produces it.

### How Do We Know We Love God?

> _"By this we know that we love the children of God, when we love God, and keep his commandments."_ (1 John 5:2)

Love is not merely sentiment; it is action. We demonstrate love for God by keeping His commandments; we demonstrate love for the brethren by the same.

### How Do We Experience Blessing?

> _"But whoso looketh into the perfect law of liberty, and continueth therein, he being not a forgetful hearer, but a doer of the work, this man shall be blessed in his deed."_ (James 1:25)

The blessing is not in hearing alone, but in doing. The law is a mirrorâ€”we must look, see our need, and then _continue_ in the transforming grace of Christ until His character is reflected in us.
