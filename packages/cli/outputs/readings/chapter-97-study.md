### God's Laws Protected Against Oppression

### The Sabbath: A Memorial of Liberty

> "And ye shall hallow the fiftieth year, and proclaim liberty throughout all the land unto all the inhabitants thereof: it shall be a jubilee unto you." (Leviticus 25:10)

Every fifty years, at the sound of the trumpet on the Day of Atonement, all Hebrew slaves were freed and all ancestral lands returned to their original families. ## Deeper Truths

### When Liberty Is Rejected, Judgment Comes

Israel failed to observe the jubilee and Sabbath releases. They became oppressors:

> "Behold, in the day of your fast ye find pleasure, and exact all your labours. Behold, ye fast for strife and debate." (Isaiah 58:3-4)

They maintained religious forms while practicing economic oppression. God's response was severe:

> "Ye have not hearkened unto Me, in proclaiming liberty, everyone to his brother... behold, I proclaim a liberty for you, saith the Lord, to the sword, to the pestilence, and to the famine." (Jeremiah 34:17)

[Q] **"Why would God punish Israel so severely for economic practices?"**

Because oppression contradicts God's character. A people claiming to worship the God of Exodus while grinding the faces of the poor bear false witness against Him. They misrepresent the Liberator as an oppressor. Such hypocrisy brings judgment not only because it harms others, but because it blasphemes God's name before watching nations.

### True Religion: Breaking Every Yoke

What does God actually desire from His people?

> "Is not this the fast that I have chosen? to loose the bands of wickedness, to undo the heavy burdens, and to let the oppressed go free, and that ye break every yoke?" (Isaiah 58:6)

True worship is inseparable from justice. The fast acceptable to God involves:

- Loosing bands of wickedness
- Undoing heavy burdens
- Setting the oppressed free
- Breaking every yoke
- Feeding the hungry
- Housing the poor
- Clothing the naked

Religion that ignores human suffering is not true religion, whatever its theological correctness.

### Christ: The Ultimate Liberator

When Jesus announced His ministry in Nazareth, He read from Isaiah:

> "The Spirit of the Lord is upon Me, because He hath anointed Me to preach the gospel to the poor; He hath sent Me to heal the brokenhearted, to preach deliverance to the captives, and recovering of sight to the blind, to set at liberty them that are bruised." (Luke 4:18)

Then He declared: "This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears" (Luke 4:21). Jesus is the fulfillment of every jubilee, every Sabbath release, every deliverance from Egypt. He is the Author of liberty.
