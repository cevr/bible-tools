# Reading 31: The Obedience of Faith

## Introduction

True faith and genuine obedience are not opposing forces but inseparable companions in the Christian life. Through the life of Abrahamâ€”the father of the faithfulâ€”Scripture reveals that saving faith always produces willing obedience, and that obedience flows not from human effort but from a living trust in God. This study explores how faith works by love to bring the believer into complete harmony with God's will.

## Core Truths

### Faith That Obeys

When God called Abraham to leave everything familiar and journey to an unknown land, the patriarch responded with simple, trusting obedience:

> _"Now the Lord had said unto Abram, Get thee out of thy country, and from thy kindred, and from thy father's house, unto a land that I will shew thee."_ (Genesis 12:1)

> _"So Abram departed, as the Lord had spoken unto him."_ (Genesis 12:4)

What enabled such radical obedience? Scripture gives us the answer:

> _"By faith Abraham, when he was called, obeyed to go out unto a place which he was to receive for an inheritance; and he went out, not knowing whither he went."_ (Hebrews 11:8, R.V.)

Notice the beautiful simplicity: **"By faith... obeyed."** Abraham did not know the destination; he knew the One who called him. His obedience was not blind duty but intelligent trust.

### The Supreme Test

Years later, God gave Abraham an even more difficult command:

> _"Take now thy son, thine only son Isaac, whom thou lovest, and get thee into the land of Moriah; and offer him there for a burnt offering."_ (Genesis 22:2)

Isaac was not merely Abraham's beloved sonâ€”he was the child of promise, the one through whom God had said all nations would be blessed. Humanly speaking, this command seemed to contradict everything God had promised.

Yet Abraham obeyed:

> _"By faith Abraham when he was tried, offered up Isaac: and he that had received the promises offered up his only begotten son."_ (Hebrews 11:17)

**Faith**: The conviction that God's character is trustworthy even when His commands seem impossible to understand (Hebrews 11:1).

So Abraham leaped into the unknown, not because he understood God's plan, but because he knew God's heart.

## Deeper Truths

### How Faith and Works Relate

James asks a penetrating question:

> _"Was not Abraham our father justified by works, when he had offered Isaac his son upon the altar? Seest thou how faith wrought with his works, and by works was faith made perfect?"_ (James 2:21â€“22)

This passage has confused many, but the truth is beautifully simple:

- **Faith** is the root
- **Works** are the fruit
- The fruit reveals the life in the root

James does not teach salvation by works. He teaches that genuine faith **produces** works as naturally as a living tree produces fruit. Abraham's willingness to offer Isaac did not earn him righteousnessâ€”it **demonstrated** the living faith already present in his heart.

A faith that produces no obedience is not saving faithâ€”it is mere intellectual assent, the kind even demons possess (James 2:19). The two apostles do not contradict; they complement. Faith alone justifies, but the faith that justifies is never aloneâ€”it always works by love.

### The Nature of Genuine Faith

Paul gives us the defining characteristic of saving faith:

> _"In Jesus Christ neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision; but faith which worketh by love."_ (Galatians 5:6)

**Faith which worketh by love**â€”this is the essence of true religion. Genuine faith:

- **Trusts** God's promises
- **Loves** the One who promises
- **Obeys** because love delights in the will of the Beloved

This is not legal obedience driven by fear of punishment or hope of reward. It is the spontaneous response of a heart captivated by divine love.

### Grace Designed for Obedience

Some imagine that grace excuses disobedience. But Paul reveals grace's true purpose:

## Principles and Great Controversy Themes

### God Values Heart-Obedience Above All

Through Samuel, God declared a principle that echoes throughout Scripture:

> _"Hath the Lord as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, as in obeying the voice of the Lord? Behold, to obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams."_ (1 Samuel 15:22)

This truth strikes at the heart of all false religion. Fallen humanity constantly seeks to substitute external forms for internal surrender. We offer God our activities, our rituals, our religious performancesâ€”anything except what He actually asks: the obedience of a loving heart.

[Q] **"Doesn't this contradict righteousness by faith? Isn't emphasizing obedience legalism?"**

Not at all. Legalism is the attempt to earn salvation through obedience. Righteousness by faith is receiving Christ's righteousness freely, then allowing that same Christ to live His obedient life within us. The difference is not between faith and obedienceâ€”it is between human effort and divine enabling. Christ does not save us _in_ our sins but _from_ our sins (Matthew 1:21). The faith that receives His pardon also receives His power for holy living.

### Christ Our Example and Enabler

Jesus Himself demonstrates perfect obedience:

> _"And being found in fashion as a man, He humbled Himself, and became obedient unto death, even the death of the cross."_ (Philippians 2:8)

> _"Though He were a Son, yet learned He obedience by the things which He suffered."_ (Hebrews 5:8)

Christ's obedience was not effortless. He "learned" obedience through suffering. As a man, He faced every temptation we face, yet chose His Father's will at infinite cost. And now:

> _"Being made perfect, He became the Author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey Him."_ (Hebrews 5:9)

**Author**: The Greek (aitios) suggests both source and cause. Christ is both the origin and the ongoing power of our salvation. His obedience is credited to us (justification) and reproduced in us (sanctification).

[ILL] A skilled musician does not simply perform a piece and leave the student to imitate. She takes the student's hands, guides the fingers, demonstrates the pressure and timing until, gradually, the music flows through the student's own hands. Christ does not merely show us obedience from afarâ€”He enters our lives by His Spirit and produces His obedience through us. "It is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of His good pleasure" (Philippians 2:13).

### The Completeness of Surrender

How thorough must our obedience become?

> _"Casting down imaginations, and every high thing that exalteth itself against the knowledge of God, and bringing into captivity every thought to the obedience of Christ."_ (2 Corinthians 10:5)

**Every thought**â€”not merely outward actions, but the inner life of the mind. This is the standard God's grace makes possible. The work is not merely behavioral modification but heart transformation, reaching to the very springs of thought.

This stands in sharp contrast to the religion of the Pharisees:

> _"Full well ye reject the commandment of God, that ye may keep your own tradition."_ (Mark 7:9)

The Pharisees were outwardly religious but inwardly rebellious. They substituted human tradition for divine commandmentâ€”appearing obedient while actually rejecting God's authority. True faith obeys God's word, not human substitutes.

## Practical Application

### What the Obedience of Faith Looks Like Daily

**1. It begins with surrender, not effort.**
Before we can obey, we must yield. Each morning, present yourself to God: "Lord, I cannot obey in my own strength. Live Your life through me today."

**2. It trusts when it cannot see.**
Abraham "went out, not knowing whither he went." We may not understand every providence, every trial, every closed door. Faith obeys anyway, trusting the character of the One who leads.

**3. It values God's commands above human traditions.**
Are there areas where convenience, culture, or personal preference override clear biblical instruction? The obedience of faith chooses God's word over comfortable compromise.

**4. It extends to the thought life.**
Victory is not merely refraining from sinful actions but bringing "every thought" into captivity. This requires moment-by-moment dependence on the indwelling Christ.

**5. It produces love for others.**
The ultimate evidence of genuine faith-obedience is transformation of character:

> _"Seeing ye have purified your souls in obeying the truth through the Spirit unto unfeigned love of the brethren, see that ye love one another with a pure heart fervently."_ (1 Peter 1:22)

Obedience to truth produces genuine love. If our religion makes us harsh, critical, or unloving, something is fundamentally wrong with our faith.

### The Consequences of Disobedience

Scripture speaks solemnly of those who refuse the obedience of faith:

> _"The Lord Jesus shall be revealed from heaven with His mighty angels, in flaming fire taking vengeance on them that know not God, and that obey not the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ."_ (2 Thessalonians 1:7â€“8)

To "obey the gospel" means to respond to its invitationâ€”repenting, believing, surrendering, following. Those who persistently refuse this invitation choose their own destiny.
