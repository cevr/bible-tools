### The First Persecution: Cain and Abel

The Bible's first murder reveals the deepest root of all persecution:

## Practical Application

### For Those Who May Face Persecution

1. **Expect it**: "All that will live godly in Christ Jesus shall suffer
   persecution" (2 Timothy 3:12). Do not be surprised when it comes.

2. **Examine your response**: When facing opposition, ask whether your reaction
   reflects Christ's spirit or Cain's. Do you return blessing for cursing?
   (Romans 12:14)

3. **Remember the promise**: "Blessed are they which are persecuted for
   righteousness' sake: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven" (Matthew 5:10).
   Heaven awaits.

4. **Rejoice**: "Rejoice, and be exceeding glad: for great is your reward in
   heaven: for so persecuted they the prophets which were before you" (Matthew
   5:12).

### For All Believers

1. **Guard against the spirit of persecution in yourself**: It is possible to
   hold right doctrine while cherishing the wrong spirit. Are you willing to
   force others to believe as you do?

2. **Support religious liberty**: Oppose every union of church and state, even
   when the church is your own. Freedom of conscience is a gift from God that
   no earthly power may rightfully revoke.

3. **Pray for the persecuted**: Believers around the world today suffer for
   their faith. "Remember them that are in bonds, as bound with them" (Hebrews
   13:3).

4. **Prepare your heart**: The prophecies indicate that persecution will
   intensify before Christ's return. Now is the time to establish such a
   connection with Christ that you can stand when tested.
