# 2026 Q1 W9 - February 21–27

## Reconciliation and Hope

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** God's reconciliation of humanity through Christ's death, the mystery revealed, and growth to maturity in Christ.
**Present Truth Theme:** Christ's Righteousness — Imputed AND Imparted — The Sanctuary's Full Gospel
**Central Focus:** Understanding HOW reconciliation actually works in us — not merely forgiveness, but Christ dwelling within, reproducing His character by faith
**Key Texts:** 2 Corinthians 5:21; Colossians 1:27; Galatians 2:20

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Have you ever tried to fix a broken relationship by sheer willpower? You determined to be patient, to respond differently, to _make_ yourself loving — only to fail repeatedly? Why does this happen?
- Lesson Connection: This week's lesson addresses reconciliation — God bringing us back to Himself through Christ's death. But the lesson reveals something far deeper than a legal transaction.
- Present Truth Bridge: When we view reconciliation through the sanctuary lens, we see the FULL gospel — not just pardon (what happened at the altar), but _Christ in you_ (what continues in the Holy Place and Most Holy Place). The mystery Paul unveils is NOT merely forgiveness — it is "Christ in you, the hope of glory" (Colossians 1:27).
  - [WB]: Write: 'RECONCILIATION = Christ IN You'
- Why This Matters Now: Christ our High Priest is ministering NOW in the Most Holy Place — not merely to cover sin, but to REMOVE it by reproducing His character in us. This is the practical engine of transformation we desperately need.
- Roadmap: We will explore (1) What we were apart from Christ, (2) How Christ's righteousness becomes ours — both imputed AND imparted, and (3) What this looks like in daily life.

### II. The Problem: Alienated and Helpless — Why Self-Effort Fails (10-12 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Before we can appreciate the gospel's power, we must understand our true condition apart from Christ — and why human effort can NEVER solve it.

#### A. The Dark Picture — Our Condition Apart from Christ (5-6 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Colossians 1:21
  - Reading: "And you, who once were alienated and enemies in your mind by wicked works..."
  - [WB]: "ALIENATED / ENEMIES / WICKED WORKS"
  - Unpacking the Truth: Notice the location of enmity — "in your mind." Sin is not merely wrong actions; it is a broken nature, a heart at war with God. Our very _mind_ — our desires, inclinations, motivations — is hostile to God.
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* 'According to this verse, where does our hostility to God reside? What does this tell us about how deep the problem goes?'
- **Foundation Text 2:** Romans 5:6
  - Reading: "For when we were still without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly."
  - [WB]: "WITHOUT STRENGTH = HELPLESS"
  - The Hopelessness of Self-Effort: "Without strength" — the Greek word means utterly helpless, powerless, impotent. This is not weakness that can be strengthened by trying harder. This is complete inability. We cannot will ourselves into loving God. We cannot discipline ourselves into righteousness.
  - [EGW]: "The best efforts that man in his own strength can make, are valueless to meet the holy and just law that he has transgressed." (GAG 177.2)
  - [WB]: "Best efforts = VALUELESS"
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* "If we are truly 'without strength,' what does this mean for all our New Year's resolutions and promises to God to 'do better'?"
- **Foundation Text 3:** Romans 3:20
  - Reading: "By the law is the knowledge of sin."
  - The Law's Purpose: The law is a mirror — it shows us what God's character looks like, and therefore shows us how far we fall short. But a mirror cannot wash a dirty face.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Imagine a person covered in mud trying to clean themselves by staring longer and harder at a mirror. The more clearly they see the filth, the more desperately they scrub — but with what? Their own muddy hands! This is self-effort at righteousness. The law shows the dirt; only Christ provides the cleansing.
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "Where in your life have you been 'staring at the mirror' — seeing your sin clearly — but trying to clean it with your own effort?"

#### B. God's Initiative — He Seeks the Lost (4-5 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Romans 5:8
  - Reading: "But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."
  - [WB]: "GOD demonstrates... WHILE we were sinners"
  - The Pursuing God: God did not wait for us to clean ourselves up. He did not require us to _become_ righteous before He would act. He came TO the enemy, TO the helpless, TO the one who could offer nothing.
- **Foundation Text 5:** 2 Corinthians 5:18 (NKJV)
  - Reading: "Now all things are of God, who has reconciled us to Himself through Jesus Christ..."
  - [WB]: "ALL of God... THROUGH Christ"
  - [EGW]: "God is always the ultimate Agent and Initiator of the reconciliation process... As John eloquently states, 'We love Him because He first loved us' (1 John 4:19)." (See Teacher's Comments)
  - The Sanctuary Connection: At the altar of sacrifice, God provided the Lamb. We brought nothing but our sin. HE provided the substitution. This is the foundation — Christ's righteousness IMPUTED, credited to our account, apart from any work of ours.
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* 'What difference does it make that God sought you WHILE you were His enemy, rather than waiting for you to seek Him?'

### III. The Mystery Revealed: Christ IN You — Righteousness Imparted (15-17 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Justification — Christ's righteousness credited to us — is glorious! But Paul reveals that reconciliation does not stop at the altar. There is MORE. The mystery hidden for ages is now revealed: Christ DWELLING in the believer, transforming from within.

#### A. The Mystery Unveiled — More Than Forgiveness (6-7 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Colossians 1:26-27 (NKJV)
  - Reading: "The mystery which has been hidden from ages and from generations, but now has been revealed to His saints... which is Christ in you, the hope of glory."
  - [WB]: "THE MYSTERY = CHRIST IN YOU"
  - Unpacking the Truth: What is this "mystery"? Not merely that God forgives sinners — the Old Testament revealed that. The mystery is the _indwelling_ of Christ Himself in Gentiles (and all believers). Christ does not merely forgive from heaven; He takes up residence WITHIN.
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "Why do you think Paul calls this a 'mystery'? What makes Christ dwelling IN us so astounding?"
- **Foundation Text 2:** Ephesians 3:17
  - Reading: "That Christ may dwell in your hearts through faith..."
  - [WB]: Add to diagram: "Through FAITH → HEART"
  - The Means of Indwelling: How does Christ come to dwell in us? "Through faith." Not through effort, not through performance, not through discipline (though these have their place). FAITH is the channel. Faith receives; faith trusts; faith surrenders.
  - [EGW]: "Christ abides in your heart by faith. You are to maintain this connection with Christ by faith and the continual surrender of your will to Him; and so long as you do this, He will work in you to will and to do according to His good pleasure." (SC 62, 63)
  - [WB]: "faith + surrender = HE WORKS IN YOU"
  - The Distinction: This is where self-effort and faith-obedience diverge! Self-effort says, "I must work harder to be like Christ." Faith-obedience says, "Christ, I surrender. YOU work in me. I receive."
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* "What is the difference between 'trying to be like Christ' and 'letting Christ live in you'? How would you explain this to a new believer?"
- **Foundation Text 3:** Galatians 2:20
  - Reading: "I have been crucified with Christ; it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me; and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself for me."
  - [WB]: "I → CRUCIFIED / CHRIST → LIVES"
  - The Faith OF the Son of God: Notice — Paul does not say merely faith _in_ Christ, but "the faith OF the Son of God." This is Christ's own faith — His perfect trust in the Father — now living IN Paul. This is the engine of transformation. It is not my weak faith achieving righteousness; it is Christ's perfect faith operating THROUGH me.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A glove lying on a table cannot pick anything up. It has the form of a hand but no power. But when a hand enters the glove, the glove moves — it picks up, it grips, it works. The power is not IN the glove; it is in the hand WITHIN the glove. So it is with us. We have no power of ourselves. But when Christ enters by faith, HIS life works through ours. We move, we obey, we love — but the power is HIS.
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "Think of a specific struggle — a temper, a habit, a fear. What would it look like to stop 'trying harder' and instead invite Christ to live His victory through you in THAT specific area?"

#### B. The Practical Transformation — What Monday Morning Looks Like (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** 2 Corinthians 5:21
  - Reading: "For He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, that we might become the righteousness of God in Him."
  - [WB]: "HE BECAME SIN → WE BECOME RIGHTEOUSNESS"
  - The Double Exchange: This is the gospel's heart. Not only does Christ take our sin (imputation of guilt to Him), but we BECOME the righteousness of God IN HIM (imputation AND impartation to us). We do not merely _have_ a righteous standing — we progressively _become_ righteous in character.
  - The Sanctuary Unveiling: At the altar, sin is laid on the sacrifice (justification — Christ bears our guilt). In the Holy Place, the priest ministers daily with blood, light, bread, and incense — representing Christ's ongoing work of sanctification in us. In the Most Holy Place, sin is finally removed from the camp — representing complete restoration, the mystery of "Christ in you" fully realized.
  - [WB]: Diagram: Altar (Justification) → Holy Place (Sanctification) → Most Holy Place (Full Restoration)
- **Foundation Text 5:** Colossians 1:22 (NKJV)
  - Reading: "...to present you holy, and blameless, and above reproach in His sight."
  - [WB]: "HOLY / BLAMELESS / ABOVE REPROACH"
  - The Goal of Reconciliation: The purpose of Christ's death is not merely pardon — it is PRESENTATION. God intends to present us holy, blameless, without spot. This is not a legal fiction; it is a reality wrought BY FAITH through Christ's indwelling.
  - [EGW]: "Genuine faith appropriates the righteousness of Christ, and the sinner is made an overcomer with Christ; for he is made a partaker of the divine nature, and thus divinity and humanity are combined." (GAG 177.2)
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "What does it mean to be presented 'without spot'? Is this only about our legal standing, or something more?"
- **Foundation Text 6:** Philippians 2:13
  - Reading: "For it is God who works in you both to will and to do for His good pleasure."
  - The Practical Mechanics: How do I obey? How does the command "love your enemy" become actual? God works in me TO WILL — He changes my desires! And TO DO — He gives power for action! My part is to surrender, to believe, to receive. HIS part is to transform.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Think of a person who hates mornings. They are NOT a "morning person." They can set alarms, drink coffee, lecture themselves — but they still hate mornings. Now imagine that same person falling deeply in love with someone who lives across town, and that person can only meet for breakfast. Suddenly, waking up early is no longer drudgery — it is delight! What changed? Not discipline; DESIRE. This is what Christ does within. He changes our WILL, our desires. Obedience flows from love, not labor.
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "In what area of your life do you sense you've been 'lecturing yourself' into obedience rather than receiving a new heart from Christ? What would surrender look like this week?"
- **Foundation Text 7:** Ephesians 5:27
  - Reading: "That He might present her to Himself a glorious church, not having spot or wrinkle or any such thing, but that she should be holy and without blemish."
  - [WB]: "GLORIOUS CHURCH — NO SPOT"
  - The Corporate and Individual Vision: This is not pie-in-the-sky idealism. Christ's present sanctuary ministry is aimed at THIS outcome. He is working NOW, by faith, in every surrendered soul, to accomplish this very thing. The question is: Will we let Him?

### IV. Continuing in the Faith — The Daily Walk (8-10 mins) [*]

- **Introduction to Section:** Paul warns that this transforming work continues ONLY "if you continue in the faith" (Colossians 1:23). Perseverance is not optional; it is the evidence of genuine faith. But HOW do we continue?

#### A. Grounded and Steadfast — The Foundation of Faith (4-5 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Colossians 1:23 (NKJV)
  - Reading: "...if indeed you continue in the faith, grounded and steadfast, and are not moved away from the hope of the gospel..."
  - [WB]: "CONTINUE / GROUNDED / STEADFAST"
  - Not "Once Saved, Always Saved": Paul's "if" is conditional. The hope is sure — but it is realized in those who PERSIST in faith. This is not earning salvation; it is _receiving_ it daily.
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "What are the three things Paul says we must do to remain in the faith? What might 'being moved away' look like practically?"
- **Foundation Text 2:** 1 Corinthians 15:58
  - Reading: "Therefore, my beloved brethren, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that your labor is not in vain in the Lord."
  - [WB]: "STEADFAST / IMMOVABLE / ABOUNDING"
  - The Danger of Human-Based Ways: Paul was concerned that the Colossians would chase after human philosophies and self-help religion (Colossians 2:8). Today's danger is the same — programs, techniques, disciplines APART from Christ's indwelling are powerless.
  - [EGW]: "We need daily to cultivate faith in a present Saviour. Trusting in a power out of and above ourselves, exercising faith in unseen support and power which is waiting the demand of the needy and dependent, we can trust amid clouds as well as sunshine, singing of present deliverance and present enjoyment of His love." (TDWG 62)
  - [WB]: "Daily faith in a PRESENT Saviour"

#### B. The Daily Surrender — How We Continue (4-5 mins) [*]

- **Foundation Text 3:** Colossians 1:28-29
  - Reading: "Him we preach, warning every man and teaching every man in all wisdom, that we may present every man perfect in Christ Jesus. To this end I also labor, striving according to His working which works in me mightily."
  - [WB]: "HIS WORKING → WORKS IN ME"
  - The Balance: Paul "labors" and "strives" — but notice, "according to HIS working." This is not self-effort. It is cooperating with Christ's energy at work within. We work BECAUSE He is working. We obey BECAUSE He gives the will and the power.
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* "How do you know the difference between 'striving according to His working' and mere self-effort?"
- **Foundation Text 4:** Colossians 2:6-7
  - Reading: "As you therefore have received Christ Jesus the Lord, so walk in Him, rooted and built up in Him and established in the faith..."
  - [WB]: "AS you RECEIVED... SO walk"
  - The Key: HOW did you receive Christ? By faith! By surrender! By admitting helplessness! SO walk — the same way. Not a different method now that you're "in." Every day, every moment — receive Him afresh by faith.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ A branch does not strain to produce fruit. It abides in the vine, and the sap flows, and fruit comes naturally. When a branch starts _trying_ to produce fruit independently, it withers. Our striving is the striving of ABIDING — staying connected, surrendered, receiving — not the striving of independent production.
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "What is one practical thing you can do each morning to 'receive Christ afresh' — to start the day in surrender rather than self-effort?"

### V. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth: The weekly lesson speaks of reconciliation and hope. Through the sanctuary lens, we see that reconciliation is NOT merely forgiveness — it is restoration. The mystery revealed is "Christ in you" — His righteousness not only credited to your account (imputed), but actively reproduced in your life (imparted). This happens BY FAITH, through daily surrender, as we abide in Him.
- The Practical Takeaway: The difference between defeated Christianity and victorious Christianity is NOT trying harder. It is the difference between self-effort and Christ's indwelling faith. When you stop trying to manufacture righteousness and start receiving Christ's life, transformation happens — not because you are strong, but because HE IS.
  - [WB]: "STOP TRYING → START RECEIVING"
- Call to Surrender/Receive: Christ your High Priest is ministering NOW for your complete transformation. He does not merely forgive — He transforms. The question is not, "Will you try harder?" The question is, "Will you surrender deeper?"
  - [WB]: Sanctuary diagram with Christ as High Priest and arrow pointing to "CHRIST IN YOU"
- _(Final Question):_ "What specific area of struggle — temper, fear, habit, resentment — will you bring to Christ TODAY, not to 'try harder,' but to surrender completely and let Him live His victory through you?"
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope:
  - [EGW]: "Our only ground of hope is in the righteousness of Christ imputed to us, and in that wrought by His Spirit working in and through us." (SC 63)
  - [WB]: "Philippians 1:6 — He who has begun a good work in you WILL complete it"
  - The same Christ who began reconciliation at the cross will FINISH it in your life. Trust Him. Receive Him. Let Him work. He will not fail.
