# 2026 Q1 W10 - February 28–March 6

## Complete in Christ

**Weekly Lesson Topic:** Completeness in Christ versus false teachings in Colossae — the ceremonial law nailed to the cross, the enduring validity of God's moral law.
**Present Truth Theme:** Christ's righteousness received by faith alone is our completeness — the sanctuary reveals how shadows give way to Substance, while the eternal law remains the transcript of His character reproduced in us.
**Central Focus:** Resting in Christ's finished work for justification while receiving His indwelling life for transformation — not by human effort or religious performance, but by faith alone.
**Key Texts:** Colossians 2:9-10; Colossians 2:6-7; Romans 7:7

**(Estimated Time: 45 Minutes Total)**

---

### I. Introduction (5-7 mins)

- Hook: Have you ever felt like you weren't doing _enough_ to be right with God? Keeping enough rules, praying enough, studying enough? Or perhaps the opposite — told that God's law doesn't matter at all, that you're "free" to live however you please?
- Lesson Connection: This week's lesson addresses a church in Colossae facing these very tensions — false teachers pushing ceremonies AND human philosophies. Paul's answer cuts through both errors.
- Present Truth Bridge: What Colossians 2 reveals, when we see it through the sanctuary lens, is the profound distinction between the _shadow_ (ceremonial system pointing forward to Christ) and the _Substance_ (Christ Himself AND His eternal moral law). Completeness is found in neither legalism nor lawlessness — but in Christ alone, whose righteousness is received by faith and whose character is reproduced in us by the same faith.
  - [WB]: Write `COMPLETE IN CHRIST: SHADOW vs. SUBSTANCE`
- Why This Matters Now: Christ is ministering now in the Most Holy Place — applying His blood, cleansing the sanctuary, preparing a people who reflect His character. The question is not "Did I do enough?" but "Am I in Him? Is He in me?" This is the message of Righteousness by Faith.
- Roadmap: We will explore (1) what it means to be _complete_ in Christ, (2) how the ceremonial shadows gave way to the Substance, and (3) how the eternal law remains God's standard — fulfilled IN us by Christ's indwelling faith.

### II. Complete in Him: The Fullness of Christ Received by Faith (15-17 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** The Colossian heresy was essentially telling believers they needed _something more_ than Christ — ceremonies, philosophies, angel worship, ascetic practices. Paul's answer is devastatingly simple: In Christ dwells ALL the fullness of God, and you are COMPLETE in Him.

#### A. The Treasure Hidden in Christ (6-7 mins)

- **Foundation Text 1:** Colossians 2:9-10 — "For in Him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily; and you are complete in Him, who is the head of all principality and power."
  - Reading/Context: Paul declares the staggering reality — the FULL Deity is in Christ, and believers are COMPLETE (filled full) in Him.
  - [WB]: `Col 2:9-10` / `"ALL THE FULLNESS" → "COMPLETE IN HIM"`
  - Unpacking the Truth: The Greek word for "complete" (πεπληρωμένοι) means "made full, filled up." It's passive — WE are made full; we don't fill ourselves. This is justification AND sanctification in seed form. Christ's righteousness covers us (justification) and fills us (sanctification). We lack nothing in Him.
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "What does Paul say dwells in Christ? And what does that mean for the believer who is 'in Him'?"
- **Foundation Text 2:** Colossians 2:3 — "in whom are hidden all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge."
  - Reading/Connection: Job asked where wisdom could be found (Job 28:12). Paul answers: in Christ alone. All treasure, all wisdom, all knowledge — HIDDEN in Him. The false teachers claimed special knowledge; Paul says true knowledge is in Christ.
  - [WB]: `Christ = ALL TREASURES OF WISDOM & KNOWLEDGE`
  - The Faith Distinction: Here is where self-effort fails. Human philosophy, tradition, ceremonies — these are attempts to find wisdom and salvation OUTSIDE of Christ or by ADDING to Christ. But faith looks to Christ alone and receives from Him ALL that is needed. The sanctuary taught this: every piece pointed to Christ. To look elsewhere was to miss the whole point.
  - [EGW]: "The Lord Jesus acts through the Holy Spirit; for it is His representative. Through it He infuses spiritual life into the soul, quickening its energies for good, cleansing from moral defilement, and giving it a fitness for His kingdom. . . . What a thought is this! In Christ 'dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily. And ye are complete in him.'" (OHC 152.4)
  - [WB]: `"COMPLETE IN HIM" — OHC 152`
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* "If we are truly 'complete' in Christ, what does that say about our attempts to add human requirements to the gospel — or our feelings of inadequacy before God?"

- **Illustration & Practical Application:**
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Imagine a child adopted into a wealthy family. The inheritance, the name, the resources — all are his the moment the adoption is finalized. He doesn't earn it by chores; he receives it by relationship. But a servant might work tirelessly in the same house, trying to earn what the child receives freely. Many Christians live as servants, striving to earn what is already theirs in Christ. Completeness is not achieved; it is received by faith.
  - The Practical Landing: On Monday morning, when you feel spiritually bankrupt — when the enemy whispers "you're not enough" — the answer is not to try harder. It is to look to Christ, receive by faith what is already yours in Him, and rest in His fullness. This REST is what the Sabbath symbolizes — ceasing from our works, trusting in His.
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "What 'servant mentality' do you need to lay down this week? Where are you striving to earn what Christ offers freely?"

#### B. Rooted and Built Up in Him (5-6 mins)

- **Foundation Text 3:** Colossians 2:6-7 — "As you therefore have received Christ Jesus the Lord, so walk in Him, rooted and built up in Him and established in the faith..."
  - Reading/Challenge: HOW did you receive Christ? By faith. HOW do you walk in Him? By faith. The same faith that saved you is the same faith that sanctifies you. Rooted like a tree, built up like a building — both images speak of continuous receiving from a source OUTSIDE ourselves.
  - [WB]: `RECEIVE by faith → WALK by faith` / `ROOTED & BUILT UP`
  - How to Receive: The plant metaphor is powerful. A tree doesn't strain to grow; it receives water, sun, nutrients. Growth is the natural result of CONNECTION to the source. So the Christian doesn't strain to be holy; he abides in Christ (John 15) and holiness is the fruit. This is Righteousness by Faith — Christ's life flowing through us.
  - [EGW]: "The plant grows by receiving that which God has provided to sustain its life. So spiritual growth is attained through cooperation with divine agencies. As the plant takes root in the soil, so we are to take root in Christ. As the plant receives the sunshine, the dew, and the rain, so we are to receive the Holy Spirit." (GAG 197.4)
  - [WB]: `RECEIVE → GROW (GAG 197)`
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection/Application):* "A plant doesn't 'try' to grow — it simply receives and grows. What would your spiritual life look like if you focused more on *receiving* from Christ than on *trying* for Christ?"

### III. Shadow and Substance: What Was Nailed to the Cross (15-17 mins)

- **Introduction to Section:** Colossians 2:14-17 is often misused to abolish God's moral law and the seventh-day Sabbath. But when we understand the sanctuary system — the shadows pointing to Christ — we see that Paul is speaking of the CEREMONIAL law, not the eternal Ten Commandments. The substance is Christ; the shadows are fulfilled.

#### A. The Handwriting of Ordinances Nailed to the Cross (7-8 mins)

- **Foundation Text 4:** Colossians 2:13-14 — "And you, being dead in your trespasses... He has made alive together with Him, having forgiven you all trespasses, having wiped out the handwriting of requirements that was against us, which was contrary to us. And He has taken it out of the way, having nailed it to the cross."
  - Reading/Context: What was nailed? The "handwriting of requirements" (χειρόγραφον τοῖς δόγμασιν). This is language of a debt certificate — a record against us. It can also refer to the ceremonial ordinances that stood between Jew and Gentile.
  - [WB]: `Col 2:14` / `"HANDWRITING OF ORDINANCES" = CEREMONIAL LAW / DEBT RECORD`
  - Unpacking the Truth: The ceremonial law — circumcision, sacrifices, festivals — these were shadows pointing forward to Christ. They condemned us because they pointed to a Savior not yet come. When Christ died, the shadow met the Substance. The veil was torn (Matt 27:51). The debt was paid. The ceremonial system, having served its purpose, was "nailed to the cross."
  - [DQ]: *(Observation):* "What was 'against us' and 'contrary to us' that Christ took out of the way? Was it God's moral law or something else?"
- **Foundation Text 5:** Deuteronomy 31:24-26 — "So it was, when Moses had completed writing the words of this law in a book... that Moses commanded the Levites... 'Take this Book of the Law, and put it BESIDE the ark of the covenant...'"
  - Reading/Connection: Notice the distinction: the moral law (Ten Commandments) was written by God's finger on stone and placed INSIDE the ark. The ceremonial law was written by Moses in a book and placed BESIDE the ark. This is not a minor detail — it's a profound sanctuary truth about what is eternal and what was temporary.
  - [WB]: Diagram: `ARK: [INSIDE = 10 Commandments (God's finger, stone)] [BESIDE = Ceremonial Law (Moses, book)]`
  - The Faith Distinction: If the moral law were nailed to the cross, how could sin be defined? Paul himself says, "I would not have known sin except through the law. For I would not have known covetousness unless the law had said, 'You shall not covet'" (Rom 7:7). The moral law stands; it reveals God's character. The ceremonial law, fulfilled in Christ, is set aside.
  - [EGW]: "Many in the Christian world also have a veil before their eyes and heart. They do not see to the end of that which was done away. They do not see that it was only the ceremonial law which was abrogated at the death of Christ. They claim that the moral law was nailed to the cross. Heavy is the veil that darkens their understanding." (1SM 239.1)
  - [WB]: `"Only the CEREMONIAL law was abrogated" — 1SM 239`
  - [DQ]: *(Reflection):* 'Why is it important to distinguish between the ceremonial law (nailed to the cross) and the moral law (eternal)? What happens to our understanding of sin and righteousness if we confuse them?'

#### B. Sabbaths: Shadow or Eternal Sign? (6-7 mins) [*]

- **Foundation Text 6:** Colossians 2:16-17 — "So let no one judge you in food or in drink, or regarding a festival or a new moon or sabbaths, which are a shadow of things to come, but the substance is of Christ."
  - Reading/Context: This is the most contested text. Is Paul abolishing the seventh-day Sabbath?
  - [WB]: `Col 2:16-17` / `"SABBATHS" = CEREMONIAL? OR WEEKLY?`
  - Unpacking the Truth: Several points clarify:
    - The sequence "festival, new moon, sabbaths" mirrors Hosea 2:11 and refers to CEREMONIAL occasions — annual feasts, monthly new moons, ceremonial sabbaths (Lev 23:11, 24, 32).
    - "Food and drink" refers to meal and drink OFFERINGS (not dietary laws) — temple sacrifices.
    - The seventh-day Sabbath was instituted at CREATION (Gen 2:2-3) — BEFORE sin, before any shadow system. It memorializes Creation, not the sacrificial system.
    - The weekly Sabbath is the SEAL of God (Exod 20:8-11; Ezek 20:12, 20) — the sign of the Creator's authority and His sanctifying work in us.
  - [SN]: _Illustration:_ Think of a family reunion that happens every year on the anniversary of the family's founding. Separately, there are special gatherings for graduations, weddings, etc. If someone says, "Those special gatherings are done; the family has grown up," does that mean the founding anniversary is abolished? No! The weekly Sabbath is the FOUNDING institution — established at Creation, part of the eternal moral law. The ceremonial sabbaths were the "special gatherings" pointing to specific redemptive events.
  - [EGW]: "I saw the ten commandments written on them [the tables of stone] with the finger of God. On one table were four, and on the other six. . . . But the fourth, the Sabbath commandment, shone above them all; for the Sabbath was set apart to be kept in honor of God's holy name. The holy Sabbath looked glorious — a halo of glory was all around it. I saw that the Sabbath commandment was not nailed to the cross." (Mar 245.3)
  - [WB]: `SABBATH = Creation / Moral Law / NOT NAILED`
  - [DQ]: *(Interpretation/Application):* "How does understanding the Sabbath as a CREATION ordinance (not a ceremonial shadow) change the way we see its relevance today? What does the Sabbath teach us about Righteousness by Faith — resting in Christ's finished work?"

- **Foundation Text 7:** Romans 7:7 — "What shall we say then? Is the law sin? Certainly not! On the contrary, I would not have known sin except through the law. For I would not have known covetousness unless the law had said, 'You shall not covet.'"
  - Reading/Challenge: Paul quotes the TENTH COMMANDMENT. This proves he considers the moral law still valid and necessary to define sin. If the Ten Commandments were nailed to the cross, Paul could not use them to define sin. Sin is still transgression of God's law (1 John 3:4).
  - [WB]: `Rom 7:7` / `"I would not have known sin except through the LAW"`
  - The Practical Landing: The moral law is not our savior — it cannot justify us. But it is the STANDARD of righteousness, the transcript of Christ's character. When Christ's faith lives in us, His character (which is the law) is reproduced in us. This is the commandments of God AND the faith of Jesus (Rev 14:12).
  - [DQ]: *(Application):* "If the moral law defines sin and reveals Christ's character, how should we relate to it — not as a means of salvation, but as the fruit of salvation?"

### IV. Conclusion & Appeal (5-8 mins)

- Summary of Present Truth: The Colossian heresy — adding ceremonies, philosophies, human requirements to Christ — is alive and well today. So is the opposite error: abolishing God's eternal law in the name of "freedom." But present truth reveals the sanctuary distinction:
  - The **ceremonial law** (shadows of Christ's sacrifice and priestly work) was fulfilled at the cross and is no longer binding.
  - The **moral law** (the Ten Commandments, including the seventh-day Sabbath) remains the eternal standard — not as a means of salvation, but as the transcript of Christ's character, reproduced in the believer by faith.
  - **Completeness** is found in Christ alone. His righteousness imputed covers us; His righteousness imparted transforms us. Both are received BY FAITH.
- The Practical Takeaway: You are COMPLETE in Him. Stop striving to add to what Christ has finished. Stop subtracting from what God has established. Rest in His fullness. Abide in Him, and His character will be reproduced in you — not by your effort, but by His life flowing through you.
  - [WB]: `COMPLETE = Christ's Righteousness RECEIVED by Faith`
- Call to Surrender/Receive: Where are you trying to earn what is freely given? Where are you subtracting from God's standard in the name of freedom? Bring both to Christ's sanctuary ministry. He is able to save to the uttermost. He is able to write His law on your heart.
  - [WB]: `"YE ARE COMPLETE IN HIM" (Col 2:10)` / `"ROOTED AND BUILT UP" (Col 2:7)`
  - _(Final Question):_ "What is one area where you will stop striving in your own strength and instead receive from Christ by faith this week? What does 'completeness in Him' look like for you practically?"
- Final Thought/Urgency & Hope: We are living in the hour of God's judgment — Christ is in the Most Holy Place. The question is not whether we've kept enough rules or whether rules even matter. The question is: Are we IN HIM? Is HIS faith living in us? The final generation will be described as those who "keep the commandments of God AND have the faith of Jesus" (Rev 14:12). Both. Not one or the other. Not by human effort, but by the indwelling Christ.
  - [EGW]: "It is the righteousness of Christ that goes before us. It is His character that we are to copy. And then what? — The glory of the Lord shall be our rearward. Our Leader goes before us, and as we follow Him, He imparts to us His righteousness, which is revealed in our lives by a well-ordered life and a godly conversation." (TDG 262.3)
  - [WB]: `Rev 14:12` / `"COMMANDMENTS OF GOD + FAITH OF JESUS"`
