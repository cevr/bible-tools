## Section 1 — The Sabbath's Origin: Authority, Not Convenience (8 min)

_Addresses: "Any day is fine," "The Sabbath is Jewish"_

### A. Created by God, Not Moses

- Genesis 2:1–3 — Three divine acts on the seventh day:
  1. God **rested** — _shabath_ (#7673) = ceased, stopped
  2. God **blessed** — _barak_ (#1288) = endowed with divine favor
  3. God **sanctified** — _qadash_ (#6942) = set apart as holy
  - [WB]: Write "RESTED → BLESSED → SANCTIFIED" (Gen 2:1–3)
  - No other day received this threefold distinction
- This was before sin, before Israel, before Sinai
  - The Sabbath belongs to Creation, not to the ceremonial system
  - [RQ]: Can something instituted at Creation — before the fall, before any
    nation — be "only for the Jews"? (Gen 2:1–3)
- Mark 2:27 — "The sabbath was made for man"
  - _Anthropos_ (#444) = mankind, humanity — not _Ioudaios_ (Jew)
  - Made _for_ man — a gift, not a chain

### B. The Seal of God's Authority

- A seal contains three elements: name, title, territory
  - [WB]: Write 'SEAL = NAME + TITLE + TERRITORY'
- Exodus 20:8–11 contains all three:
  - **Name**: "the LORD thy God"
  - **Title**: Creator — "made"
  - **Territory**: "heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is"
  - [WB]: Write the three seal elements from Exodus 20:11
  - No other commandment identifies _who_ God is and _why_ He has authority
- Ezekiel 20:12 — "I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them,
  that they might know that I am the LORD that sanctify them"
  - The Sabbath is a _sign_ — it identifies the true God as Creator and
    Sanctifier
  - [WB]: Write "SABBATH = SIGN of Creation + Sanctification" (Ezek 20:12)
- Ezekiel 20:20 — "Hallow my sabbaths; and they shall be a sign between me and
  you, that ye may know that I am the LORD your God"
  - [RQ]: If the Sabbath is the sign that identifies the true God, what happens
    when the sign is removed? (Ezek 20:20)

### C. The Sabbath and Worship

- The Sabbath answers the most fundamental question: _Who do you worship?_
  - It points to the Creator as the source of all authority
  - Remove the Sabbath, and any deity can claim worship — there is no
    distinguishing mark
- Isaiah 58:13–14 — "If thou turn away thy foot from the sabbath, from doing thy
  pleasure on my holy day; and call the sabbath a delight, the holy of the LORD,
  honourable... then shalt thou delight thyself in the LORD"
  - Not restriction — relationship
  - The Sabbath is described as _honourable_, _holy_, a _delight_
- [EGW]: "The Sabbath is a sign of the relationship existing between God and His
  people, a sign that they honor His law. It distinguishes between His loyal
  subjects and transgressors." (6T 349.3)

## Section 2 — Christ and the Apostles Kept the Sabbath (8 min) [*]

_Addresses: "The apostles changed the day," "Sunday is the Christian Sabbath"_

### A. Christ's Sabbath Practice

- Luke 4:16 — "As his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day"
  - _Ethos_ (#1485) = established practice, regular habit
  - Not cultural accommodation — deliberate example from the Lord of the Sabbath
- He healed on the Sabbath (Matt 12:10–12; Luke 13:10–16; John 5:1–9)
  - He never broke the Sabbath — He broke Pharisaical additions
  - Matthew 12:12 — "It is lawful to do well on the sabbath days"
  - He restored the Sabbath to its original purpose: blessing, not burden
- Matthew 24:20 — "Pray ye that your flight be not... on the sabbath day"
  - Spoken about AD 70 — nearly 40 years after the cross
  - [RQ]: Why would Jesus ask His followers to pray about Sabbath observance
    decades later if He had abolished it at the cross? (Matt 24:20)

### B. The Apostles' Sabbath Practice

- Acts 13:14 — Paul went into the synagogue on the Sabbath
- Acts 13:42–44 — Gentiles requested Paul preach again "the next sabbath"
  - "The next sabbath day came almost the whole city together to hear the word of
    God" (v. 44)
  - [RQ]: If Sunday was the new worship day, why didn't Paul say "Come tomorrow
    — we worship on the first day now"? (Acts 13:42)
  - [WB]: Write "PAUL → SABBATH → GENTILES" (Acts 13:42–44)
- Acts 16:13 — "On the sabbath we went out of the city by a river side, where
  prayer was wont to be made"
  - No synagogue in Philippi — Paul still kept the Sabbath, outdoors
- Acts 17:2 — "Paul, as his manner was, went in unto them, and three sabbath days
  reasoned with them out of the scriptures"
  - _Ethos_ again — same word used of Jesus in Luke 4:16
- Acts 18:4 — Paul in Corinth "reasoned in the synagogue every sabbath, and
  persuaded the Jews and the Greeks"
  - Jews _and_ Greeks — the Sabbath was not treated as a Jewish-only practice

### C. The "First Day" Texts — Examined

- There are only eight references to the first day of the week in the NT
- None command worship; none transfer the Sabbath
  - [WB]: Write 'FIRST DAY = 8 texts. SABBATH TRANSFER = 0 texts.'
- Acts 20:7 — "Upon the first day of the week... disciples came together to
  break bread"
  - This was a Saturday night meeting (biblical days begin at sunset)
  - Paul departed the next morning (Sunday) — traveling, not resting
  - [EB]: Acts 2:46 — They broke bread "daily" — breaking bread is not a
    Sabbath marker
- 1 Corinthians 16:2 — "Upon the first day of the week let every one of you lay
  by him in store"
  - _Par' heauto_ = "by himself" — at home, private bookkeeping
  - A collection for famine relief, not a worship service
- Revelation 1:10 — "I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day"
  - Which day is the "Lord's day"?
  - Isaiah 58:13 — God calls the Sabbath "my holy day"
  - Mark 2:28 — "The Son of man is Lord also of the sabbath"
  - [RQ]: When Scripture itself identifies the Sabbath as the day belonging to
    the Lord, on what basis do we assign that title to Sunday? (Isa 58:13; Mark
    2:28)

## Section 3 — Who Changed It? The Historical Record (10 min)

_Addresses: "Sunday has always been the Christian day," "God must have
authorized the change"_

### A. Prophecy Predicted the Change

- Daniel 7:25 — "He shall speak great words against the most High, and shall
  wear out the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws"
  - [WB]: Write "THINK TO CHANGE: (1) TIMES (2) LAWS" (Dan 7:25)
  - "Times and laws" — the only commandment involving both time _and_ law is the
    fourth
  - The word is _think_ (_sebar_) — he would _intend_ to change it, not
    actually succeed in God's sight
  - [RQ]: If God Himself authorized a change of day, why does Daniel describe it
    as the act of an opposing power? (Dan 7:25)
- This power arises from the fourth beast (Rome) — we will examine this in
  detail in Part 3 (Historicism)

### B. The Historical Record — Rome's Own Admission

- The change from Sabbath to Sunday was gradual, not apostolic
  - [WB]: Timeline: APOSTLES (Sabbath) → 2nd–4th Century (gradual shift) →
    AD 321 (Constantine's Sunday law) → Council of Laodicea c. 364
    (Sabbath-keeping anathematized)
- Constantine's Sunday edict (AD 321): "On the venerable Day of the Sun let
  magistrates and people residing in cities rest"
  - A civil law — pagan in origin, later Christianized
- The Council of Laodicea (c. AD 364), Canon 29: "Christians shall not
  Judaize and be idle on Saturday, but shall work on that day; but the Lord's
  day they shall especially honour"
  - The church had to _forbid_ Sabbath-keeping — proving Christians were still
    keeping it in the 4th century
- [Aside]: You don't legislate against a practice that nobody follows. The very
  existence of anti-Sabbath canons is evidence of continued Sabbath observance.

### C. Rome Claims the Authority

- The Roman Catholic Church openly claims the change as its own act:
  - "The Church... transferred the solemnity from Saturday to Sunday" — from the
    Council of Trent catechism tradition
  - "Sunday is our mark of authority... The Church is above the Bible, and this
    transference of Sabbath observance is proof of that fact." — Catholic Record,
    September 1, 1923
  - [WB]: Write "BIBLE AUTHORITY: Sabbath" vs. "CHURCH AUTHORITY: Sunday"
- [RQ]: If the change of day rests on church tradition rather than Scripture,
  which authority do we follow? (Isa 8:20; Acts 5:29)
- Isaiah 8:20 — "To the law and to the testimony: if they speak not according to
  this word, it is because there is no light in them"
- [EGW]: "The mark of the beast is the papal sabbath, which has been accepted by
  the world in the place of the day that God has appointed." (8T 117.2)

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — The Sabbath in the Last Days (5 min)

### A. The Sabbath as the Final Test

- The Sabbath question is ultimately about authority: God's Word or human
  tradition
- Revelation 14:7 — "Worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and
  the fountains of waters"
  - This language directly echoes Exodus 20:11 — the Sabbath commandment
  - The first angel's message calls the world _back_ to the Creator — back to
    His Sabbath
  - [WB]: Write "REV 14:7 = EXOD 20:11" — side by side
- Revelation 14:9–10 — The third angel warns against worshipping the beast and
  receiving his mark
  - The contrast: commandments of God vs. commandments of men
- Revelation 14:12 — "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that
  keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus"
  - [WB]: Write "SEAL OF GOD (Sabbath — Creator's authority)" vs. "MARK OF BEAST
    (Sunday — man's authority)" (Rev 14:7, 9, 12)

### B. The Sabbath Restored in the New Earth

- Isaiah 66:22–23 — "From one new moon to another, and from one sabbath to
  another, shall all flesh come to worship before me, saith the LORD"
  - The Sabbath is not temporary — it stretches from Eden to the new earth
  - [WB]: Write "EDEN (Gen 2) → SINAI (Exod 20) → CHRIST (Luke 4) → NEW EARTH
    (Isa 66)" — one unbroken line
  - [RQ]: If the Sabbath will be kept for eternity in the new earth, why would
    God abolish it now? (Isa 66:22–23)

### C. A Question of Loyalty

- Every generation faces a loyalty test — the Sabbath is ours
- It is the seal of the living God, the sign of His creative and sanctifying
  authority
- The question is not "Which day?" — Scripture answers that plainly
- The question is "Whose authority will I follow?"

### Call to Action — Honor the Creator's Day

- **Remember** the Sabbath as God commanded — the only commandment He said to
  remember, because He knew the world would forget (Exod 20:8)
- **Reject** the claim that any human authority can change God's holy day (Dan
  7:25; Isa 8:20)
- **Rest** in the Sabbath as a sign of your Creator and Sanctifier — not as
  legalism but as love (Ezek 20:12; Isa 58:13–14)
- **Prepare** for the final test of loyalty — the Sabbath will distinguish those
  who follow God's Word from those who follow human tradition (Rev 14:12)
- **Resolve**: _I will keep the day God blessed, whatever the cost, because my
  allegiance belongs to the Creator_
