## Section 1 — The Bible Promises a Prophetic Gift in the Last Days (8 min)

_Addresses: "The prophetic gift ended with the apostles"_

### A. God Always Leads Through Prophets

- Amos 3:7 — "Surely the Lord GOD will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret
  unto his servants the prophets"
  - This is God's stated method — He reveals before He acts
  - [WB]: Write "GOD'S METHOD: reveal through prophets → then act" (Amos 3:7)
  - Before the Flood — Noah. Before the Exodus — Moses. Before the exile —
    Jeremiah. Before Christ's first coming — John the Baptist.
  - [RQ]: If God always sent a prophet before major events, would He leave the
    final generation without prophetic guidance? (Amos 3:7)

### B. The Prophetic Gift Was Not Limited to Biblical Times

- Ephesians 4:11–13 — Christ gave "some, apostles; and some, prophets; and
  some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers; For the perfecting of the
  saints... till we all come in the unity of the faith"
  - "Till" = _mechri_ (#3360) — the gifts continue _until_ the church reaches
    unity and the measure of the stature of Christ
  - [WB]: Write "PROPHETIC GIFT → 'TILL' unity + fullness of Christ" (Eph
    4:11–13)
  - Has the church reached that unity? Has the church reached the fullness of
    Christ's character? Then the prophetic gift has not ended.
- 1 Corinthians 1:7 — "Ye come behind in no gift; waiting for the coming of our
  Lord Jesus Christ"
  - Paul expects spiritual gifts — including prophecy — to continue until
    Christ's coming

### C. Joel's Promise and the Last Days

- Joel 2:28–29 — "I will pour out my spirit upon all flesh; and your sons and
  your daughters shall prophesy, your old men shall dream dreams, your young men
  shall see visions"
  - Peter quotes this at Pentecost (Acts 2:17–18) as beginning to be fulfilled
  - But Pentecost was the _former rain_ — Joel's full fulfillment includes the
    _latter rain_ at the end of time
  - [WB]: Write "FORMER RAIN (Pentecost) → LATTER RAIN (end times) → prophetic
    gift active" (Joel 2:28–29; Acts 2:17)
- Revelation 12:17 — The dragon targets the remnant who has "the testimony of
  Jesus Christ"
  - Revelation 19:10 defines this as "the spirit of prophecy"
  - [RQ]: If the spirit of prophecy is an identifying mark of the remnant, and
    we claim to be that remnant, where is the prophetic gift among us? (Rev
    12:17; 19:10)

## Section 2 — The Biblical Tests of a Prophet (10 min)

_Addresses: "How do we know Ellen White was a true prophet?"_

### A. The Tests

- The Bible does not say "accept every prophet" — it says _test_ them
  - 1 Thessalonians 5:20–21 — "Despise not prophesyings. Prove all things; hold
    fast that which is good"
  - 1 John 4:1 — "Beloved, believe not every spirit, but try the spirits
    whether they are of God"
- The biblical tests of a prophet:
  1. **Agreement with Scripture** — Isaiah 8:20 — "To the law and to the
     testimony: if they speak not according to this word, it is because there is
     no light in them"
  2. **Fulfilled predictions** — Deuteronomy 18:21–22 — "If the thing follow
     not, nor come to pass... the LORD hath not spoken"
  3. **Fruit of the life** — Matthew 7:16, 20 — "Ye shall know them by their
     fruits"
  4. **Confession of Christ's incarnation** — 1 John 4:2 — "Every spirit that
     confesseth that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh is of God"
  5. **Manner of receiving visions** — Numbers 12:6 — "I the LORD will make
     myself known unto him in a vision, and will speak unto him in a dream"
  - [WB]: Write the 5 tests in a column (Isa 8:20; Deut 18:22; Matt 7:16; 1
    John 4:2; Num 12:6)

### B. Ellen White Tested by Scripture

- **Test 1: Agreement with Scripture**
  - Her writings consistently uphold the Bible as the supreme authority
  - "The Bible, and the Bible alone, is to be our creed." (1SM 416.3)
  - Her teachings align with the doctrines we have established in Parts 1–7:
    the law, the Sabbath, the sanctuary, the judgment, the state of the dead
  - She never contradicts clear biblical teaching

- **Test 2: Fulfilled Predictions**
  - Health principles (link between diet and disease, tobacco and cancer) —
    stated decades before medical science confirmed them
  - The rise of spiritism as a major end-time deception (GC 588) — increasingly
    evident
  - The ecumenical movement bringing Protestantism back toward Rome (GC 445) —
    visibly unfolding
  - The deterioration of American constitutional principles regarding religious
    liberty (GC 442) — increasingly relevant
  - [WB]: Write 'FULFILLED: health-disease link / spiritism / ecumenism / religious
liberty erosion'

- **Test 3: Fruit**
  - A global educational system, health system, and publishing ministry
  - Lives transformed by her counsels
  - Matthew 7:17 — "A good tree cannot bring forth evil fruit"

- **Test 4: Confession of Christ**
  - Central theme of her work: the beauty and sufficiency of Christ
  - "The sacrifice of Christ as an atonement for sin is the great truth around
    which all other truths cluster." (GW 315.2)

- **Test 5: Manner of Visions**
  - Over 2,000 visions and dreams across 70 years of ministry
  - Physical phenomena witnessed by many: open eyes, no breathing during vision,
    supernatural strength
  - Consistent with biblical prophetic experience (Dan 10:17; Num 24:4)

### C. The Weight of Evidence

  evidence
  - [WB]: Write "ALL 5 TESTS PASSED = weight of evidence" (1 Thess 5:21)

## Section 3 — Answering the Objections (8 min) [*]

_Addresses: "Plagiarism," "Contradicts sola scriptura," "Just personal
opinions"_

### A. The Plagiarism Charge

- Ellen White used literary sources — this is documented and not disputed
- But using sources is not the same as plagiarism, and it does not disqualify
  prophetic inspiration
  - Luke used sources (Luke 1:1–3) — "Having had perfect understanding of all
    things from the very first"
  - The Holy Spirit guided what to include and how to apply it
  - [WB]: Write 'LUKE used sources (Luke 1:1–3) = inspired. EGW used sources =
inspired.'
- The issue is not _originality_ but _authority_ — did God guide the work?
  - Biblical prophets quoted, alluded to, and built upon each other's writings
    (Micah/Isaiah parallels, Daniel drawing from Jeremiah)
- [RQ]: If Luke's use of sources doesn't disqualify his Gospel as inspired, why
  would Ellen White's use of sources disqualify her writings? (Luke 1:1–3)

### B. Does the Prophetic Gift Contradict Sola Scriptura?

- Sola scriptura means the Bible is the supreme, final authority — not the
  _only_ way God communicates
  - The Bible itself contains the writings of dozens of prophets — the gift of
    prophecy is _part_ of what the Bible teaches
  - [WB]: Write "SOLA SCRIPTURA: Bible is the SUPREME standard → prophets are
    TESTED by it" (Isa 8:20)
- Ellen White's writings are a _lesser light_ pointing to the _greater light_
  - "The Lord has given a lesser light to lead men and women to the greater
    light." (CM 125.2)
  - Her writings do not add to the Bible — they illuminate it, as a magnifying
    glass does not add words to a page
- [RQ]: If the Bible predicts the gift of prophecy in the last days (Joel 2:28;
  Rev 12:17), does believing in that gift contradict the Bible or fulfill it?

### C. "Just Personal Opinions"

- Ellen White distinguished between her inspired writings and her personal
  opinions — a mark of honesty, not weakness
  - Paul did the same (1 Cor 7:10 vs. 7:12)
- Her major works (The Great Controversy, Desire of Ages, Steps to Christ,
  Patriarchs and Prophets, etc.) were presented as messages from God
- The test is not "Was she perfect?" — no prophet was
  - Moses struck the rock. Elijah ran from Jezebel. Jonah ran from God.
  - Prophetic authority does not require personal perfection
- [EGW]: "In regard to infallibility, I never claimed it; God alone is
  infallible." (1SM 37.2)

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — The Gift That Guards (5 min)

### A. The Spirit of Prophecy Is a Gift, Not a Burden

- Proverbs 29:18 — "Where there is no vision, the people perish"
  - _Chazon_ (#2377) = prophetic vision
  - Without prophetic guidance, God's people drift
  - [WB]: Write "NO VISION → PEOPLE PERISH" (Prov 29:18)
- The prophetic gift has guided the Adventist movement through:
  - Doctrinal clarity (sanctuary, Sabbath, health)
  - Institutional development (education, health care, publishing)
  - Spiritual counsel (revival, reformation, character development)

### B. The Remnant's Identifying Mark

- Revelation 12:17 — The remnant has _both_ the commandments of God _and_ the
  testimony of Jesus
  - Remove either one and the identification fails
  - [WB]: Write "REMNANT = Commandments of God + Testimony of Jesus (Spirit of
    Prophecy)" (Rev 12:17; 19:10)
- The prophetic gift is not incidental — it is constitutive of the remnant's
  identity
- [RQ]: If we abandon the spirit of prophecy, do we still meet the biblical
  criteria for the remnant? (Rev 12:17)

### C. Read for Yourself

- The best defense of Ellen White's writings is reading them
- Many who criticize have never read her works — they rely on critics' summaries
- [Aside]: You would not reject a Bible book based on a critic's review without
  reading it yourself. Give the same fairness to the prophetic gift.

### Call to Action — Honour the Prophetic Gift

- **Test** every prophet by the biblical standard — including Ellen White. Do not
  accept or reject on hearsay (1 Thess 5:21; Isa 8:20)
- **Read** the Spirit of Prophecy writings for yourself — begin with Steps to
  Christ, The Desire of Ages, or The Great Controversy
- **Distinguish** the roles: the Bible is the supreme standard; the Spirit of
  Prophecy is the lesser light that illuminates the greater (CM 125.2)
- **Thank God** for the prophetic gift — it is a sign of His love and care for
  His last-day people (Amos 3:7; Eph 4:11–13)
- **Resolve**: _I will value, study, and follow the prophetic guidance God has
  given, always testing it by His Word_
