## Section 1 — The Remnant Is a Biblical Concept (8 min)

_Addresses: "The remnant concept is SDA invention"_

### A. The Remnant Pattern Through Scripture

- The remnant concept runs throughout the entire Bible — it is not an SDA
  invention
  - **Noah's family** — 8 saved out of a world (Gen 7:23; 1 Pet 3:20)
  - **Lot's family** — rescued from Sodom (Gen 19:29)
  - **Joseph** — "God sent me before you to preserve you a posterity"
    (Gen 45:7)
  - **7,000 in Elijah's day** — who had not bowed to Baal (1 Kings 19:18)
  - **Judah after exile** — the remnant returned (Ezra 9:8; Isa 10:20–22)
  - [WB]: Write timeline: NOAH → LOT → JOSEPH → ELIJAH → JUDAH → END-TIME
    REMNANT (Rev 12:17)

### B. The Remnant Is Always Small, Faithful, and Unpopular

- Isaiah 1:9 — "Except the LORD of hosts had left unto us a very small
  remnant, we should have been as Sodom"
- Romans 9:27 — "Though the number of the children of Israel be as the sand of
  the sea, a remnant shall be saved"
- Romans 11:5 — "Even so then at this present time also there is a remnant
  according to the election of grace"
  - [WB]: Write "REMNANT = small + faithful + by grace" (Rom 9:27; 11:5)
- The remnant is not the majority — it never has been
  - Noah preached 120 years and 8 were saved
  - Elijah thought he was alone — God had 7,000
  - [RQ]: If the remnant has always been small and unpopular, should its small
    size be used as evidence against it? (Matt 7:14)
- Matthew 7:14 — "Strait is the gate, and narrow is the way... and few there
  be that find it"

### C. The End-Time Remnant Is Prophetically Specified

- Revelation 12 traces the history of God's people (the woman = the church):
  - v. 1–5: The early church
  - v. 6, 14: The 1260 years of persecution (538–1798)
  - v. 17: After the 1260 years — the _remnant_ of her seed
  - [WB]: Write 'REV 12: Early church → 1260 years → REMNANT emerges after
1798'
- The remnant must arise _after_ 1798 — this is a chronological marker
- The remnant is the final segment of the true church — holding the same faith
  as the apostles, purified through the persecution of the Dark Ages

## Section 2 — The Marks of the Last-Day Remnant (10 min)

_Addresses: "Any church could be the remnant"_

### A. Mark 1: Keep the Commandments of God

- Revelation 12:17 — "Which keep the commandments of God"
  - Not some commandments — _the_ commandments of God
  - This includes the fourth — the Sabbath (Exod 20:8–11)
  - [WB]: Write "MARK 1: Keep ALL the commandments — including the 4th" (Rev
    12:17)
- Revelation 14:12 — "Here are they that keep the commandments of God"
  - The same people, described again after the three angels' messages
- [RQ]: How many Christian denominations today teach and practice the keeping of
  all ten commandments, including the seventh-day Sabbath? (Rev 12:17)

### B. Mark 2: Have the Testimony of Jesus Christ

- Revelation 12:17 — "And have the testimony of Jesus Christ"
- Revelation 19:10 — "The testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy"
  - The remnant has the prophetic gift active in its midst
  - [WB]: Write "MARK 2: Testimony of Jesus = Spirit of Prophecy" (Rev 12:17;
    19:10)
- As established in Part 8, the Seventh-day Adventist Church has the prophetic
  ministry of Ellen G. White — tested by the five biblical criteria and
  confirmed

### C. Additional Identifying Characteristics

- Beyond the two explicit marks of Revelation 12:17, the three angels' messages
  (Rev 14:6–12) describe the remnant's message and mission:
  - **Preaches the everlasting gospel** to every nation (Rev 14:6) — a global
    mission
  - **Proclaims the judgment hour** (Rev 14:7) — understands the sanctuary and
    the investigative judgment
  - **Calls out of Babylon** (Rev 14:8) — identifies and rejects false doctrines
  - **Warns against the mark of the beast** (Rev 14:9–11) — understands the
    Sabbath-Sunday conflict
  - **Keeps the commandments and has the faith of Jesus** (Rev 14:12) —
    righteousness by faith producing obedience
  - [WB]: Write 'REMNANT CHECKLIST: ✓ Global mission / ✓ Judgment hour / ✓ Call
out of Babylon / ✓ Sabbath seal / ✓ Prophetic gift / ✓ Commandments +
faith'
- [RQ]: How many churches match _all_ of these criteria simultaneously? (Rev
  14:6–12)

### D. Arose After 1798

- Revelation 12:17 places the remnant _after_ the 1260 years of persecution
  (538–1798)
- The Seventh-day Adventist Church arose out of the Millerite movement in the
  1840s–1860s — precisely after 1798
  - [WB]: Write '1798 → Millerite movement → SDA Church organized (1863)'
- This is not coincidence — it is prophetic timing

## Section 3 — Answering the Objections (8 min) [*]

_Addresses: "Arrogant," "Small American sect," "Any church is fine"_

### A. "Claiming to Be the Remnant Is Arrogant"

- The claim is not "We are better than other Christians"
  - It is "We believe prophecy identifies a specific end-time movement with
    specific characteristics, and we match those characteristics"
  - [WB]: Write "NOT: 'We are better'" / "BUT: 'Prophecy describes this — we
    match it'" (Rev 12:17)
- Israel was chosen not because they were superior but because God gave them a
  mission (Deut 7:7–8)
  - Chosenness is for service, not superiority
- [RQ]: Was it arrogant for Israel to accept their prophetic calling, or would
  it have been faithless to deny it? (Deut 7:7–8)
- [EGW]: "In a special sense Seventh-day Adventists have been set in the world
  as watchmen and light bearers. To them has been entrusted the last warning for
  a perishing world." (9T 19.1)
  - This is about responsibility, not privilege

### B. "Just a Small American Sect"

- The SDA Church has over 22 million baptized members worldwide
- Present in over 200 countries and territories
- One of the fastest-growing Christian denominations globally
- Operates the largest Protestant educational and healthcare systems in the world
  - [WB]: Write '22+ million / 200+ countries / largest Protestant education +
health systems'
- The majority of Adventists today are not American — the church is predominantly
  African, Asian, and Latin American
- [RQ]: Does a movement's geographic origin disqualify its prophetic legitimacy?
  Did Christianity begin in Rome or in a small corner of the Roman Empire? (Acts
  1:8)

### C. "Any Church Can Be the True Church"

- God has sincere people in every denomination — Revelation 18:4 proves this
  ("Come out of her, _my people_")
- But having sincere people is not the same as having the _full prophetic
  message_
  - Many churches teach some truth — the remnant holds the _complete system_ of
    truth revealed in the three angels' messages
- The remnant is not defined by sincerity alone — it is defined by the _content
  of its message_ and the _marks of its identity_
  - [WB]: Write "SINCERITY (many churches) ≠ PROPHETIC IDENTITY (specific
    marks)" (Rev 12:17)
- [Aside]: Many doctors are sincere and skilled. But if you need brain surgery,
  you go to the neurosurgeon — not because other doctors are bad, but because the
  specialist has what you specifically need. The remnant carries the specific
  message for this specific time.

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — The Weight of Identity (5 min)

### A. Privilege Brings Responsibility

- Luke 12:48 — "For unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required"
  - [WB]: Write "MUCH GIVEN → MUCH REQUIRED" (Luke 12:48)
  - Remnant identity is not a badge to wear — it is a burden to bear
- The remnant exists _for the world_ — not for itself
  - The three angels' messages must reach every nation, kindred, tongue, and
    people (Rev 14:6)
  - We exist to give this message — if we don't, who will?

### B. Living Worthy of the Calling

- Ephesians 4:1 — "Walk worthy of the vocation wherewith ye are called"
- The remnant must be what it preaches:
  - Commandment-keepers who actually keep the commandments in love
  - Spirit of Prophecy believers who actually read and follow the counsel
  - Righteousness-by-faith preachers who actually live by faith
  - Health message advocates who actually honour their bodies
- [RQ]: If the remnant doesn't live what it teaches, has it truly received the
  message or merely memorized it? (Eph 4:1)
- [EGW]: "The world is watching with intense interest to see what fruit is borne
  by professed Christians." (9T 20.1, paraphrased)

### C. The Final Call

- Revelation 18:1–4 — The angel that lightens the whole earth with glory
  - This is the latter rain — the final power for the final message
  - The remnant's message, empowered by the Holy Spirit, will reach every soul
- God is preparing a people — not perfect by their own effort, but perfected
  by Christ's indwelling power — to carry this message and stand through the
  final crisis

### Call to Action — Accept the Responsibility

- **Own** your prophetic identity — not with pride but with humility and
  gratitude (Deut 7:7–8; Luke 12:48)
- **Live** what you profess — the remnant is defined not just by doctrines held
  but by a character transformed (Eph 4:1)
- **Share** the message — every distinctive you've studied in this series
  exists to be proclaimed to the world (Rev 14:6)
- **Prepare** for the latter rain — seek the Holy Spirit's power to carry the
  final message with love and courage (Joel 2:28–29; Rev 18:1)
- **Resolve**: _I accept my place in God's end-time movement — not because I
  deserve it, but because He called me. I will live and proclaim the full
  message, whatever the cost._
