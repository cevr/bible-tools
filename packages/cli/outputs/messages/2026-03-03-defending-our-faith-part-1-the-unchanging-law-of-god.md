## Section 1 — The Law Before Sinai (8 min)

_Addresses: "Only for the Jews," "Didn't exist before Moses"_

### A. Evidence from Creation

- Genesis 2:1–3 — God blessed and sanctified the seventh day
  - No Jew existed. No nation existed. Only Adam and Eve — representatives of
    all humanity
  - [WB]: Timeline: CREATION (Sabbath instituted) → FALL → PATRIARCHS → SINAI
    (Law codified)
  - [RQ]: For whom was the Sabbath made if it was instituted before any nation
    existed? (Gen 2:1–3; Mark 2:27)
  - Mark 2:27 — "The sabbath was made for man" — _anthropos_, not _Ioudaios_

### B. Evidence from the Patriarchs

- Genesis 4:7 — "Sin lieth at the door"
  - Sin requires a standard; "where no law is, there is no transgression" (Rom
    4:15)
  - Cain knew the standard and violated it
- Genesis 26:5 — "Abraham obeyed my voice, and kept my charge, my commandments,
  my statutes, and my laws"
  - [RQ]: How could Abraham keep God's commandments and laws if they didn't exist
    until Moses? (Gen 26:5)
  - Four Hebrew terms used: _mishmereth_ (charge), _mitsvah_ (commandments),
    _chuqqah_ (statutes), _torah_ (laws)
  - This is comprehensive — Abraham lived by the full moral standard

### C. Evidence from Sinai's Distinction

- At Sinai, God did not _create_ the law — He _codified_ it
- Two categories, deliberately separated:
  - [WB]: Draw two columns: STONE TABLETS (Moral Law — God's finger, Ex 31:18)
    vs. BOOK (Ceremonial Law — Moses' hand, Deut 31:24–26)
  - The tablets placed _inside_ the ark (Deut 10:2) — the book placed _beside_
    it (Deut 31:26)
  - The tablets reflect God's eternal character; the book pointed forward to
    Christ's sacrifice
- [EB]: Colossians 2:14 — The "handwriting of ordinances" nailed to the cross is
  the ceremonial law (Moses' book), not the Decalogue (God's tablets)
  - The Greek _cheirographon_ = handwritten document — matches Moses' handwriting,
    not God's finger
- [EGW]: "The law of God existed before the creation of man or else Adam could
  not have sinned... After the transgression of Adam the principles of the law
  were not changed, but were definitely arranged and expressed to meet man in his
  fallen condition." (1SM 220.3)

## Section 2 — Christ, the Law-Giver and Law-Keeper (10 min) [*]

_Addresses: "Jesus fulfilled the law so it's gone," "Jesus never said to keep
the Sabbath"_

### A. "Fulfil" Does Not Mean "Abolish"

- Matthew 5:17–18 — Central Verse
  - Greek _pleroo_ (#4137) = to make full, to fill up, to perfectly exemplify
  - [WB]: Write _PLEROO_ = "FILL FULL" ≠ "TERMINATE"
  - A glass of water is "fulfilled" when it is filled to the brim — not when it
    is shattered
  - [RQ]: If Jesus intended to abolish the law, why did He say not even a jot or
    tittle would pass until heaven and earth pass? (Matt 5:18)
- Matthew 5:19 — "Whosoever therefore shall break one of these least
  commandments, and shall teach men so, he shall be called the least in the
  kingdom of heaven"
  - Jesus connects breaking _and teaching others to break_ — a warning to
    preachers who dismiss the law
- Isaiah 42:21 — "He will magnify the law, and make it honourable"
  - Christ didn't shrink the law — He expanded its reach (Matt 5:21–22, 27–28)

### B. Christ Upheld the Sabbath

- Luke 4:16 — "As his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day"
  - _Ethos_ (#1485) = established habit, regular practice
  - Not cultural accommodation — deliberate example
- Mark 2:27–28 — "The sabbath was made for man... the Son of man is Lord also of
  the sabbath"
  - He is Lord _of_ the Sabbath — its rightful owner and interpreter, not its
    destroyer
  - [RQ]: Does a Lord abolish what He owns, or does He restore it to its true
    purpose? (Mark 2:28)
- Matthew 12:12 — "It is lawful to do well on the sabbath days"
  - He corrected Pharisaical distortion, not the Sabbath itself
- Matthew 24:20 — "Pray that your flight be not... on the sabbath day"
  - Spoken of Jerusalem's fall in AD 70 — nearly 40 years after the cross
  - [WB]: Write "AD 31 (Cross) → AD 70 (Sabbath still binding)" (Matt 24:20)
  - If the Sabbath ended at the cross, this instruction makes no sense
- [EB]: Luke 23:56 — The women "rested the sabbath day according to the
  commandment" — between the crucifixion and the resurrection

### C. Faith Establishes the Law

- Romans 3:31 — "Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we
  establish the law"
  - [WB]: Write "FAITH → ESTABLISHES LAW" (not abolishes) (Rom 3:31)
  - Paul is emphatic: _me genoito_ — "may it never be!" — the strongest negation
    in Greek
  - [RQ]: If faith makes the law void, why does Paul use the strongest possible
    denial? (Rom 3:31)
- [Aside]: A good driver doesn't abolish traffic laws by obeying them — he
  demonstrates their purpose. Christ, by His perfect life, vindicated the law.

## Section 3 — The Law Written on the Heart (7 min)

_Addresses: "Law in the heart = no need for deliberate keeping," "Old Covenant
vs. New Covenant"_

### A. The New Covenant Promise

- Hebrews 8:10 (quoting Jeremiah 31:33) — "I will put my laws into their mind,
  and write them in their hearts"
  - [WB]: Write "OLD COVENANT: Law on stone (external)" → "NEW COVENANT: Same
    law on heart (internal)" (Heb 8:10)
  - The law didn't change — the _location_ changed
  - [RQ]: Does having the law written on the heart mean the standard changes, or
    that our disposition toward that unchanging standard is transformed? (Heb
    8:10)
  - The problem was never the law — "the law is holy, and the commandment holy,
    and just, and good" (Rom 7:12)
  - The problem was the human heart — "the carnal mind is enmity against God"
    (Rom 8:7)

### B. Divine Power for Obedience

- Ezekiel 36:26–27 — "A new heart also will I give you... and I will put my
  spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes"
  - Not "try harder" — divine enablement
  - The Spirit _causes_ obedience — the law becomes the believer's delight
- [EB]: Psalm 40:8 — "I delight to do thy will, O my God: yea, thy law is within
  my heart" — Messianic prophecy; Christ's own experience with the law
- [EGW]: "The law of Ten Commandments is not to be looked upon as much from the
  prohibitory side, as from the mercy side. Its prohibitions are the sure
  guarantee of happiness in obedience." (BE, August 15, 1898, par. 5)

### C. Obedience as Evidence of Relationship

- 1 John 2:3–4 — "Hereby we do know that we know him, if we keep his
  commandments. He that saith, I know him, and keepeth not his commandments, is a
  liar"
  - John gives no middle ground — knowing Christ and keeping His commandments are
    inseparable
  - [WB]: Write "KNOW HIM = KEEP COMMANDMENTS" (1 John 2:3–4)
- 1 John 5:3 — "This is the love of God, that we keep his commandments: and his
  commandments are not grievous"
  - Love is not abstract feeling — it is expressed in obedience
  - And for the Spirit-filled believer, obedience is not a burden
- [EB]: Daniel 7:25 — A power would "think to change times and laws" — the very
  attack we see in the claim that the law was abolished or changed
  - Prophecy predicted the attack on God's law — our defense is not paranoia but
    prophetic awareness

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — The Line Is Drawn (5 min)

### A. The Law in the Final Conflict

- Revelation 14:12 — "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that
  keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus"
  - [WB]: Write "COMMANDMENTS OF GOD + FAITH OF JESUS = LAST-DAY SAINTS" (Rev
    14:12)
  - Two marks of God's end-time people: commandment-keeping and faith
  - Not either/or — both
- Revelation 12:17 — "The dragon was wroth with the woman... which keep the
  commandments of God"
  - Satan's rage is specifically directed at commandment-keepers
  - [RQ]: Why would Satan target people for keeping a law that was supposedly
    abolished? (Rev 12:17)

### B. Not Legalism — Love

- The law is not a means to earn salvation (Eph 2:8–9)
- It is the standard of conduct for those already saved by grace
- For the renewed heart, obedience is not a burden but a joy (Psa 119:35, 47, 97)
- [WB]: Write "GRACE saves → LAW guides → SPIRIT empowers" (Eph 2:8; Psa
  119:35; Ezek 36:27)
- [EGW]: "Those who are living upon the earth when the intercession of Christ
  shall cease in the sanctuary above are to stand in the sight of a holy God
  without a mediator. Their robes must be spotless, their characters must be
  purified from sin by the blood of sprinkling." (GC 425.1)

### Call to Action — Stand on the Unchanging Word

- **Embrace** God's law not as a legalistic code but as an expression of His
  character and love (1 John 5:3; Psa 19:7)
- **Receive** the new heart that makes obedience the natural outflow of a
  transformed life (Ezek 36:26–27)
- **Reject** the claim that the law was abolished — Scripture gives no support
  for it (Matt 5:17–18; Rom 3:31)
- **Prepare** to stand among those who keep the commandments of God and the faith
  of Jesus (Rev 14:12)
- **Resolve**: _My life will honor every precept of God's eternal law, by His
  grace and for His glory_
