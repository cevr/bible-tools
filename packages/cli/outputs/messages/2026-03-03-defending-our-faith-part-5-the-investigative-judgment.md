## Section 1 — The Biblical Basis for Pre-Advent Judgment (10 min)

_Addresses: "The investigative judgment is not in the Bible"_

### A. Daniel 7 — Judgment Before the Kingdom

- Daniel 7:9–10 — The judgment scene
  - Thrones set up, Ancient of Days takes His seat
  - Books opened — a legal proceeding
  - [WB]: Write "JUDGMENT SET → BOOKS OPENED" (Dan 7:9–10)
- Daniel 7:13–14 — _After_ the judgment, the Son of man comes to the Ancient of
  Days and receives the kingdom
  - [WB]: Write 'JUDGMENT (v. 9–10) → SON OF MAN RECEIVES KINGDOM (v. 13–14) →
SAINTS POSSESS KINGDOM (v. 22, 27)'
  - The sequence is clear: judgment _first_, kingdom _second_
  - This judgment happens in heaven — the Son of man comes to the Ancient of
    Days, not to earth
  - [RQ]: If the judgment in Daniel 7 occurs before the Son of man receives His
    kingdom, how can it be a post-advent event? (Dan 7:9–14)

### B. The Judgment Determines Who Inherits the Kingdom

- Daniel 7:22 — "Judgment was given to the saints of the most High; and the time
  came that the saints possessed the kingdom"
  - Judgment given _in favour of_ the saints — it is a verdict, not a threat
  - [WB]: Write "JUDGMENT = VERDICT FOR THE SAINTS" (Dan 7:22)
- Matthew 22:11–14 — Parable of the wedding feast
  - The king inspects the guests _before_ the feast begins
  - One guest found without the wedding garment is cast out
  - [RQ]: If the king inspects guests before the feast, does Christ examine
    professed believers before His return? (Matt 22:11–13)
  - The wedding garment = Christ's righteousness (Isa 61:10; Rev 19:8)
  - The inspection is not to find fault — it is to confirm who has received the
    garment

### C. Scripture Repeatedly Teaches Pre-Advent Judgment

- Ecclesiastes 12:14 — "God shall bring every work into judgment, with every
  secret thing, whether it be good, or whether it be evil"
- Acts 17:31 — "He hath appointed a day, in the which he will judge the world in
  righteousness"
  - _Appointed_ — a set time, not indefinite
- 1 Peter 4:17 — "The time is come that judgment must begin at the house of God"
  - Judgment begins with _God's professed people_ — not the world first
  - [WB]: Write "JUDGMENT BEGINS AT THE HOUSE OF GOD" (1 Pet 4:17)
  - [RQ]: If judgment begins with believers, when does this occur — before or
    after Christ returns? (1 Pet 4:17)
- Revelation 14:7 — "The hour of his judgment is come"
  - Present tense — _is come_ — not "will come someday"
  - This is the first angel's message — announced to the world as a present
    reality
- [EB]: Malachi 3:1–3 — "The Lord... shall suddenly come to his temple...
  he shall sit as a refiner and purifier of silver: and he shall purify the
  sons of Levi"
  - Christ comes to His temple (sanctuary) to purify His people — judgment
    language

### D. The Books of Heaven

- The judgment involves records:
  - Book of Life (Phil 4:3; Rev 3:5; 20:12)
  - Book of Remembrance (Mal 3:16)
  - Record of deeds (Rev 20:12 — "judged out of those things which were written
    in the books")
  - [WB]: Write "BOOKS: Life / Remembrance / Deeds" (Dan 7:10; Rev 20:12)
- Not because God forgets — but because the universe is watching
  - The judgment is transparent. Every being sees that God is just.
- [EGW]: "The judgment is now passing in the sanctuary above. For many years this
  work has been in progress. Soon—none know how soon—it will pass to the cases
  of the living." (GC 490.1)

## Section 2 — Answering the Objections (8 min) [*]

_Addresses: "If Christ paid it all, why investigate?" and "This is legalism"_

### A. "Christ Paid It All — Why a Judgment?"

- Christ's death provides _provision_ for all; the judgment reveals who has
  _accepted_ that provision
  - [WB]: Write "CROSS = provision for ALL" → "JUDGMENT = who accepted it?"
  - A scholarship paid in full still requires a student to enroll
- The judgment does not question the sufficiency of Christ's sacrifice — it
  examines whether individuals have received it
  - John 3:18 — "He that believeth on him is not condemned: but he that believeth
    not is condemned already"
  - Belief must be genuine — the judgment distinguishes authentic faith from
    mere profession (Matt 7:21–23)
- [RQ]: If many will say "Lord, Lord" and be turned away (Matt 7:22–23), at what
  point are genuine and false believers distinguished? (Matt 22:11–14)

### B. "This Is Just Legalism"

- The judgment is not about earning salvation — it is about _vindicating God's
  decisions_
  - When God saves someone, the universe must see that the decision was just
  - When God excludes someone, the universe must see that person was given every
    opportunity
- The judgment demonstrates that God is fair, not arbitrary
  - Romans 3:4 — "That thou mightest be justified in thy sayings, and mightest
    overcome when thou art judged"
  - God Himself submits to examination — He invites scrutiny of His justice
  - [WB]: Write "GOD ON TRIAL → judgment vindicates HIS character" (Rom 3:4)
- The basis of judgment is not perfect performance — it is relationship with
  Christ
  - 1 John 2:1 — "If any man sin, we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus
    Christ the righteous"
  - Christ is the defense attorney, not the prosecutor

### C. "No Other Christians Believe This"

- The _label_ "investigative judgment" is Adventist — the _concept_ is biblical
- Any theology that includes a judgment _before_ rewards are given teaches
  pre-advent judgment
  - 2 Corinthians 5:10 — "We must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ"
  - Revelation 22:12 — "My reward is with me, to give every man according as his
    work shall be"
  - If Christ brings rewards _with_ Him when He comes, the determination of
    rewards happened _before_ He comes
  - [RQ]: If Christ brings rewards at His coming (Rev 22:12), when were those
    rewards determined? (Dan 7:9–10)

## Section 3 — Judgment and Assurance (8 min)

_Addresses: "Judgment destroys assurance"_

### A. The Judgment Is Good News for the Believer

- Daniel 7:22 — "Judgment was given to the saints" — the verdict favours God's
  people
  - [WB]: Write "JUDGMENT = VINDICATION" (Dan 7:22)
- Romans 8:1 — "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in
  Christ Jesus"
  - If you are _in Christ_, the judgment cannot condemn you
- Romans 8:33–34 — "Who shall lay any thing to the charge of God's elect? It is
  God that justifieth. Who is he that condemneth? It is Christ that died, yea
  rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God, who also
  maketh intercession for us"
  - [WB]: Write "ACCUSER: Satan charges → ADVOCATE: Christ intercedes → VERDICT:
    No condemnation" (Rom 8:33–34)
  - Satan accuses — Christ intercedes — the verdict stands in your favour

### B. The Problem Is Not Judgment — It Is False Security

- The people who should fear judgment are those who _profess_ Christ but live
  in unrepented sin
  - Matthew 7:21–23 — "Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord"
  - These are not atheists — they are religious people who prophesied and cast
    out demons
- The judgment is not threatening to the genuinely converted — it is threatening
  to the self-deceived
  - [RQ]: Is it the judgment that is the problem, or the false assurance that
    says "I'm saved" while living in known sin? (Matt 7:21–23)
- Hebrews 4:16 — "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we
  may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need"
  - The judgment seat is also the throne of grace — approach with confidence

### C. Assurance Through Abiding

- 1 John 2:28 — "And now, little children, abide in him; that, when he shall
  appear, we may have confidence, and not be ashamed before him at his coming"
  - Confidence at His coming requires _abiding_ in Him now
  - The investigative judgment is not a surprise exam — it is a confirmation of
    an ongoing relationship
- [EGW]: "All who have truly repented of sin, and by faith claimed the blood of
  Christ as their atoning sacrifice, have had pardon entered against their names
  in the books of heaven; as they have become partakers of the righteousness of
  Christ, and their characters are found to be in harmony with the law of God,
  their sins will be blotted out, and they themselves will be accounted worthy of
  eternal life." (GC 483.1)
- [Aside]: A student who studied all semester doesn't fear the final exam — the
  exam simply confirms what they already know. The judgment confirms what God
  already knows about those who abide in Christ.

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — The Judgment Vindicates God and His People (5 min)

### A. The Cosmic Purpose of the Judgment

- The judgment is not _primarily_ for God's benefit — He knows who are His (2
  Tim 2:19)
- It is for the watching universe
  - Every saved person will be seen to have been saved justly
  - Every lost person will be seen to have been given every opportunity
  - [WB]: Write "THE UNIVERSE WATCHES → GOD'S JUSTICE DEMONSTRATED" (Rom 3:4;
    1 Cor 4:9)
- After the millennium, even the wicked acknowledge God's justice (Phil 2:10–11;
  Rev 15:3–4)
  - The judgment ensures that sin never rises again (Nah 1:9)

### B. The Hour of His Judgment _Is_ Come

- Revelation 14:7 — This is our present reality
  - Not a future threat — a present ministry
  - Christ is working _for_ you in the Most Holy Place right now
- The judgment should produce:
  - Sobriety, not terror (2 Cor 5:10–11)
  - Heart-searching, not despair (Lev 23:29)
  - Confidence, not anxiety (Rom 8:1; 1 John 2:28)
  - Urgency, not complacency (Heb 3:13 — "while it is called To day")

### Call to Action — Face the Judgment with Confidence

- **Trust** your Advocate — Christ is interceding for you right now, not
  standing against you (1 John 2:1; Heb 7:25)
- **Abide** in Christ — the judgment confirms the relationship, not replaces it
  (1 John 2:28)
- **Search** your heart — the antitypical Day of Atonement calls for honest
  self-examination, not self-torture (Lev 23:29; 2 Cor 13:5)
- **Rejoice** that God's justice will be vindicated — every accusation of Satan
  will be answered, and you will stand acquitted (Dan 7:22; Rom 8:33–34)
- **Resolve**: _I will live as one whose case is before the court of heaven —
  not in fear, but in the confidence of Christ's righteousness covering me_
