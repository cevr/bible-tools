## Section 1 — What Righteousness by Faith Actually Means (10 min)

_Addresses: "This is works salvation," "You're adding to the gospel"_

### A. Righteousness Is Christ's, Not Ours

- Romans 3:21–22 — "The righteousness of God which is by faith of Jesus Christ
  unto all and upon all them that believe"
  - Righteousness is _of God_ — it originates with Him, not with human effort
  - It comes _by faith_ — received, not earned
  - [WB]: Write "RIGHTEOUSNESS: Source = GOD / Channel = FAITH / Recipient =
    BELIEVER" (Rom 3:21–22)
- Philippians 3:9 — "Not having mine own righteousness, which is of the law,
  but that which is through the faith of Christ, the righteousness which is of
  God by faith"
  - Paul explicitly rejects self-generated righteousness
  - [RQ]: If Paul rejected his own righteousness, how can we be accused of
    teaching works salvation when we say the same? (Phil 3:9)

### B. Two Dimensions of One Righteousness

- **Imputed righteousness (justification)** — Christ's righteousness credited to
  our account
  - Romans 4:5 — "His faith is counted for righteousness"
  - 2 Corinthians 5:21 — "Made him to be sin for us... that we might be made
    the righteousness of God in him"
  - [WB]: Write "IMPUTED = Christ's record COVERS me" (Rom 4:5)
- **Imparted righteousness (sanctification)** — Christ's character reproduced
  in us
  - Galatians 2:20 — "Christ liveth in me"
  - Philippians 2:13 — "It is God which worketh in you both to will and to do
    of his good pleasure"
  - [WB]: Write "IMPARTED = Christ's character TRANSFORMS me" (Gal 2:20; Phil
    2:13)
- Both are by faith. Both are gifts. Neither is earned.
  - Imputation without impartation = legal fiction
  - Impartation without imputation = legalism
  - [WB]: Write "IMPUTED + IMPARTED = full gospel" — both by faith, both by
    grace
- [EGW]: "The righteousness by which we are justified is imputed; the
  righteousness by which we are sanctified is imparted. The first is our title
  to heaven, the second is our fitness for heaven." (MYP 35.2)

### C. The 1888 Message

- At the 1888 General Conference, A.T. Jones and E.J. Waggoner brought a
  message emphasizing:
  - Christ's all-sufficient righteousness received by faith
  - The power of Christ to overcome sin _in the believer_
  - The law and the gospel working together, not against each other
- Ellen White endorsed the message: "The Lord in His great mercy sent a most
  precious message to His people... presenting justification through faith in
  the Surety; it invited the people to receive the righteousness of Christ,
  which is made manifest in obedience to all the commandments of God." (TM
  91.2)
- [WB]: Write "1888: Righteousness by faith → obedience to all commandments →
  NOT legalism but the power of Christ" (TM 91.2)
- [RQ]: If righteousness by faith produces obedience to all the commandments, is
  it legalism or the gospel working to its fullest? (TM 91.2)

## Section 2 — Character Perfection: Biblical, Not Legalistic (10 min)

_Addresses: "Sinless perfectionism," "Impossible standard"_

### A. What the Bible Teaches About Perfection

- Matthew 5:48 — "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in
  heaven is perfect"
  - _Teleios_ (#5046) = complete, mature, having reached the intended goal
  - Not absolute perfection (equal to God) — but _completeness of character
    development_
  - [WB]: Write "TELEIOS = complete, mature, reaching the goal" (Matt 5:48)
  - A ripe apple is "perfect" — it has reached the purpose for which it was
    grown
- Philippians 3:12–14 — Paul says "not as though I had already attained" but
  presses "toward the mark"
  - Perfection is a _direction_ and a _process_, not a static claim
  - [WB]: Write "PRESSING → toward the mark → prize of the high calling" (Phil
    3:14)
- Hebrews 6:1 — "Let us go on unto perfection" — _teleiotes_ = maturity,
  completeness
  - The author of Hebrews _commands_ progress toward perfection

### B. Christ Made It Possible in Human Flesh

- Romans 8:3–4 — "God sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh, and
  for sin, condemned sin in the flesh: That the righteousness of the law might be
  fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit"
  - [WB]: Write 'CONDEMNED SIN IN THE FLESH → righteousness FULFILLED IN US'
    (Rom 8:3–4)
  - Christ took human nature to prove that obedience is possible through divine
    power in human flesh
  - [RQ]: If God condemned sin in the flesh _so that_ the law's righteousness
    would be fulfilled _in us_, is it presumptuous to believe this promise or
    faithless to deny it? (Rom 8:3–4)
- Hebrews 2:14, 16–18 — Christ took "the seed of Abraham" — human nature — and
  was "in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin" (Heb 4:15)
  - He overcame in the same nature we possess — then offers to live in us by
    His Spirit
- Revelation 3:21 — "To him that overcometh will I grant to sit with me in my
  throne, even as I also overcame"
  - "As I also overcame" — He overcame in human nature; we overcome through Him

### C. Character Maturity, Not Sinless Claims

- Character perfection is not:
  - Claiming to be without sin (1 John 1:8 — "If we say that we have no sin, we
    deceive ourselves")
  - Living without temptation
  - Absolute moral perfection equal to God
  - Earning salvation through behaviour
- Character perfection _is_:
  - Complete surrender to Christ's indwelling (Gal 2:20)
  - Victory over known sin through the Spirit (Rom 8:13)
  - A fully mature, Christ-reflecting character (Eph 4:13 — "the measure of the
    stature of the fulness of Christ")
  - A settled disposition that will not choose sin even under extreme pressure
  - [WB]: Write "PERFECTION ≠ sinless claims" / "PERFECTION = settled character
    fully surrendered to Christ"
- [EGW]: "Christ is waiting with longing desire for the manifestation of Himself
  in His church. When the character of Christ shall be perfectly reproduced in
  His people, then He will come to claim them as His own." (COL 69.1)
- [Aside]: A child learning piano eventually plays without conscious effort. It's
  not that they became incapable of hitting wrong notes — it's that practice and
  skill made right playing _natural_. Character perfection is the gospel so
  deeply internalized that righteousness becomes second nature.

## Section 3 — Answering the Objections (6 min) [*]

_Addresses: "If we could be perfect, we don't need Jesus" and "This creates
anxiety"_

### A. "If We Could Be Perfect, We Wouldn't Need Jesus"

- This misunderstands the claim entirely
  - We are never perfect _apart from_ Christ — we are perfect _because of_
    Christ in us
  - The branch does not bear fruit without the vine (John 15:4–5)
  - [WB]: Write "APART FROM ME: nothing" / "IN ME: much fruit" (John 15:5)
  - The more mature the character, the more _dependent_ on Christ, not less
- It's like saying "If medicine heals you, you don't need the doctor" — the
  healing _is_ the doctor's work

### B. "This Creates Anxiety, Not Assurance"

- Anxiety comes from self-focus: "Am I good enough?"
- Assurance comes from Christ-focus: "Is _He_ powerful enough?"
  - Jude 1:24 — "_He_ is able to keep you from falling"
  - Philippians 1:6 — "He which hath begun a good work in you _will_ perform
    it until the day of Jesus Christ"
  - [WB]: Write "ANXIETY: Am I good enough?" vs. "ASSURANCE: Is He able?" (Jude
    1:24; Phil 1:6)
- The doctrine of character perfection properly understood produces _more_
  assurance, not less
  - Because the promise is that _God_ will finish the work — not that _you_
    must finish it
- [RQ]: If God says He will complete the work He started in you, is it faith or
  unbelief to say He cannot? (Phil 1:6)

### C. "This Is Just Last Generation Theology — A Fringe View"

- The belief that God's last-day people will reflect Christ's character fully is
  not fringe — it is central to the Great Controversy narrative
  - Job was tested to vindicate God's character — the 144,000 undergo the same
    test on a cosmic scale
  - Revelation 14:5 — "In their mouth was found no guile: for they are without
    fault before the throne of God"
  - [WB]: Write "JOB → 144,000 → vindication of God's character" (Job 1:8; Rev
    14:5)
- This is not about human achievement — it is about divine power demonstrated
  through surrendered vessels
- [EGW]: "Those who are living upon the earth when the intercession of Christ
  shall cease in the sanctuary above are to stand in the sight of a holy God
  without a mediator." (GC 425.1)

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — Christ in You, the Hope of Glory (5 min)

### A. The Gospel Taken to Its Conclusion

- Colossians 1:27 — "Christ in you, the hope of glory"
  - [WB]: Write "CHRIST IN YOU = the hope of glory" (Col 1:27)
  - The gospel is not just forgiveness — it is transformation
  - The gospel is not just a past event (the cross) — it is a present process
    (sanctification) and a future consummation (glorification)
- Romans 8:29 — "Whom he did foreknow, he also did predestinate to be conformed
  to the image of his Son"
  - Conformed to Christ's image — this is the goal. This is what God is doing.

### B. The Final Generation Will Demonstrate the Gospel

- 1 John 3:2–3 — "When he shall appear, we shall be like him; for we shall see
  him as he is. And every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even
  as he is pure"
  - Seeing Christ produces likeness to Him
  - The hope of His coming motivates purification
- The 144,000 are not super-humans — they are deeply surrendered believers
  through whom Christ vindicates His character before the universe
  - Same pattern as Job: God points to His people and says "Have you considered
    my servants?"
- The Great Controversy ends when God's character is fully vindicated — and that
  vindication comes through lives transformed by grace

### Call to Action — Let Christ Complete His Work

- **Surrender** daily to Christ's indwelling — perfection is not self-effort but
  self-emptying (Gal 2:20)
- **Believe** God's promise — He _will_ complete the work He started in you
  (Phil 1:6; Jude 1:24)
- **Press** toward the mark — not in anxiety but in the confidence that Christ
  overcame in your nature and lives in you now (Phil 3:14; Rev 3:21)
- **Expect** transformation — the gospel is the power of God unto salvation, not
  just forgiveness but complete restoration (Rom 1:16; Rom 8:29)
- **Resolve**: _I will stop asking "Can I be perfect?" and start asking "Can
  Christ finish what He started in me?" — and the answer is yes._
