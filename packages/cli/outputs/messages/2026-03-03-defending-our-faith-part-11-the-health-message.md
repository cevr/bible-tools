## Section 1 — God Cares About the Body (8 min)

_Addresses: "Diet has nothing to do with religion," "The body doesn't matter
spiritually"_

### A. The Body Is God's Temple

- 1 Corinthians 6:19–20 — "Your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost"
  - _Naos_ (#3485) = the inner sanctuary, the holy place — the dwelling of God
  - [WB]: Write "YOUR BODY = TEMPLE (naos) of the Holy Spirit" (1 Cor 6:19)
  - In Part 4 we studied the sanctuary — now the same language is applied to
    your physical body
  - If the earthly sanctuary was treated with reverence, how should we treat the
    temple of the Spirit?
  - [RQ]: If defiling God's ancient temple brought judgment, what does defiling
    the temple of your body invite? (1 Cor 3:17)
- 1 Corinthians 3:16–17 — "If any man defile the temple of God, him shall God
  destroy; for the temple of God is holy, which temple ye are"
  - Strong language — _phtheiro_ (#5351) = to corrupt, to ruin
  - God takes the body-temple seriously

### B. Holistic Anthropology — The Bible's View of Humanity

- In Part 7 we established: the soul is not separable from the body (Gen 2:7)
  - DUST + BREATH = LIVING SOUL
  - If the body and soul are a unity, then what affects the body affects the mind
    and spirit
  - [WB]: Write "BODY ↔ MIND ↔ SPIRIT = one integrated being" (Gen 2:7; 1
    Thess 5:23)
- 1 Thessalonians 5:23 — "The very God of peace sanctify you wholly; and I pray
  God your whole spirit and soul and body be preserved blameless"
  - _Holoteleis_ (#3651) = complete in every part
  - Sanctification is _whole-person_ — it cannot bypass the body
- Romans 12:1 — "Present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto
  God, which is your reasonable service"
  - _Logike latreia_ = rational worship, logical service
  - Bodily stewardship is an act of worship — not a cultural extra

### C. The Mind-Body Connection

- Daniel 1:8, 15–17 — Daniel refused the king's diet and chose a plant-based
  diet
  - After ten days: healthier in appearance
  - After three years: "ten times better" in wisdom and understanding
  - [WB]: Write "DANIEL'S DIET → clearer mind → deeper wisdom" (Dan 1:15, 17)
  - [RQ]: If Daniel's diet affected his mental clarity and spiritual
    discernment, does diet matter for the final generation that needs maximum
    spiritual clarity? (Dan 1:17)
- What we eat and drink directly affects our ability to:
  - Think clearly
  - Discern truth from error
  - Resist temptation
  - Hear the Spirit's voice
- [EGW]: "Anything that lessens physical strength enfeebles the mind and makes
  it less capable of discriminating between right and wrong." (COL 346.1)

## Section 2 — The Biblical Diet: From Eden to the End (10 min)

_Addresses: "Clean/unclean is Old Testament only," "Jesus declared all foods
clean"_

### A. God's Original Diet (Creation)

- Genesis 1:29 — "I have given you every herb bearing seed... and every tree, in
  the which is the fruit of a tree yielding seed; to you it shall be for meat
  [food]"
  - [WB]: Write "EDEN DIET: fruits, grains, nuts, seeds" (Gen 1:29)
  - No animal flesh in the original diet — this was God's ideal
  - Genesis 1:30 — Animals also ate plants — no death in the original creation
- After the fall, "herbs of the field" added (Gen 3:18) — vegetables
- This was the diet for over 1600 years (Creation to the Flood)
  - [RQ]: If God designed the human body and prescribed its original diet, does
    He know what fuel works best? (Gen 1:29)

### B. After the Flood — Clean and Unclean

- Genesis 9:3 — After the flood, God permitted animal flesh
  - Vegetation destroyed; animals allowed as emergency provision
  - But with restriction: "the blood... shall ye not eat" (Gen 9:4)
- Genesis 7:2 — Noah took clean animals by sevens, unclean by twos
  - The clean/unclean distinction existed _before_ Moses and _before_ Israel
  - [WB]: Write 'CLEAN/UNCLEAN distinction: NOAH (Gen 7:2) → MOSES (Lev 11) →
still valid'
  - [RQ]: If the clean/unclean distinction predates the Mosaic law, how can it
    be dismissed as ceremonial? (Gen 7:2)
- Leviticus 11 — Codified what was already known:
  - Clean land animals: split hoof _and_ chew cud (v. 3)
  - Clean fish: fins _and_ scales (v. 9)
  - Unclean: pig, shellfish, catfish, scavenger birds, etc.
  - [WB]: List key clean/unclean categories from Leviticus 11

### C. The "Jesus Declared All Foods Clean" Argument

- Mark 7:19 — Some translations add "(Thus he declared all foods clean)"
  - This is a translators' interpretation, not Jesus' words
  - KJV reads: "Because it entereth not into his heart, but into the belly, and
    goeth out into the draught, purging all meats"
  - The context is eating with unwashed hands (v. 2–5) — a Pharisaical
    tradition, not a discussion about clean vs. unclean animals
  - [WB]: Write 'MARK 7 CONTEXT: unwashed hands (tradition) — NOT abolishing
Lev 11'
  - [RQ]: If Jesus abolished the clean/unclean distinction in Mark 7, why did
    Peter still consider unclean animals unclean years later? (Acts 10:14)

- Acts 10:14 — Peter's vision
  - Peter says "I have never eaten any thing that is common or unclean" — years
    after Christ's ascension
  - The vision's interpretation: "God hath shewed me that I should not call any
    _man_ common or unclean" (Acts 10:28)
  - [WB]: Write "ACTS 10: About PEOPLE, not PORK" (Acts 10:28)
  - The vision is about Gentile inclusion, not dietary permissiveness

- 1 Timothy 4:3–5 — "Commanding to abstain from meats, which God hath created
  to be received with thanksgiving"
  - "Which God hath created to be received" — not all animals were created to be
    food
  - "Sanctified by the word of God" — the word of God (Lev 11; Gen 1:29)
    specifies what is food
  - [RQ]: If food is sanctified by the word of God, and the word of God
    distinguishes clean from unclean, does this verse abolish the distinction or
    affirm it? (1 Tim 4:5)

### D. The End Times Confirm It

- Isaiah 66:15–17 — At Christ's return, God will judge "them that eat swine's
  flesh, and the abomination, and the mouse"
  - If the clean/unclean distinction was abolished, why does the prophet include
    eating unclean animals among the sins judged at the end?
  - [WB]: Write 'ISA 66:17 — eating swine/abominations → judgment at the end'
  - [RQ]: If God judges those who eat unclean things at His coming, has the
    distinction been abolished? (Isa 66:17)

## Section 3 — Answering the Objections (8 min) [*]

_Addresses: "What you eat doesn't affect salvation," "This is legalism"_

### A. "What You Eat Doesn't Affect Salvation"

- Correct: diet does not _earn_ salvation
- But neither does anything else we _do_ — salvation is by grace through faith
  (Eph 2:8–9)
- The question is not "Does diet save me?" but "Does my treatment of God's
  temple honour Him?"
  - [WB]: Write "DIET doesn't SAVE → but SAVED people honour God's temple" (1
    Cor 6:19–20)
- The same logic would dismiss the Sabbath ("keeping a day doesn't save you"),
  honesty ("not lying doesn't save you"), etc.
  - Obedience in any area is the _fruit_ of salvation, not its cause
- [RQ]: If honouring God with our bodies is "reasonable service" (Rom 12:1), is
  it legalism or worship? (Rom 12:1)

### B. "This Is Just Cultural — Adventists Are Food Legalists"

- The original diet was given in Eden — before any culture existed
- The clean/unclean distinction was given to Noah — before Israel existed
- These are not cultural conventions — they are creation ordinances
  - [WB]: Write "CREATION ORDINANCE → not cultural but universal" (Gen 1:29;
    7:2)
- Science increasingly confirms the health benefits of the Adventist lifestyle:
  - Adventist Health Studies (Loma Linda University) show significantly lower
    rates of heart disease, cancer, and diabetes among Adventists
  - Vegetarian/plant-based Adventists live 7–10 years longer on average
- [Aside]: When the rest of the world catches up to what the Bible taught 3,500
  years ago, maybe it's not legalism — it's wisdom.

### C. "Paul Said We Can Eat Anything"

- Romans 14:2–3 — "One believeth that he may eat all things: another, who is
  weak, eateth herbs"
  - Context: food offered to idols and conscience issues among Jewish and Gentile
    believers — not clean vs. unclean
  - Paul is addressing _scruples_ between believers, not abolishing God's
    dietary law
- 1 Corinthians 8 — Same context: food offered to idols
  - Paul's concern is Christian liberty vs. weak conscience, not a license to
    eat anything
- [RQ]: When Paul discusses food disputes, is the context always idol-offered
  food and conscience — or does he anywhere discuss the Leviticus 11
  clean/unclean classification? (Rom 14; 1 Cor 8)
  - He never addresses Leviticus 11 — because it was not in dispute. Jews and
    Gentile God-fearers both observed it.

### Call to Action — Honour God with Your Body

- **Acknowledge** that your body is God's temple — not your own possession (1 Cor

  defined what is food and what is not (Lev 11; Gen 7:2)

  mind (Dan 1:17)

  is His temple, my mind is His instrument, and my life is His witness._
