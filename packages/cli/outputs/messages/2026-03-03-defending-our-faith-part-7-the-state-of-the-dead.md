## Section 1 — What Is the Soul? (8 min)

_Addresses: "The soul is immortal and separable from the body"_

### A. Creation Defines the Soul

- Genesis 2:7 — "And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and
  breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul"
  - [WB]: Write 'DUST (body) + BREATH (spirit/ruach) = LIVING SOUL (nephesh)'
    (Gen 2:7)
  - Man does not _have_ a soul — man _is_ a soul
  - _Nephesh_ (#5315) = living being, creature — used also of animals (Gen 1:20,
    21, 24)
  - The "soul" is the whole person, not a detachable ghost
- [RQ]: If a "soul" is body plus breath, what happens when breath is removed?
  (Gen 2:7; Eccl 12:7)
- Ecclesiastes 12:7 — "Then shall the dust return to the earth as it was: and
  the spirit shall return unto God who gave it"
  - The _ruach_ (#7307) that returns to God is the breath of life — not a
    conscious entity
  - The same _ruach_ is in animals (Eccl 3:19–21)
  - [WB]: Write "DEATH = DUST returns to earth + BREATH returns to God" (Eccl
    12:7)

### B. Only God Has Immortality

- 1 Timothy 6:15–16 — God "only hath immortality, dwelling in the light which
  no man can approach unto"
  - _Monos_ (#3441) = alone, only — exclusive to God
  - [WB]: Write "IMMORTALITY belongs to GOD ALONE" (1 Tim 6:16)
  - If God _alone_ has immortality, humans do not inherently possess it
- 1 Corinthians 15:53–54 — "This mortal must put on immortality"
  - Immortality is _put on_ — received as a gift at the resurrection
  - It is not something we already have
  - [RQ]: If humans already possess immortal souls, why does Paul say immortality
    must be _put on_ at the resurrection? (1 Cor 15:53–54)

### C. The First Lie

- Genesis 2:17 — God said: "Thou shalt surely die"
- Genesis 3:4 — The serpent said: "Ye shall not surely die"
  - [WB]: Write "GOD: 'You will die'" vs. "SERPENT: 'You won't die'" (Gen 2:17;
    3:4)
  - Every doctrine that teaches humans are inherently immortal echoes the
    serpent, not God
- [EGW]: "The great original lie which he told to Eve in Eden — 'Ye shall not
  surely die' — was the first sermon ever preached on the immortality of the
  soul... and it is sounding from every pulpit in Christendom." (ST, May 19,
  1890, par. 4, paraphrased)

## Section 2 — What Happens at Death? (8 min)

_Addresses: "The soul goes to heaven or hell at death"_

### A. The Dead Know Nothing

- Ecclesiastes 9:5–6 — "The dead know not any thing"
  - No knowledge, no memory, no emotion, no participation in earthly life
  - [WB]: Write "THE DEAD: know nothing / no reward / no love, hatred, envy /
    no portion in anything" (Eccl 9:5–6)
- Psalm 146:4 — "His breath goeth forth, he returneth to his earth; in that very
  day his thoughts perish"
  - _Eshtonoth_ (#6250) = thoughts, plans — they perish at death
  - [RQ]: If thoughts perish at death, how can the dead be conscious in heaven
    or hell? (Psa 146:4)
- Psalm 115:17 — "The dead praise not the LORD, neither any that go down into
  silence"
  - If the righteous dead were in heaven, wouldn't they praise God? Their silence
    proves their unconsciousness.
- Psalm 6:5 — "For in death there is no remembrance of thee: in the grave who
  shall give thee thanks?"

### B. Jesus Called Death "Sleep"

- John 11:11–14 — "Our friend Lazarus sleepeth; but I go, that I may awake him
  out of sleep... Jesus said unto them plainly, Lazarus is dead."
  - Jesus equates death with sleep — unconsciousness awaiting awakening
  - [WB]: Write "DEATH = SLEEP" (John 11:11–14)
- Acts 7:60 — Stephen "fell asleep" (not "went to heaven")
- 1 Thessalonians 4:13 — "Them which are asleep"
  - Paul uses the same language — the dead sleep until the resurrection
- [RQ]: If Lazarus had been in heaven for four days, wouldn't his resurrection be
  a cruelty — pulling him from paradise back to this world? (John 11:43–44)
  - Lazarus says nothing about an afterlife experience — because there was none

### C. The "Problem Texts" Explained

- Luke 23:43 — "To day shalt thou be with me in paradise"
  - The comma placement is the issue. Original Greek had no punctuation.
  - Read: "Verily I say unto thee to day, thou shalt be with me in paradise"
  - Jesus did not go to paradise that day — He went to the grave (John 20:17 —
    "I am not yet ascended to my Father")
  - [WB]: Write "TODAY I TELL YOU → you will be with me in paradise" (Luke
    23:43)
- Philippians 1:23 — "Having a desire to depart, and to be with Christ"
  - Paul expresses desire, not a timeline
  - For the dead, the next conscious moment after death _is_ the resurrection —
    no perceived gap
  - [EB]: 2 Corinthians 5:8 — "Absent from the body... present with the Lord"
    — same principle. From the sleeper's perspective, death and resurrection are
    instantaneous.
- Revelation 6:9–10 — "Souls under the altar" crying out
  - Figurative language — Abel's blood "cried out" from the ground (Gen 4:10)
  - The cry for justice, not literal conscious souls

## Section 3 — Is Hell Eternal Torment? (10 min) [*]

_Addresses: "Hell must be eternal or God's justice is incomplete,"
"Annihilationism makes sin trivial"_

### A. The Wages of Sin Is Death, Not Eternal Life in Torment

- Romans 6:23 — "The wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life"
  - [WB]: Write "WAGES: death" vs. "GIFT: eternal life" (Rom 6:23)
  - Death is the opposite of eternal life — not a different _kind_ of eternal
    life
  - If the wicked live forever in hell, they have eternal life — just a miserable
    version of it
  - [RQ]: If eternal life is a _gift_ given only to the saved, on what basis do
    the wicked also receive eternal existence? (Rom 6:23)
- John 3:16 — "Whosoever believeth in him should not _perish_, but have
  everlasting life"
  - _Apollumi_ (#622) = to destroy utterly, to perish
  - The alternative to eternal life is _perishing_ — not eternal torment

### B. The Wicked Are Destroyed, Not Preserved

- Malachi 4:1 — "The day cometh, that shall burn as an oven; and all the proud,
  yea, and all that do wickedly, shall be stubble: and the day that cometh shall
  burn them up... it shall leave them neither root nor branch"
  - [WB]: Write "BURN UP → neither root nor branch" (Mal 4:1)
  - Stubble burns and is consumed — it does not burn forever
- Malachi 4:3 — "Ye shall tread down the wicked; for they shall be ashes under
  the soles of your feet"
  - Ashes — the final state of the wicked. Not writhing, but consumed.
- Psalm 37:10 — "For yet a little while, and the wicked shall not be"
- Psalm 37:20 — "The wicked shall perish... into smoke shall they consume away"
- Obadiah 1:16 — "They shall be as though they had not been"
  - [RQ]: How do you reconcile "as though they had not been" with the idea of
    eternal conscious torment? (Obad 1:16)
- 2 Peter 2:6 — Sodom and Gomorrah "condemned... with an overthrow, making them
  an ensample unto those that after should live ungodly"
  - Sodom is not still burning — it was reduced to ashes (Jude 1:7 — "suffering
    the vengeance of eternal fire")
  - "Eternal fire" = fire with eternal _results_, not eternal _duration_
  - [WB]: Write "ETERNAL FIRE = permanent results, not perpetual burning" (Jude
    1:7; 2 Pet 2:6)

### C. Revelation's Lake of Fire

- Revelation 20:9 — "Fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them"
  - _Katephagen_ (#2719) = consumed, devoured — not preserved
- Revelation 20:14 — "Death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the
  second death."
  - The lake of fire _is_ the second death — not eternal life in flames
  - Death itself is destroyed — if the wicked lived forever, death would not be
    destroyed
  - [WB]: Write "LAKE OF FIRE = SECOND DEATH" (Rev 20:14)
- Revelation 21:4 — "There shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying,
  neither shall there be any more pain"
  - If the wicked are eternally tormented, pain and crying never end — this
    promise is broken
  - [RQ]: Can God wipe away all tears while billions scream in agony forever?
    (Rev 21:4)

### D. The Character of God Is at Stake

- Eternal torment makes God worse than any human tyrant
  - Finite sin receiving infinite punishment is not justice — it is cruelty
  - [WB]: Write "FINITE SIN → INFINITE PUNISHMENT?" — does this reflect God's
    character?
- Jeremiah 7:31 — God says of human sacrifice: "which I commanded them not,
  neither came it into my heart"
  - If burning children alive never entered God's heart, how could burning humans
    _forever_ reflect His character?
- 1 John 4:8 — "God is love"
  - Love may destroy what threatens its beloved — but love does not torture
    endlessly
- [EGW]: "How repugnant to every emotion of love and mercy, and even to our sense
  of justice, is the doctrine that the wicked dead are tormented with fire and
  brimstone in an eternally burning hell." (GC 535.1)

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — Death Defeated (5 min)

### A. The Resurrection Is the Hope

- 1 Thessalonians 4:16–17 — "The Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a
  shout... and the dead in Christ shall rise first"
  - The dead are not already in heaven — they are raised _at His coming_
  - [WB]: Write "THE HOPE: not death → heaven, but death → RESURRECTION" (1
    Thess 4:16)
- 1 Corinthians 15:51–55 — "In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye... the dead
  shall be raised incorruptible"
  - "O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy victory?"
  - Death is swallowed up in victory — but only at the resurrection, not before
- [RQ]: If the righteous are already in heaven, what is the point of the
  resurrection? (1 Cor 15:51–55)

### B. Sin Will Be No More

- Nahum 1:9 — "Affliction shall not rise up the second time"
  - Sin and sinners are destroyed permanently — the universe is clean
- Revelation 21:1–4 — New heaven and new earth — no more death, sorrow, crying,
  or pain
  - This promise is only coherent if sin and its consequences are _completely_
    eradicated
- The doctrine of annihilationism does not make sin trivial — the second death
  is the most terrible thing in the universe
  - It is the permanent, irreversible loss of existence — the loss of everything

### Call to Action — Rest in the True Hope

- **Reject** the serpent's first lie — humans are not inherently immortal; life
  is a gift from God alone (Gen 3:4; 1 Tim 6:16)
- **Rest** in the biblical hope — the dead sleep, and the resurrection is the
  moment of reunion (1 Thess 4:16–17)
- **Trust** God's character — He is just, merciful, and loving; He destroys sin
  without torturing sinners forever (Mal 4:1; 1 John 4:8)
- **Guard** yourself against spiritism — if the dead are unconscious, any
  "spirit" claiming to be a dead loved one is a demonic deception (Deut 18:10–12;
  1 Tim 4:1)
- **Resolve**: _I will hold to the resurrection hope and trust that the God who
  is love will end sin justly and completely_
