### Introduction — The Reversal (5 min)

- Start with the question everyone gets wrong:
  - Which covenant came first — Old or New?
  - The instinct: Old Covenant = Old Testament, New Covenant = New Testament
  - The Bible's answer: **the "New" Covenant is the oldest thing in Scripture**
- Genesis 3:15 — the first words God speaks after the Fall are a covenant promise
  > "And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel."
  - "I will" — God's initiative, God's action, God's promise
  - This is the New Covenant. It predates Sinai by millennia.
- Revelation 13:8 — the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world
  > "And all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world."
  - The New Covenant plan predates not just Sinai, but creation itself
- So what is the "Old Covenant"?
  - It is humanity's interruption — the moment we said "We will do" instead of receiving "I will do"
  - [WB]: Two columns:
    - FIRST (called "New"): "I WILL" — God's promise (Gen 3:15)
    - INTERRUPTION (called "Old"): "WE WILL" — Man's promise (Ex 19:8)
- Hebrews 13:8:
  > "Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever."
  - If God never changes, He didn't start with law and switch to grace
  - He started with grace. We switched to self-effort. He's been calling us back ever since.
- [RQ]: If the "New" Covenant came first, what does that tell us about God's character — and about why we keep needing to hear it again?

### Section 1 — The Original Covenant: Eden (7 min)

#### A. God Speaks First

- After the Fall, notice who acts:
  - Adam hides. God seeks.
  - Adam covers himself with fig leaves. God provides skins.
  - Adam blames. God promises.
- Genesis 3:15 — the covenant is entirely God's initiative
  > "And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel."
  - Five words define the New Covenant: **"I will put enmity"**
  - God doesn't ask Adam to generate hatred for sin — He promises to put it there
  - This is Ezekiel 36:26–27 in seed form:
    > "A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh. And I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes, and ye shall keep my judgments, and do them."
  - Eden and Ezekiel — same covenant, same "I will," thousands of years apart

#### B. God Covers First

- Genesis 3:21:
  > "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the LORD God make coats of skins, and clothed them."
  - A covering provided by God through sacrifice, not human weaving
  - The fig leaves (Gen 3:7) = humanity's first attempt at self-covenant — covering their own shame by their own effort
  - The skins = God's covenant in action — covering requires death, substitution, divine initiative
- [WB]: FIG LEAVES (man's covering / "Old Covenant" impulse) → SKINS (God's covering / Original Covenant)
- The pattern is set in the first three chapters of the Bible:
  - God promises. God provides. God covers.
  - Humanity's role: receive, trust, cooperate — not originate.

#### C. The Covenant Has Always Been About Restoration

- Genesis 1:26–27:
  > "And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth. So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them."
- 2 Corinthians 3:18:
  > "But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord."
- The New Covenant's goal was never just forgiveness — it is **restoration of the image of God**
  - Salvation = the heart changed to reflect God's character
  - The law written on stone was always meant for the heart
- [RQ]: If the original covenant's goal is God's image restored in us, can any system built on human effort accomplish that? (Gen 3:7 vs. Gen 3:21)

### Section 2 — The Pattern of Interruption: Cain to Hagar (7 min)

#### A. Cain — The First "Old Covenant" Worshiper

- Genesis 4:3–5:
  > "And in process of time it came to pass, that Cain brought of the fruit of the ground an offering unto the LORD. And Abel, he also brought of the firstlings of his flock and of the fat thereof. And the LORD had respect unto Abel and to his offering: But unto Cain and to his offering he had not respect. And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell."
  - Cain: fruit of the ground — the labor of his own hands, no blood, no substitute
  - Abel: firstlings of the flock with fat — faith in God's provision, trusting the Substitute
- Cain is not irreligious. He worships. He brings an offering. He is sincere and industrious.
  - But his offering says: "I will provide for myself"
  - Abel's offering says: "God will provide the Lamb"
- [WB]: CAIN → "MY EFFORT" (Rejected) / ABEL → "GOD'S LAMB" (Accepted)
- This is the pattern that will repeat through all of Scripture:
  - God offers His covenant. Someone says "I have a better idea."
- [Aside]: Like paying a real debt with homemade play money — no matter how carefully printed, it has no value. Only the King's currency (Christ's righteousness) is accepted.
- [RQ]: Can a person be sincere, religious, and industrious like Cain, yet still lost because the foundation is self instead of the Lamb? (Gen 4:5–6)

#### B. Abraham — Receiving and Then Interrupting the Original

- Abraham receives the covenant promise directly from God
  - God promises a son. Abraham believes. It is counted as righteousness.
- Then the interruption:
  - Genesis 16:1–4 — Sarah and Abraham "help God out" through Hagar
    > "Now Sarai Abram's wife bare him no children: and she had an handmaid, an Egyptian, whose name was Hagar. And Sarai said unto Abram, Behold now, the LORD hath restrained me from bearing: I pray thee, go in unto my maid; it may be that I may obtain children by her. And Abram hearkened to the voice of Sarai. And Sarai Abram's wife took Hagar her maid the Egyptian, after Abram had dwelt ten years in the land of Canaan, and gave her to her husband Abram to be his wife. And he went in unto Hagar, and she conceived."
  - Ishmael = the product of flesh trying to fulfill God's promise by human devising
- Then God reasserts the original:
  - Genesis 21:1–3 — Isaac born when Abraham's body is "as good as dead"
    > "And the LORD visited Sarah as he had said, and the LORD did unto Sarah as he had spoken. For Sarah conceived, and bare Abraham a son in his old age, at the set time of which God had spoken to him. And Abraham called the name of his son that was born unto him, whom Sarah bare to him, Isaac."
  - Isaac = impossible by human strength, purely miracle of God's word
- Galatians 4:22–26 — Paul names the pattern explicitly:
  > "For it is written, that Abraham had two sons, the one by a bondmaid, the other by a freewoman. But he who was of the bondwoman was born after the flesh; but he of the freewoman was by promise. Which things are an allegory: for these are the two covenants; the one from the mount Sinai, which gendereth to bondage, which is Agar. For this Agar is mount Sinai in Arabia, and answereth to Jerusalem which now is, and is in bondage with her children. But Jerusalem which is above is free, which is the mother of us all."
  - Hagar → Sinai → Bondage → "I can help God out"
  - Sarah → Jerusalem Above → Freedom → "God must do the impossible in me"
- [EGW]: "The covenant of grace was first made with man in Eden... This same covenant was renewed to Abraham... But if the Abrahamic covenant contained the promise of redemption, why was another covenant formed at Sinai? ... The people did not realize the sinfulness of their own hearts, and that without Christ it was impossible for them to keep God's law..." (PP 370.2–371.4)
- Romans 4:20–22:
  > "He staggered not at the promise of God through unbelief; but was strong in faith, giving glory to God; And being fully persuaded that, what he had promised, he was able also to perform. And therefore it was imputed to him for righteousness."
  - Faith = relying on God to do exactly as He promised, despite appearances

### Section 3 — The Great Interruption: Sinai (6 min) [*]

#### A. Israel Says "We Will Do"

- God brings Israel out of Egypt by His power alone — pillar of fire, Red Sea, manna
  - Everything up to this point is "I will" — God acting, Israel receiving
- Then the interruption:
  - Exodus 19:5–8:
    > "Now therefore, if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people: for all the earth is mine: And ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation. These are the words which thou shalt speak unto the children of Israel. And Moses came and called for the elders of the people, and laid before their faces all these words which the LORD commanded him. And all the people answered together, and said, All that the LORD hath spoken we will do. And Moses returned the words of the people unto the LORD."
  - Sounds loyal. But notice what's missing: no "by Thy grace," no "God working in us"
  - Old Covenant at Sinai: ratified on the promises of **the people**
  - New Covenant: ratified on the "better promises" of **God**:
    > **Hebrews 8:6** — "But now hath he obtained a more excellent ministry, by how much also he is the mediator of a better covenant, which was established upon better promises."
- [WB]: SINAI: "All that the LORD hath spoken WE WILL DO" (Ex 19:8) — Man's promise interrupts God's covenant

#### B. How Long Did the Vow Last?

- Exodus 32:1–4 — the golden calf:
  > "And when the people saw that Moses delayed to come down out of the mount, the people gathered themselves together unto Aaron, and said unto him, Up, make us gods, which shall go before us; for as for this Moses, the man that brought us up out of the land of Egypt, we wot not what is become of him. And Aaron said unto them, Break off the golden earrings, which are in the ears of your wives, of your sons, and of your daughters, and bring them unto me. And all the people brake off the golden earrings which were in their ears, and brought them unto Aaron. And he received them at their hand, and fashioned it with a graving tool, after he had made it a molten calf: and they said, These be thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt."
  - The vow shattered before Moses came down the mountain
  - Every covenant built on human willpower has the same shelf life
- [RQ]: How long did their vow last before the golden calf? What does this reveal about any covenant built on human willpower alone?

#### C. God's Diagnosis

- Deuteronomy 5:28–29:
  > "And the LORD heard the voice of your words, when ye spake unto me; and the LORD said unto me, I have heard the voice of the words of this people, which they have spoken unto thee: they have well said all that they have spoken. O that there were such an heart in them, that they would fear me, and keep all my commandments always, that it might be well with them, and with their children for ever!"
  - God says their words are "well said" — the desire is right
  - But He laments: "O that there were such a heart in them"
  - Their promise **exposes what they lack** — the very heart needed to keep it
  - This is the purpose of the "Old Covenant" — not a different plan, but a diagnostic
  - God allowed them to try so they would discover they cannot
- 1 Samuel 8:5–7 — the pattern continues into kingship:
  > "And said unto him, Behold, thou art old, and thy sons walk not in thy ways: now make us a king to judge us like all the nations. But the thing displeased Samuel, when they said, Give us a king to judge us. And Samuel prayed unto the LORD. And the LORD said unto Samuel, Hearken unto the voice of the people in all that they say unto thee: for they have not rejected thee, but they have rejected me, that I should not reign over them."
  - Rejecting God as King = shifting from direct covenant reliance to human systems
  - Same pattern: God offers Himself. They say "We want something we can manage."
- [Aside]: Many live "Saul-style" Christianity — concerned with looking right externally — while God is calling us to a "David-style" experience: a heart after His own heart, fully yielded for Him to write His law within.

### Section 4 — God Reasserts the Original: Sanctuary to Heart (5 min) [*]

#### A. The Sanctuary Is God Re-Teaching the First Covenant

- Exodus 25:8:
  > "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them."
  - Not "that they may come to Me" — "that **I** may dwell among **them**"
  - God's initiative. God's presence. God moving toward humanity.
  - The sanctuary is the original covenant made visible

#### B. Every Piece Teaches "I Will"

- Outer Court — God's provision, not man's:
  - Altar of Burnt Offering:
    > **Leviticus 1:3–4** — "If his offering be a burnt sacrifice of the herd, let him offer a male without blemish: he shall offer it of his own voluntary will at the door of the tabernacle of the congregation before the LORD. And he shall put his hand upon the head of the burnt offering; and it shall be accepted for him to make atonement for him."
    - The sinner leans on the sacrifice, not on self — same principle as Genesis 3:21
  - Laver:
    > **Exodus 30:18–21** — "Thou shalt also make a laver of brass, and his foot also of brass, to wash withal: and thou shalt put it between the tabernacle of the congregation and the altar, and thou shalt put water therein. For Aaron and his sons shall wash their hands and their feet thereat: When they go into the tabernacle of the congregation, they shall wash with water, that they die not; or when they come near to the altar to minister, to burn offering made by fire unto the LORD: So they shall wash their hands and their feet, that they die not: and it shall be a statute for ever to them, even to him and to his seed throughout their generations."
    - Cleansing comes from God's provision, not self-scrubbing
  - [WB]: Outer Court = "Christ for us" → Justification by His sacrifice
    > **Romans 5:9** — "Much more then, being now justified by his blood, we shall be saved from wrath through him."

- Holy Place — Daily education in dependence:
  - Table of Shewbread — Living by every word of God:
    > **Matthew 4:4** — "But he answered and said, It is written, Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God."
  - Candlestick — Receiving light and reflecting Christ's character:
    > **Matthew 5:14–16** — "Ye are the light of the world. A city that is set on an hill cannot be hid. Neither do men light a candle, and put it under a bushel, but on a candlestick; and it giveth light unto all that are in the house. Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven."
  - Altar of Incense — Constant intercession:
    > **Hebrews 7:25** — "Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them."
  - Daily ministry teaches continual reliance on Christ, not on past vows

- Most Holy Place — Law, mercy, and the new heart:
  - Ark of the Covenant:
    > **Exodus 25:21–22** — "And thou shalt put the mercy seat above upon the ark; and in the ark thou shalt put the testimony that I shall give thee. And there I will meet with thee, and I will commune with thee from above the mercy seat, from between the two cherubims which are upon the ark of the testimony, of all things which I will give thee in commandment."
    - Law inside the ark, under the mercy seat, sprinkled with blood
    - The law was never meant to stand alone — always under mercy, always covered by blood
  - Hebrews 9:23–24:
    > "It was therefore necessary that the patterns of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these. For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us."
  - 2 Corinthians 3:3:
    > "Forasmuch as ye are manifestly declared to be the epistle of Christ ministered by us, written not with ink, but with the Spirit of the living God; not in tables of stone, but in fleshy tables of the heart."
    - Law transferred from stone to heart = New Covenant sanctuary in the believer
  - [WB]: Draw: Sanctuary → Arrow → "Heart as Most Holy Place" (Law written within)

#### C. Jeremiah Isn't Introducing Something New — He's Calling Back to Eden

- Jeremiah 31:31–33:
  > "Behold, the days come, saith the LORD, that I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel, and with the house of Judah: Not according to the covenant that I made with their fathers in the day that I took them by the hand to bring them out of the land of Egypt; which my covenant they brake, although I was an husband unto them, saith the LORD: But this shall be the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel; After those days, saith the LORD, I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts; and will be their God, and they shall be my people."
  - "Not according to the covenant... which **they** brake" — the Old Covenant failed because it depended on them
  - "I will put... I will write... I will be" — notice: all "I will." Same language as Genesis 3:15.
  - Jeremiah isn't announcing a new plan. He's telling Israel: **God is going back to the beginning.**
- Daniel 8:14:
  > "And he said unto me, Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed."
  - The final phase of Christ's work: cleansing hearts and lives in harmony with the law
  - The sanctuary cleansed = God's people brought fully into the original covenant
- [RQ]: If the goal of the sanctuary service is God dwelling with a cleansed people, what does that say about what He wants to do in our minds and characters today?

### Conclusion — Choose Which Covenant You Live In (3–4 min)

- The Bible's story is simpler than we thought:
  - God made a covenant in Eden: "I will."
  - Humanity has been interrupting it ever since: "We will."
  - Every failure — Cain, Hagar, Sinai, the golden calf, the kings — is the same mistake: replacing God's promise with human effort
  - Every restoration — Abel, Isaac, David, the sanctuary, Jeremiah — is the same return: receiving God's "I will" by faith
- [EGW]: "The new-covenant promise is, 'I will put My laws into their hearts, and in their minds will I write them.'... If the law is written in the heart, will it not shape the life? Obedience — the service and allegiance of love — is the true sign of discipleship." (MB 51.1)
- The "New" Covenant is not Plan B. It is the only plan God ever had. He calls it "new" because we keep forgetting the original.

### Call to Action — Return to the First Covenant

1. **Unlearn self-promise religion** — Lay aside "I will do better next time" as the foundation
   - Peter made the same vow:
     > **Luke 22:33–34** — "And he said unto him, Lord, I am ready to go with thee, both into prison, and to death. And he said, I tell thee, Peter, the cock shall not crow this day, before that thou shalt thrice deny that thou knowest me."
   - Every "we will do" has the same ending
2. **Learn to claim God's promises personally** — Pray the "I will" texts:
   > **Deuteronomy 30:6** — "And the LORD thy God will circumcise thine heart, and the heart of thy seed, to love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, that thou mayest live."
   > **Philippians 1:6** — "Being confident of this very thing, that he which hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ."
   > **Hebrews 8:10** — "I will put my laws into their mind, and write them in their hearts."
   - "Lord, do in me what You have promised."
3. **Daily cooperate with the Inner Teacher** — Come to Scripture asking: "What must I unlearn today? What truth will You write deeper into my mind?"
   > **John 16:13** — "Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth."
   > **Romans 12:2** — "And be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God."
4. **Choose dependence over determination** — In every temptation, look away from your willpower to Christ's present power:
   > **Hebrews 12:2** — "Looking unto Jesus the author and finisher of our faith; who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross, despising the shame, and is set down at the right hand of the throne of God."
   > **Jude 1:24** — "Now unto him that is able to keep you from falling, and to present you faultless before the presence of his glory with exceeding joy."

- [WB]: Action Steps:
  1. Unlearn: "We will do" (self-trust)
  2. Learn: "I will" (God's promises)
  3. Practice: Moment-by-moment dependence

- [RQ]: As we near the closing scenes of earth's history, where is your confidence resting — on your renewed resolutions, or on Christ's living promise to write His law in your heart and finish the work in you?

**The covenant God made in Eden is still open. It has always been "I will." Will you stop interrupting — and let Him finish?**
