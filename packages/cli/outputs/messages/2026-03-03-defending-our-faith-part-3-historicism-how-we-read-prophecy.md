## Introduction — Recap and Setup (4 min)

- Parts 1–2 established: God's law is eternal, the Sabbath is His seal, and
  Daniel 7:25 predicted a power that would change times and laws
- But _how_ do we know Daniel 7:25 points to a historical power? Why not a
  future antichrist? Why not a past tyrant?
- The answer lies in our _hermeneutic_ — our method of reading prophecy
  - [WB]: Write 'HERMENEUTIC = How you read prophecy → determines what you
conclude'
- Three schools of prophetic interpretation exist:
  1. **Historicism** — prophecy traces continuous history from the prophet's day
     to the end
  2. **Futurism** — prophecy is mostly unfulfilled, pushed to a future
     tribulation
  3. **Preterism** — prophecy was mostly fulfilled in the ancient past
  - [WB]: Write the three schools in columns
- [RQ]: If your method of reading prophecy is wrong, can your conclusions be
  right? (2 Pet 1:19–20)
- Historicism is the method used by the Reformers, the Millerites, and the SDA
  pioneers — and it is the method taught by Scripture itself

## Section 1 — What Is Historicism? (8 min)

_Addresses: "SDA interpretation is novel," "Day-year is made up"_

### A. Prophecy as Continuous History

- Daniel 2 is the clearest demonstration of the historicist method
  - Nebuchadnezzar's image: head of gold → chest of silver → belly of brass →
    legs of iron → feet of iron and clay → stone
  - Daniel 2:37–40 — Four kingdoms in unbroken succession
  - [WB]: Draw the image with labels: BABYLON → MEDO-PERSIA → GREECE → ROME →
    DIVIDED EUROPE → GOD'S KINGDOM
- Key principle: the prophecy covers _all_ of history from Daniel's day to
  Christ's kingdom
  - No gaps. No parenthetical ages. Continuous fulfillment.
  - [RQ]: Does Daniel 2 skip any era between Babylon and the stone kingdom? (Dan
    2:37–45)
- Daniel 7 repeats the same pattern with different symbols
  - Lion → Bear → Leopard → Terrible beast → Ten horns → Little horn → Judgment
    → Son of man receives kingdom
  - Daniel 7:17 — "These great beasts, which are four, are four kings"
  - Daniel 7:23 — "The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom"
  - Same four empires, same continuous flow of history

### B. The Reformers' Unanimous Witness

- Historicism was not invented by Adventists — it was the standard Protestant
  method for centuries
  - [WB]: Write "REFORMERS → HISTORICIST" and list:
  - **Wycliffe** (1320s–1384): identified the papacy as antichrist
  - **Huss** (1369–1415): same conclusion from Daniel and Revelation
  - **Luther** (1483–1546): "We are not the first who apply the antiChristian
    kingdom to the papacy"
  - **Calvin** (1509–1564): recognized the little horn of Daniel 7
  - **Knox, Zwingli, Tyndale, Wesley** — all historicists
- [RQ]: If every major Reformer read prophecy this way, when and why did
  Protestantism abandon it? (Amos 3:7)
- [EGW]: "The prophecies of Daniel and the Revelation should be carefully
  studied, and in connection with them the words, 'Behold the Lamb of God, which
  taketh away the sin of the world.'" (FE 393.1)

### C. Prophetic Structure Demands Historicism

- Daniel's prophecies are built on a principle: _recapitulation_
  - Each vision (Dan 2, 7, 8, 10–12) covers the same historical span from
    different angles
  - [WB]: Draw four parallel timelines — Dan 2 / Dan 7 / Dan 8 / Dan 10–12 —
    all ending at the same point
- Each successive vision _zooms in_ on the period most relevant to God's people:
  - Daniel 2 — broad sweep, four empires to the kingdom
  - Daniel 7 — adds the little horn and the judgment
  - Daniel 8 — narrows to Medo-Persia, Greece, and the horn that takes away the
    _tamid_ (daily)
  - Daniel 10–12 — detailed history from Persia to the time of the end
- This layered, cumulative structure only makes sense under historicism

## Section 2 — The Day-Year Principle: Biblical Proof (10 min)

_Addresses: "Day-year is arbitrary," "You can't just make a day equal a year"_

### A. God Himself Established the Principle

- Numbers 14:34 — "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land,
  even forty days, each day for a year, shall ye bear your iniquities, even
  forty years"
  - [WB]: Write "40 DAYS → 40 YEARS" (Num 14:34)
  - God explicitly maps prophetic days to literal years
- Ezekiel 4:6 — "I have appointed thee each day for a year"
  - Ezekiel lies on his side — 40 days representing 40 years for Judah
  - [WB]: Write "1 DAY = 1 YEAR" (Ezek 4:6)
  - Two witnesses to the principle — the Torah (Numbers) and the Prophets
    (Ezekiel)
- [RQ]: If God Himself uses the day-for-a-year ratio, is it "made up" when we
  apply the same ratio to Daniel's time prophecies? (Num 14:34; Ezek 4:6)

### B. The 70 Weeks Prove It

- Daniel 9:24–27 — "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people"
  - 70 weeks = 490 prophetic days
  - If literal days: 490 days ≈ 1 year 4 months — far too short for the events
    described (Messiah's coming, atonement, destruction of city)
  - If day-year: 490 years — and history confirms exact fulfillment
  - [WB]: Write "70 WEEKS × 7 = 490 DAYS → 490 YEARS" (Dan 9:24)
- The starting point: "from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to
  build Jerusalem" (Dan 9:25)
  - Artaxerxes' decree — 457 BC (Ezra 7:7, 11–26)
  - [WB]: Timeline: 457 BC → 408 BC (wall built, 49 years/7 weeks) → AD 27
    (Messiah anointed, 483 years/69 weeks) → AD 31 (crucifixion, midst of 70th
    week) → AD 34 (490 years complete, gospel to Gentiles)
- **AD 27** — Jesus baptized, begins ministry: "The time is fulfilled" (Mark
  1:15)
  - _Fulfilled_ — the prophetic clock reached its predicted moment
- **AD 31** — "In the midst of the week he shall cause the sacrifice and the
  oblation to cease" (Dan 9:27)
  - Crucifixion in the middle of the 70th week — 3½ years into His ministry
- **AD 34** — Stephen stoned (Acts 7), gospel formally turns to the Gentiles
  (Acts 8–10)
  - The 490 years end
- [RQ]: When the 70 weeks predict the exact year of Christ's baptism and
  crucifixion on the day-year principle, how can the principle be called
  arbitrary? (Dan 9:24–27)
- [EGW]: "The seventy weeks, or 490 years... are the key to the 2300 days." (GC
  326.1, paraphrased)

### C. The 2300 Days Depend on the Same Principle

- Daniel 8:14 — "Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the
  sanctuary be cleansed"
  - The 70 weeks are "cut off" (_chathak_, #2852) from the 2300 days — same
    starting point
  - [WB]: Draw timeline: 457 BC → 2300 years → **1844 AD**
  - If the 70 weeks are years (proven by Christ's life), the 2300 days are years
    by the same principle
- We will examine what happened in 1844 in Part 4 (The Sanctuary)
- The point here: the day-year principle is not an SDA invention — it is
  demonstrated by Scripture and confirmed by history

## Section 3 — Futurism and Preterism: Counter-Reformation Inventions (8 min) [*]

_Addresses: "Historicism is fringe," "Most scholars disagree"_

### A. Why Alternatives Were Needed

- The Reformers' historicist reading had devastating implications for Rome
  - If Daniel 7's little horn = the papacy, and Revelation's Babylon = Rome,
    then the Reformation was prophetically legitimate
  - Rome needed an alternative interpretation — urgently
  - [WB]: Write 'HISTORICISM → PAPACY IDENTIFIED → ROME NEEDED ALTERNATIVES'

### B. Futurism — Francisco Ribera (1590)

- Jesuit priest Francisco Ribera proposed: the antichrist is a single future
  individual who appears in a 7-year tribulation at the end of time
  - [WB]: Write 'FUTURISM (1590) — Ribera, Jesuit — pushes antichrist to the
future'
  - This removed the papacy from prophetic scrutiny entirely
  - Daniel's prophecies stop applying to historical Rome and leap to an
    undefined future
- Today, futurism dominates Protestant evangelicalism (Left Behind, dispensational
  premillennialism)
  - [RQ]: How did a Jesuit Counter-Reformation theory become the dominant
    Protestant view? (2 Tim 4:3–4)
- The "gap theory" — a parenthetical church age inserted between the 69th and
  70th week of Daniel 9
  - No scriptural basis. Daniel 9 presents continuous time.
  - If the 69th week ended at Christ's baptism (AD 27), the 70th week follows
    immediately — it doesn't jump 2000+ years

### C. Preterism — Luis de Alcázar (1614)

- Jesuit priest Luis de Alcázar proposed: most of Revelation was fulfilled by
  the fall of Rome in AD 476
  - [WB]: Write 'PRETERISM (1614) — Alcázar, Jesuit — pushes antichrist to the
past'
  - The little horn becomes Antiochus Epiphanes (2nd century BC)
  - Revelation's beasts become Nero or other Roman emperors
- This also removes the papacy from prophetic identification
- [EB]: Daniel 8:17 — "Understand, O son of man: for at the time of the end
  shall be the vision"
  - Daniel 8:19 — "I will make thee know what shall be in the last end of the
    indignation"
  - The vision reaches to "the time of the end" — Antiochus Epiphanes in 164 BC
    does not qualify

### D. Both Serve the Same Purpose

- [WB]: Draw diagram: FUTURISM ← ROME → PRETERISM (arrows pushing antichrist
  identification away from the papacy in both directions)
- Two Jesuit scholars, two opposite conclusions, one shared goal: deflect
  historicist identification
- [RQ]: When two contradictory theories are invented by the same institution to
  counter the same conclusion, what does that tell you about the conclusion they
  are trying to avoid? (John 8:32)
- [EGW]: "Satan has used the papacy to exalt himself above God and above His law;
  but the time has come for every honest soul to come out of her." (GC 581,
  paraphrased)

## FINAL PEAK SECTION — The Prophetic Heritage (5 min)

### A. We Stand in a Line of Witnesses

- Historicism is not fringe — it is the original Protestant prophetic method
- From Wycliffe to Wesley, from Miller to the SDA pioneers — the same method,
  the same conclusions
  - [WB]: Write 'WYCLIFFE → HUSS → LUTHER → CALVIN → KNOX → WESLEY → MILLER →
SDA PIONEERS'
- We did not invent this method. We inherited it. And when Protestantism
  abandoned it for Jesuit alternatives, we kept it.
- [RQ]: If the Reformers were right about salvation by faith alone, why would
  they be wrong about prophecy? (Heb 11:1–2)

### B. The Method Matters Because the Message Depends on It

- If futurism is correct: the three angels' messages have no present
  application, the Sabbath is irrelevant, and there is no investigative judgment
- If preterism is correct: Revelation is finished history, there is no end-time
  remnant, and prophecy has nothing to say to us
- If historicism is correct: we are living in the time of the end, the judgment
  has begun, and God is calling a people to prepare
  - [WB]: Write 'HISTORICISM → JUDGMENT NOW → SABBATH MATTERS → REMNANT CALLED'
- The hermeneutic is not academic — it determines whether the gospel has a
  present, urgent claim on your life

### C. Prophecy as a Light in Darkness

- 2 Peter 1:19 — "A more sure word of prophecy; whereunto ye do well that ye
  take heed, as unto a light that shineth in a dark place"
  - Prophecy is not speculative — it is _more sure_ than eyewitness testimony
    (v. 18)
  - Peter saw the transfiguration and still says prophecy is more reliable
- [EGW]: "Those who are engaged in proclaiming the third angel's message are
  searching the Scriptures upon the same plan that Father Miller adopted." (RH,
  November 25, 1884)

### Call to Action — Hold Fast the Prophetic Word

- **Study** the prophecies of Daniel and Revelation — they are not sealed but
  opened for the final generation (Dan 12:4, 9–10)
- **Recognize** that futurism and preterism are Counter-Reformation tools
  designed to obscure truth — test every system by Scripture (Isa 8:20)
- **Treasure** the historicist heritage — you stand in the line of the Reformers,
  and God has entrusted you with the final message (Rev 14:6–12)
- **Resolve**: _I will read prophecy as God intended — as continuous history
  pointing to the judgment hour and the return of Christ_
